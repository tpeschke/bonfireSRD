Introduction

Archetypes represent your character’s background training and starting skills and there are two types of Archetypes: Generalist and Specialist.

Generalist Archetypes

These Archetypes represent the overall theme.

For instance, Fighter Archetypes tend to be good at things related to combat. So, if you’re looking to make a combat focused character, you can look there. They are Expert, Weird-Adept, Servant, Socialite, and Fighter. You must pick two starting out.

Generalists will have a wide variety of discounts within their theme that are good for just getting straight bonuses.

Purchasing Archetypes

First choose a Generalist Primary and a Generalist Secondary Archetype and pay the price in CrP for both based on your race.

You use the Vitality Die, and the Primary Drawback sections from the Primary, and combine both the free Advanced Skills (listed under the Main Skill Suite) and the Archetype Abilities sections, though you completely remove 1 ability marked with * from your Secondary Archetype, unless you Double Down.

All Generalists, except for the Servant, can be selected as both your Primary and Secondary Archetype, called Double Down. So, you can be a Fighter/Fighter but not a Servant/Servant.

The benefits for double downing will be noted at the end of the Archetype but they only remain while both Archetypes are not Specialized.

If two characters have the same ability, unless it says otherwise, those abilities stack.

Specialist Archetypes

These represent a more focused version of the Generalist Archetypes.

They’re more expensive but come with additional powers. When you purchase a Specialist Archetype, it replaces the Generalist Archetype of the same type except with GM permission. So, for instance, if you’re an Expert, you can replace it with the Ranger or the Thief.

Purchasing Specialist Archetypes

At 1st level and at every level up, you can purchase a Specialist Archetype which replaces your Generalist Archetypes. If at 1st level, see the Specialist Archetypes at 1st Level sidebar below.

You can replace one or both at each level, in either order, barring double-down Weird-Adepts who have to specialize both at the same time into the same Specialist Archetype.

You can also choose to replace neither if it doesn’t fit your character because once you purchase a Specialist Archetype, you are stuck with it.

Specialist Archetypes completely replaces everything from the Generalist Archetype they’re replacing though it doesn’t retroactively remove things you’ve already rolled for or free purchases.

So, a Fighter, with a d10 Vitality Die decides, at level 4 to become a Soldier with a Vitality Die of a d8. He doesn’t then reroll all his Vitality Die - from that point forward, he simply uses the d8 instead.

They also gain a free purchase of that Specialist's Main Skill Suite as noted in Step 8 of Chapter 2.1: Step-by-Step Overview.

You’ll pay an additional 20 CrP for each one you’re replacing regardless of race.

Your GM may choose to reject an Archetype combination based on the fact that the two Archetypes don’t fit together thematically. So, while a Thief/Priest (Beast Covenant) might be really powerful because of the Beast Covenant’s Shape Changing ability, if there’s not a Patron of Thieves and Animals in the campaign world, you’re out of luck.

Remember that if you’re replacing your Secondary Archetype, you’ll still need to select a leveling ability from your new Archetype to remove.

Specialist Archetypes at 1st Level

If you’re more familiar with the game, your playstyle, and which Archetypes suit you, you might consider purchasing your Specialization(s) at 1st level. That’s perfectly fine, but you’ll still need to pay the CrP for both the Generalist and the Specialist.

You’re considered to have purchased your Generalist Archetype first AND THEN purchased your Specialist, which means that you still get any free stuff from abilities that say "at first level" from your Generalist, so Experts get some free skills and Weird-Adepts get their starting Rudiments.

Your Primary Specialist’s Vitality Die and Primary Drawback also replaces your Primary Generalist’s.

Generalist Archetypes

Expert

Choose this Archetype if you want to use your wits to defeat your opponent or avoid combat altogether.

Experts are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines, magic, or their sword arm as a source of strength. They tend to rely on Intellect and Willpower, primarily, though there are Experts that focus on Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution for more physical skills.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suites Two Different non-Combat Suites of your Choice

Archetype Abilities

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

*Skill Focus (Chosen Skill Suite 1, 5) At first level and every other level up, Experts receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the first Skill Suite you chose. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

*Skill Focus (Chosen Skill Suite 2, 5) At first level and every other level up, Experts receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the second Skill Suite you chose. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

Primary Drawback

None

Expert Double Down

Skill Adept

All Advanced Skills are reduced by 3 CrP and you gain a 30% discount to non-Combat Skill Suites.

Skill Focuses

You get these every level up, instead of every other.

Fighter

Choose this Archetype if you want to kill monsters and worthy foes in mortal combat and like thinking on your feet.

War is a ladder by which capable people are able to climb the ranks of society. Fighters focus on combat and battle, either by training and discipline or by out maneuvering their opponent. Unsurprisingly, this Archetype focuses on Constitution, but how you approach combat may decide whether you favor Strength, Dexterity, or even Intellect or Willpower.

*Martial Focus (Chosen Combat Skill Suite 2) A Fighter picks things up as they go, otherwise, they get themselves and their companions killed. This can’t be stacked with their other free Skill Suites from other Martial Focuses.

This can’t be stacked with their other free Skill Suites from other Martial Focuses.

Servant

Choose this Archetype if you want harness powers beyond the mortal realm to alter the battlefield and circumstances to your favor.

Servants represent those that have devoted themselves to higher causes, lifting their eyes from the Middle Realm to a more pure order, whether that’s the Divines, the Ancestral Spirits, or the Bones of Ymir. Servants play the best with others, often modifying the other Archetype’s powers with Divine assistance.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suites Trades and Lore

Servants get 3 free purchases of the Religion Advanced Skill for free.

Archetype Abilities

*Called & Chosen A Servant seeks to do the will of those who sent them, furthering their agendas in the Middle Realm and so they are blessed. They add their total level to their Max Favor.

Simple Faith A Servant that's not been initiated into the deeper mysteries of their cult harbors a faith that is simple and pure before their patrons. Each level they obtain, after the first, gives them a permanent +1 to their Max Favor. Unlike Called & Chosen, this bonus isn't removed when the Servant Specializes, although it no longer grows.

Depths Of Devotion A Servant understand the bonds of giving your life to a cause greater than your own and its strengths. They gain a free Devotion to their Divine, starting at 3. They also start Anointed in their cult.

*Former Life A Servant can be called from anywhere and any walk of life. Select any Archetype ability; This can be from a Generalist or a Specialist Archetype. Former Life has all the same effects as that ability, though it still counts as a separate leveling ability.

This ability stays with you after you replace the Servant Generalist Archetype, however, if it is a leveling ability, it stops leveling, even if your pick the Specialist Archetype it's from.

Symbol Of Faith As part of their initiation, the Servant receives a holy symbol of their faith. This symbol doesn't help channel their holy power but doesn't help them keep the faith. When brought into a Confrontation that relates question their faith or going against their cult's tenets, they receive their Honor dice instead of the standard d6!.

Primary Drawback

None

Socialite

Choose this Archetype if you want to talk your way out of problems and inspire others to give you your way.

Socialites are people people. They seem to get a real kick out of interacting with others, especially if it ends with them getting their way. While others' first weapons are swords, a Socialite's first weapon is their charm. Unsurprisingly, Socialites tend to rely on Intellect and Presence.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suite Streetwise

Archetype Abilities

*Strong Personality As a Socialite grows, she becomes more confident in who she is and what she wants. At 2nd level and every other level after that, a Socialite can increase the Rank of one of their Traits by 1 or they save them and cash in two of these increases for a new weak Trait starting at Rank 0.

*Camaraderie A Socialite is able to form quick and powerful bonds with those he is around. At 2nd level and at every other level, the Socialite, or someone he's been traveling with, can either increase the strength of one of their Devotions by 1 or gain a new one at Rank 1.

Work On Your Personality A Socialite knows that time spent on your personality is never wasted. When increasing Traits through CrP, the Socialite receives a 4 CrP discount.

None

Strong Personality

Camaraderie

This starts at 1st level and is increased to every level.

Weird-adept

Choose this Archetype if you want an eclectic set of powers that you can apply to most situations in a creative manner but may not be helpful for all problems.

Through much study and/or natural talent, Weird-Adepts can channel the Painted World, using its power to alter reality. Weird-Adepts rely more on clever applications of spells rather than outright doing damage and so can often subtly shift the playing field in their favor. They rely mainly on Intellect and Willpower since they need a high Stress Threshold as it is extremely stressful to shape Weird.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suite Weirdcraft

Archetype Abilities

Rudimentary Knowledge Weird-Adepts know the basics of shaping the Weird and so start out with 5 random Rudiments. They gain a new one every 3rd level they've obtained.

Applied Folkweird While anyone can shape a simple cantrip, the Weird-Adepts knows how to apply those lessons to shape a real spell. After 10 seconds of light concentration (they can move up to a Jog and make Checks, although they do so on a d10!) with at least 1 hand free and the ability to speak, the Weird-Adept generates a number of Spell Points equal to their Level + Weirdcraft Skill Suite Rank + applicable Weirdcraft Advanced Skill's Rank which must be immediately spent to shape a spell.

Any SP remaining after the initial Shaping of a spell becomes fixed within it; these may only be used to extend its Interval per the normal rules, and each spell tracks its SP separately. The Weird-Adept can begin concentrating to gather more SP immediately after Shaping a spell, but these new SP can only be used to Shape a new spell, not added to an existing one.

Should the Weird-Adept not be able to get enough SP to even get 1 Interval, they can take Stress equal to the SP they're missing in Weirdburn to get at least 1 Interval of the spell up and running.

Bending reality and shaping primordial force is not an easy thing to do and nature doesn’t like it. When they first shape a spell, they take 1 Stress for every 10 Drain (rounded up) the spell has +5 for every Category above Never that their Fatigue Threshold is at (5 for Critical, 10 for Wounded, 15 for Bloodied, etc.). This is a one-time cost so continuing to shape the same spell doesn't further Stress them out (as long as they don't suffer weirdburn, of course).

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft Skill Suite) At first level and every other level up, Weird-Adept receive 1 free purchase of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite.

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft, 2) At first level and every other level up, Weird-Adept receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Weirdcraft Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

Primary Drawback

None

Weird-Adept Double Down

Rudimentary Knowledge

Start with 9 Rudiments, with 2 being your choices and the rest random. Gain a new one every other level.

Applied Folkweird

Shape after 7 seconds of concentration and gain SPs equal to twice your level + Weirdcraft Skill Suite Rank + applicable Weirdcraft Advanced Skill's Rank.

Skill Focuses

You get these are every level up, instead of every other.

Expert Specialist Archetypes

Adventurer

Choose this Archetype if you want a good range of skills and be able to help others in Skill Challenges.

Adventures gain the most free skills of all Archetypes allowing for the largest breathe of knowledge, however, they’re cheaper skills. They can also help others, giving them a chance to try again at Skill Checks they fail.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suites Two Different, non-Lore, non-Combat Skill Suites

Archetype Abilities

*Helping Hand The Adventurer is not just about knowing things but also giving others a helping hand in times of need. When another person uses an Adventurer’s help, either as an Asset, or one of his Helper Skills or Preparation, they receive an additional +1 to their roll. This bonus increases by +1 for every 2 levels the Adventurer has obtained.

Middle Name: Danger For the Adventurer, danger is calming and straightforward and that’s what they crave. Whenever an Adventurer takes damage, they can heal that amount of Stress.

*Outthinking While they may not be the best with the blade, the Adventurer is often able to beat their opponents by outthinking them. Gain a use of your Honor dice whenever you use a Str-, Con-, or Dex-based Skill that affects a combat, regardless of whether or not it's successful. Gain an additional one for every 3 levels you've obtained.

Skill Focus (Any < 7 CrP, 7) At first level and every level up, Adventurers receive 7 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills under 7 CrP in base cost (before Skill Adept). These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Primary Drawback

Adventurers tend to eschew book smarts for street smarts: the Lore Skill Suite and any Lore Advanced Skills cost double if he's purchasing them (so he can still pick up Lore Skills under 7 CrP as part of his Skill Focus).

Ranger

Choose this Archetype if you want to have a good range of survival skills, use your team to overcome Obstacles, but also move quicker on and off the battlefield.

The Ranger’s strengths rely mostly in their survival skills, making them excellent to have in the wilderness but they also gain a bonus to their movement that allows them to out-pace others during skirmishes.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suite Survival

Archetypes Abilities

*Quick Footed The Ranger relies on being able to cross great swaths of land quickly, chasing down their prey. At first level and for every 2 levels he’s obtained, the Ranger increases his base movement rate categories by 1 foot + 1 per Movement Categoy above a Stroll. He can't increase it again until all other movement categories have been increased in a like manner.

For example, a first-level, human Ranger can choose Stroll/Crawling, Walking, Jogging, Running, Sprinting. If he chooses Walking, his Walk becomes 7 ft / second (because it is +2 since Walk is 1 Movement Category above Stroll). At level 3, he can choose Strolling/Crawling, Jogging, Running, or Sprinting, but not Walking. If he chooses Running, his Running speed becomes 19 ft / second (since Running is 3 Categories above Stroll, adding that base +1).

After he's increased all, he can go back and increase his Walk again.

Put Your Heads Together For survival, communication is key and doing so quickly is often extremely important. When using each other as Assets, all allies of the Ranger have their Organization considered 1 better.

*Hiking Conditioning A Ranger is used to running and jumping for hours on end with equipment on their back so gain a +1S to their Carry for every 2 levels obtained.

Skill Focus (Survival, 5) At first level and every level up, Rangers receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Survival Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Primary Drawback

A Ranger finds armor restricting and irritating: all Armor Advanced Skills are 2 CrP more expensive and the Armor Skill Suite is 10 CrP more expensive.

Sage

Choose this Archetype if you want to play an extremely knowledge person as well as use that knowledge to give bonuses to your company.

The Sage gets some of the most expensive and cerebral skills for free but also, because of his intense training, he has the ability to instantly reroll any skill he chooses.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suites Lore Twice

Archetype Abilities

*Memory Training The Sage’s training doesn’t just revolve around studying but also on how to study, allowing them better recall of information than those without such mental discipline. The Sage may reroll 1 Int, Wis, or Pres based Skill Check for 7 Stress. They may do this multiple times but each time they do so between opportunities where they can heal Stress costs them double, however, they also lower this Stress gain by 1 for every 3 levels they’ve obtained, down to 3.

*Frank Discussion You can often get more out of a discussion than you can simply thinking about something. When used as an Asset, the person receives a +1 to their Skill Check and an additional +1 for every 3 levels the Sage has obtained.

Skill Focus (Lore, 5) At first level and every level up, Sages receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skill from the Lore Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Sage has spent most of his time buried in study and so may take a bit longer to react to reality: they always roll an initiative die 1 size larger.

Thief

Choose this Archetype if you want to play an expert in stealth and intelligence gathering.

The Thief has the widest variety of skills, picking from a uniquely built suite, rather than a predetermined one on top of their other free skills.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suite Streetwise

Thieves receive 3 free purchases of Language (Thieves Cant).

Archetype Abilities

Thief’s Training At first level and every 2 levels they obtain, the Thief receives 4 free purchases in Advanced Skill from the list found below. This can be doubled up into a single skill or spread between any number of them.

Thief's Training Skills

A thief may choose from the following skills:

*Brother/sisterhood When you know others have your back and you have theirs, it’s much easier to keep your calm. Everyone within sight (or other form of communication) of the thief increases their Stress Threshold by 5. This bonus increases by 1 for every 2 levels the Thief has obtained.

*Skill Focus (Streetwise, 3) At first level and every level up, Thieves receive 3 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skill from the Streetwise Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Fighter Specialist Archetypes

Cateran

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a fighter that flanks their opponent, keeping them from moving or engaging others in combat.

The Cateran is all about flanking your opponent and keeping other fighters locked down. As such, they work best with other fighters.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suite Tactics Twice

Archetype Abilities

Baby-sitting While an individual Cateran may vary in his ability to take care of infants, they all share the ability to keep combatants locked down.

Whenever a person engaged with the Cateran tries to disengage, the Cateran gets an immediate free attack that, if it hits, deals half damage. This happens regardless of where they are on the Clock and doesn’t affect when their next attack happens.

Closed Openings Flanking is the art of dividing people’s attention and the Cateran has perfected that art. Anyone flanked by a Cateran lowers their attack die by 1 size, regardless of who they’re attacking.

*Mutieren A Cateran knows to move their blade to get into places their opponents wouldn't expect. If facing an enemy whose weapon has a base Parry or who is carrying a shield, the Cateran receives a +1 to Attack +1 for every 2 levels they've obtained. This ability isn't effective against people who gained their Parry solely through training.

*Shadowing By keeping close to their opponent, the Cateran can negate people’s defensive posturing. By taking 7 Stress, a Cateran can negate any bonuses an opponent gets from moving, whether that’s shifting, stepping back, or whatever and also stops their opponent from moving.

The Cateran can do this as many times as they want but the Stress cost doubles each time they do so, though this resets once they’ve had time to destress. Also, this Stress cost is lowered by 1 for every 3 levels they’ve obtained, down to 3 Stress.

Bum Rush A Cateran can also use his allies to more effectively flank their opponents. When flanking from the side, you and your allies lower your target’s defense die by 1, including the dice of those using shields (though they still get a chance to Parry). This doesn’t, however, give you any benefit for attacking from directly behind.

*Martial Focus (Melee Weapons, 2) At first level and every level up, Caterans receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Melee Combat Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

*Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Cateran is a group fighter and fighting alone can be unnerving for him. When engaged with enemies alone and not flanking, the Cateran is unable to use his Honor.

Knight

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a heavy armored tank that inspires others to fight hard.

The Knight focuses on shield and armor, and with their larger Vitality Die, they tend to be pretty tanky. Additionally, their Inheritance allows them to get the more expensive weapons for free.

Vitality Die

d12

Skills

Main Skill Suite Tactics

Archetype Abilities

*Chivalry From their strict code comes a strength and confidence that others can only dream of and envy. For the duration of a combat, you gain 1 free use of your Honor Die. You gain an additional free Honor Die for every level you obtain. You can only apply 1 of these bonus Honor Die to a single roll, though that doesn’t stop you adding Honor Dice that you’ve gotten from other sources.

*Call To Valor A Knight is a hero and inspires others to rise above. For the duration of a combat all allies gain 1 free use of an Honor Die to share between them. They gain an additional free Honor Die for every 4 levels you’ve obtained. They can only apply 1 of these bonus Honor Dice to a single roll, though that doesn’t stop them adding Honor Dice that they got from other sources, and the Honor Dice is the size of the person’s Honor Dice that redeems it.

Inheritance This character may have had an extra leg up in life, allowing them the opportunity to start with better equipment than others. They gain an additional 250 sc that must be spent on one item that represents an heirloom passed down to them. They also gain 15 sc for each CrP cashed in at the end of Character Creation.

*Martial Focus (Special, 2) At first level and every level up, Knights receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Armor, Shield, or Melee Combat Skill Suites. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

*Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Knight is a creature of Honor and respects Honor where they find it. They may be killers, but they have a code, whether articulated or not. A Knight may never use a long-ranged weapon on a foe that has greater than Average Honor.

Soldier

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a warrior that sticks close to the rest of the company and allows them to adapt more quickly to changing situations.

Bonfire characters are not meant to stand alone, and this goes double for the Soldier, who works the best with others. They’re able to shadow others around the battlefield and really focus on being good at one or two weapons.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suite Tactics

Archetype Abilities

Small Unit Tactics Soldiers work as a team. If they or anyone they’re adjacent (including enemies) uses a tactic that moves them (Shift, Step Back, etc.), the Soldier or their adjacent ally may also use that Tactic or Shift with no penalties.

*Buddy System Soldiers fight as a unit, moving and acting as one where possible. When an adjacent ally switches Tactics, the Soldier may immediately switch to that Tactic as well. On top of this, adjacent allies also receive a +1 to their Trauma Threshold, which increases by 1 for every level the soldier has obtained

*Weapon Specialization At first level, the soldier selects 2 weapons. At first level and every level up, Soldiers receive a +1 to Attack, Damage, and Parry and a -1 to Recovery with these weapons.

*Martial Focus (Any Combat Skill Suite, 2) At first level and every level up, Soldiers receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from any Combat Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

*Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

A Soldier focuses so much time drilling with his preferred weapon that he can often forget that principles with one weapon can apply to another. All Ranged Weapon and Melee Skill Suites are 15 CrP more expensive.

Thug

Choose this Archetype if you want to play someone slips through armor and wears down their opponent so they can't fight back.

The Thug is designed to hit hard and make each strike count. As such they have abilities that remove an enemy’s damage reduction and increase the chance to knock someone out.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suite Streetwise Twice

Archetype Abilities

*Critical Strike Sometimes the winner isn't the one that can deal out the most pain but the one who knows where to put the pain. At first level, the Thug gains a +1 to effective damage when deciding whether or not an attack triggers a Trauma Check though this extra "damage" isn't added to the d20! roll. This bonus increases by 1 for every other level the Thug has obtained.

*Run 'em Ragged At the beginning of combat or a Skill Challenge, the Thug gains a pool of Stress equal to 5 times his level. On a successful hit or against any Check that's opposed by another person (even if they're unaware of him), he may subtract an amount of Stress from that pool. If he does so, he inflicts that much Stress on his target, which is healed when the person Takes a Breather.

*Precision Attack The Thug is an expert at making every attack count. At first level, he lowers the DR of his opponent by 1 and an additional 1 for every other level he's obtained. For /d DR, this is subtracted from the final total.

Example, a level 4 Thug with a knife (3 dice) vs Scale (2/D), would reduce the enemy’s DR to 4 (6 total DR - 2 from Precision Attack), while one using a club (1 dice) would reduce it to 0 (2 total DR - 2 from Precision Attack).

*Martial Focus (Any Combat Skill Suite, 2) At first level and every level up, Thugs receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from any Combat Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

*Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Thug never really rises above his street rat origins: the Leadership, Administration, Rally, and Recruiting Advanced Skills all cost double.

Against Them All When they’re in the thick of it is when the Vanguard thrives. The Vanguard adds a free roll of their Honor Dice to their Attack rolls against 1 opponent when engaged with 2 or more enemies.

*In, Out, & Around Knowing how to slip and out of your opponent’s guards allow the Vanguard to get in and out of a jam quickly.

A Vanguard can use his full defense against an additional opponent if the two opponents are adjacent to each other and, should anyone try to disengage with him, he may take 5 Stress, in which case, he gains a free attack against them although this is at half damage.

*First Strike The Vanguard hits hard and hits first. Or at least, they try to.

At the beginning of combat, Vanguards receive a pool of Measure equal to 1 ft + 1 per every level they’ve obtained. When they first become Engaged with an opponent, they may spend ft from that pool to temporarily increase their Measure for the purposes of determining who strikes first.

So, a level 6 Vanguard would have a pool of 7 ft to work with. If wielding a Stiletto (Measure 1.5 ft) and closing in to Engage with an enemy wielding a Rapier (Measure 4 ft), he could spend 3 ft of it to allow him to get his hit off first, perhaps crippling his enemy before they have change to respond. Doing so would leave him with 3 ft in his pool for the rest of combat.

After that, combat resumes as normal, so he wouldn’t be able to attack the Rapier wielder if he is being Fended Off.

*Martial Focus (Any Combat Skill Suite, 2) At first level and every level up, Vanguards receive 2 free skill purchases. Primary Drawback

Vanguards are a position of Honor, and they don’t take charging into battle lightly. A Vanguard loses 1 Honor for every battle they’re not the first on either side to land a melee attack.

Servant Specialist Archetypes

Priest

Choose this Archetype if you want your companions to benefit from their Favor more and receivea kick-back for doing so.

Priests focus on shaping Miracles on other people rather than themselves, making them a good support Archetype for other classes. Their Miracles vary widely based on their patron Divine.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suite None

Priests receives 3 purchases of the Religion (The Priest’s Covenant) Advanced Skill for free

Archetype Abilities

Divine Covenant During Character Creation, a Priest selects 1 Covenant and 1 Cosmic Link, representing their deity’s sphere of influence and personality, respectively.

Covenants change which Miracles the Priest has access to while Cosmic Links change the ways you gain and spend Favor.

Anatomy of the Divine

Covenants

Divines have spheres of power that represent their aspects. These are codified into Covenants, or promises they make with their servants in exchange for a portion of the Divine’s power.

Cults of that Divine typically pick one, maybe two Covenants, of their chosen Divine to focus on.

For instance, Wülder, a Gothic god, watches over Raiding, Industry and Trade. There could be 3 different priests to Wülder, one focusing on his role as the Bridge Between Men, one seeing him as primarily a watcher over people going on cattle raids, and one worshiping him as the greater crafter and source of inspiration.

While they all worship the same Divine, the Miracles they can invoke in his name are different.

This means that priests of different cults may not get along with each other and there can even be intra-faith wars to purge heretics (those that focus on different Covenant from them).

The main bonus of Covenants is that they make Miracles within them easier to invoke. The specifics are elaborated on in the Invoking Miracles in Chapter 1.5: The Divine.

Cosmic Links

The Higher Planes are those that exist above the crude and primitive world and so, naturally, the Divines are linked to the events and behaviors of the celestial bodies that make their homes in the heavens.

A Cosmic Link is the specific celestial body that the Divine is associated with, and it usually gives an insight to the personality of the god. The Cosmic Link doesn’t necessarily mean your Patron is primarily the Divine of that Cosmic thing. So, a Divine associated with the moon might not be primarily a moon god, in the same way that while Venus was associated with the Morning Star, she was primarily viewed as a love goddess by the Romans. Still, your holy symbols may incorporate the moon motif commonly to represent her.

For clarification’s sake: when something says “decrease” in this section’s explanations, you go down the chart by that many steps. If it says “increase” go up, so increase will always be a bonus, and decrease will always be bad.

Comet

This is associated with fervent but fleeting powers. Whenever you invoke a Miracle, you gain two Honor Dice with a +3 but lose an additional Favor.

Evening Star

The evening star is a source of strength and beauty: it's the first star to rise in the evening. When you invoke a Miracle, if you roll below the average, you gain the average instead. So, if you roll d8 and get a 3 or less, you get 4 instead.

Moon

The moon is a steady force, its effects are subtle but constant. When you invoke a Miracle, if you roll two Honor Dice, keeping the highest.

Morning Star

The morning star is the last star in the heavens before day and is a lone watcher. When you invoke a Miracle and roll the minimum amount, you don’t lose any Favor.

Sun

The sun is the lord of heaven, strong and unbending, its power unquestioned. When you invoke a Miracle and roll the max amount, you don’t lose any Favor.

Cyclical

Often hailing from older, more primitive times, the Cyclical deity represents earthly inevitabilities in a dichotomy, sometimes with themselves and sometimes with an opposing deity. This can be Spring and Summer, life and death, creation and destruction. They may be weak now, but they will wax unstoppable. When you invoke a Miracle, and roll under its average, your next Miracle gains a +1. This is cumulative.

If you roll above its average, this bonus is then removed, and you get a -1 to your next Miracle. Again, this is cumulative and resets when you roll below.

So, if you have a d6! Honor Dice, and you roll a 2, the next Miracle you invoke gets a +1. That next Miracle you roll, you get a 3 for a total of 4 (because of the +1), giving you a +2 the next roll. On that roll, you roll a 5 for a total of 7 ( 5 + 2 ). The next Miracle you then invoke gets a -1

Shepherd Of The PeopleIf someone else prays for a Miracle within sight of the Priest, the Priest can spend his own Favor to increase the effective cost of that Miracle. He gains half, rounded down, of the Favor spent this way back. If the person is Anointed in the Priest’s cult, the Priest also receives another use of their Honor Die.

Honor Of The DivinesWhile invoking a Miracle, if you or someone you've spent Favor on rolls the maximum value on your Honor Die, you also gain an extra use of your Honor Die.

Favored Of The Divines The Priest’s mission isn’t a selfish one, but it does have some benefits and they’re loved for their devotion to their Divines beyond others. They add their total level to their Max Favor.

*Catechism By studying the holy texts, the Priest is about to discern the will of the Divines and better understand the Higher Realms. They receive a free purchase of the Religion (Divines) Advanced Skill at first level and at every level up.

Symbol Of Faith The Priest continues to find mooring in their holy symbol. This symbol doesn't help channel their holy power but doesn't help them keep the faith. When brought into a Confrontation that relates question their faith or going against their cult's tenets, they receive their Honor dice instead of the standard d6!.

Primary Drawback

A Priest has sworn themselves to a Higher Power and as a token of their Covenant, they must select a taboo; an action or thing they must never do, such as cut their hair or eat meat. This must be appropriate to the Divine and be approved by the GM before play begins.

Because of their Anointing, they're also unable to take another Servant Archetype as a Secondary, nor are they allowed to double double in this one.

Weird-adept Specialist Archetypes

Runegalder

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a weird-adept and are familiar with other games' spellcasters.

Unlike other magic-users, the Runegalder seals magical power in his bond, allowing him to not risk his body when working with supernatural forces. This allows him to cast quickly and consistently but limits the amount and flexibility of his spells.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suite Lore and Weirdcraft

Runegalders receive 3 free purchases of Literacy in their native language.

Archetype Abilities

Vancian Shaping The Runegalder chains the power of spells to various runes that he then invests, sealing them with his blood until he’s ready to release them.

Every 24 hours, the Runegalder can seal 3 spells. He can also bind an additional spell for every 3 levels he’s obtained. This means that he may cast those spells at any time by simply speaking the rune they’re linked to, once prepared.

Speaking the rune releases it from its binding so speaking the same rune again before he’s resealed it will not recast the spell - the spell’s power is gone.

He may cast a spell multiple times per day, but he’ll need to bind each instance of the spell to a different rune.

He may also unseal a rune without actually shaping it, but he is not able to immediately reseal a new rune right after.

The Runegalder's magic is 'dead' and so is less potent than other types of magic, thus allowing some to shrug off their spells.

When shaping a spell, the Runegalder rolls a d20! + 10 + their level. All those that would be affected by the spell roll a d20! + their level. If their total is above the Runegalder's, they're unaffected by his magic. This roll is made regardless of whether or not the person wants to be affected by the spell.

Arcane Components The Runegalder gains 3 additional random Rudiments and gains an additional 2 Rudiments every level.

Grimoire This is the Runegalder’s spell book and keeps the spells that he binds to runes. Unlike a normal spell book, however, the spells are scribed in meteorite ink and blood that seals their magic power in the text itself. This means that the Runegalder’s Grimoire does the channeling latently for him.

At first level he constructs 3 spells using his known Rudiments and an additional 1 at level up. These spells can’t have a Drain greater than 7 + their level (so at 1st level, his spells can only have a Drain of 8) when it is scribed into the Grimoire. A spell’s Rudiments cannot be redistributed and changed: spells in the Grimoire are immutable.

So, a 2nd level Runegalder scribes a new spell. He can max out at 7 Drain. He chooses to make it a Wall Shape (3 Drain), at Full Range (1 Drain), Near Origin (1 Drain) and for its Effect, he chooses Disorient (2 Drain), for a total of 7. He could have also made it Chain Shape instead for 1 Drain, and Stacked the Disorient Effect, if he wanted.

Note that spells cast by the Runegalder only last their Interval unless their Effect is stacked, in which case, it either increases that Interval by a like amount or gains the Stacking bonus but not both. This is decided when the spell is cast.

Spells inscribed at different levels count as completely different spells and must be sealed to different runes. So, if the Runegalder has a 8 SP Flash Grenade and a 11 SP Flash Grenade, he would need two runes to cast both. He couldn’t simply bind “Flash Grenade” to one rune and then choose which he wanted to use in the moment.

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft, 1) Through the course of her career, a Runegalder seeks to further their knowledge of the occult. They receive 1 free purchase of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite at every other level up.

Primary Drawback

The Runegalder uses his body as a prison for latent magical energies which wears on a person over time. He ages at twice the normal rate.

They also find other types of Weirdshaping strange and so are unable to take another Weird-Adept Archetype as their Secondary, however, they are able to double specialize in their Primary.
