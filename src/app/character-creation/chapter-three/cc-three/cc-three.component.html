Introduction

Archetypes represent your character’s background training and starting skills and there are two types of Archetypes: Generalist and Specialist.

Types of Archetypes

Generalist Archetypes

These Archetypes represent the overall theme.

For instance, Fighter Archetypes tend to be good at things related to combat. So, if you’re looking to make a combat focused character, you can look there. They are Expert, Weird-Adept, Servant, Socialite, and Fighter.

Generalists will have a wide variety of discounts within their theme that are good for just getting straight bonuses.

Specialist Archetypes

These represent a more focused version of the Generalist Archetypes. They’re more expensive but come with additional powers.

Generalist Archetypes

Expert

Choose this Archetype if you want to use your wits to defeat your opponent or avoid combat altogether.

Experts are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines, magic, or their sword arm as a source of strength. They tend to rely on Intellect and Willpower, primarily, though there are Experts that focus on Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution for more physical skills.

These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

Fighter

Choose this Archetype if you want to kill monsters and worthy foes in mortal combat and like thinking on your feet.

War is a ladder by which capable people are able to climb the ranks of society. Fighters focus on combat and battle, either by training and discipline or by out maneuvering their opponent. Unsurprisingly, this Archetype focuses on Constitution, but how you approach combat may decide whether you favor Strength, Dexterity, or even Intellect or Willpower.

Servant

Choose this Archetype if you want harness powers beyond the mortal realm to alter the battlefield and circumstances to your favor.

Servants represent those that have devoted themselves to higher causes, lifting their eyes from the Middle Realm to a more pure order, whether that’s the Divines, the Ancestral Spirits, or the Bones of Ymir. Servants play the best with others, often modifying the other Archetype’s powers with Divine assistance.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suites Trades and Lore

Servants get 3 free purchases of the Religion Advanced Skill for free.

Archetype Abilities

*Called & Chosen A Servant seeks to do the will of those who sent them, furthering their agendas in the Middle Realm and so they are blessed. They add their total level to their Max Favor.

Simple Faith A Servant that's not been initiated into the deeper mysteries of their cult harbors a faith that is simple and pure before their patrons. At every level after the first, they gain a permanent +1 to their Max Favor. Unlike Called & Chosen, this bonus isn't removed when the Servant Specializes, although it no longer grows.

Depths Of Devotion A Servant understand the bonds of giving your life to a cause greater than your own and its strengths. They gain a free Devotion to their Divine, starting at 8. They also start Anointed in their cult.

*Former Life A Servant can be called from anywhere and any walk of life. Select any Archetype ability; This can be from a Generalist or a Specialist Archetype. Former Life has all the same effects as that ability, though it still counts as a separate leveling ability.

This ability stays with you after you replace the Servant Generalist Archetype, however, if it is a leveling ability, it stops leveling, even if your pick the Specialist Archetype it's from.

Symbol Of Faith As part of their initiation, the Servant receives a holy symbol of their faith. This symbol doesn't help channel their holy power but does help them keep the faith. When brought into a Confrontation that relates question their faith or going against their cult's tenets, they receive their Grit dice instead of the standard d6!.

*Breath of Knowledge A Servant is a well-versed individual, regardless of their faith. At every level, including the first, Servants get either 2 free purchases of any Advanced Combat Skill, 4 free purchases of any Advanced non-Combat Skill, or they can increase their cult-related Devotions by 3.

Purchases of Skills can’t be doubled up on each other, even if they come from other sources (Skill Focus, Martial Focus, or Personality Focus).

Primary Drawback

None

Socialites are people people. They seem to get a real kick out of interacting with others, especially if it ends with them getting their way. While others' first weapons are swords, a Socialite's first weapon is their charm. Unsurprisingly, Socialites tend to rely on Intellect and Presence.

Weird-adept

Choose this Archetype if you want an eclectic set of powers that you can apply to most situations in a creative manner but may not be helpful for all problems.

Through much study and/or natural talent, Weird-Adepts can channel the Painted World, using its power to alter reality. Weird-Adepts rely more on clever applications of spells rather than outright doing damage and so can often subtly shift the playing field in their favor. They rely mainly on Intellect and Willpower since they need a high Stress Threshold as it is extremely stressful to shape Weird.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suite Weirdcraft twice

Archetype Abilities

Applied Folkweird While anyone can shape a simple cantrip, the Weird-Adepts knows how to apply those lessons to shape a real spell. After 10 seconds of concentration (they can move up to a Jog and make Checks, although they do so on a d10!, including Attack and Defense Checks) with at least 1 hand free and the ability to speak, the Weird-Adept generates a number of Spell Points equal to their Weirdcraft Skill Suite Rank or the applicable Weirdcraft Advanced Skill's Rank which must be immediately spent to shape a spell.

Any SP remaining after the initial Shaping of a spell becomes fixed within it; these may only be used to extend its Interval per the normal rules, and each spell tracks its SP separately. The Weird-Adept can begin concentrating to gather more SP immediately after Shaping a spell, but these new SP can only be used to Shape a new spell, not added to an existing one.

Should the Weird-Adept not be able to get enough SP to even get 1 Interval, they can take Stress equal to the SP they're missing in Weirdburn to get at least 1 Interval of the spell up and running.

Bending reality and shaping primordial force is not an easy thing to do and nature doesn’t like it. When they first shape a spell, they take 1 Stress for every 10 Drain (rounded up) the spell has +5 for every Category above Never that their Fatigue Threshold is at (5 for Critical, 10 for Wounded, 15 for Bloodied, etc.). This is a one-time cost so continuing to shape the same spell doesn't further Stress them out (as long as they don't suffer weirdburn, of course).

Rionhuld is a Weird-Adept/Fighter with a Weirdcraft of 4 and an Animism of 7. He goes to shape the Weaponsharp Spell in preparation for battle. He’s 6 seconds into the 10 second wait time when they’re attacked. He manages to dodge the outlaw’s blade, even defending on a d10! and, 4 seconds later, his spell takes form.

Because Weaponsharp is an Investiture spell, he gets 7 SP to shape with, so he picks the Circle and Burst Rudiments, giving it a drain of 6.

The spell takes effect, he takes 6 Stress from the fallout (since his Fatigue is Critical) and immediately begins shaping again, this time, he goes for Haste. After 10 seconds, he gets 4 SP because he doesn’t have the Animism Advanced Skill so uses his Weirdcraft Skill Suite Rank. He goes for Personal and Aura but he doesn’t have the SP to cover that entirely so Weirdburns for the difference. This gives him 13 Stress (7 from the Weirdburn and 6 from normal fallout).

Note that Rionhuld can’t add those 4 SP to the Weaponsharp already shaped: he needs to put it towards a new spell.

Rudimentary Knowledge Weird-Adepts know the basics of shaping the Weird and so start out with 5 random Rudiments. They gain a new one every 3 levels after that.

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft Skill Suite) At every even level, not including the first, Weird-Adept receives 1 free purchase of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite.

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft, 2) At every level, including the first, Weird-Adept receives 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Weirdcraft Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

Primary Drawback

None

Weird-Adept Double Down

Rudimentary Knowledge

Start with 9 Rudiments, with 2 being your choices and the rest random. Gain a new one every other level.

Applied Folkweird

Shaping only takes 7 seconds, instead of 10.

Skill Focus (Weirdcraft Skill Suite)

You get these are every level up, not including the first, instead of every other.

Skill Focus (Weirdcraft, 2) to (Weirdcraft, 3)

You get 3 Weirdcraft Advanced Skills instead of 2.

Acquiring Rudiments

At 1st level and as dictated by your Archetypes, you’ll have the opportunity to acquire new Rudiments, which you can then use to shape spells. You’ll do so by picking a Rudiment table and then rolling on it. If you roll a Rudiment you already have, you can reroll for free until a new one comes up. For Effects, you roll on the Effects Rudiments table and then go to the corresponding sub-table.

On all tables, you can modify your roll by up to 1 for every 3 Ranks of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite you have. This does wrap around so, if you have Weirdcraft of 9 and roll a 11 on the Animism Effects Table, you can choose any Rudiment between 8 and 2. You may also spend 1 CrP to reroll any of the tables, even during level up.

Players that seek out the knowledge to gain a specific Rudiment during play can forgo this roll and simply gain that Rudiment.

At first level, it is required that you have at least 1 Rudiment from each type (so, 1 Shape Rudiment, 1 Origin, and at least 1 Effect) but once that’s satisfied, they can go back.

You can also purchase additional Rudiments for 30 CrP a pop, although, if that's the case, you get to choose the Rudiment.

Roll on this the Effects Rudiments table to determine which Effects Table to roll on. If you have a Rank in a Weirdcraft Advanced Skill in a specific Tradition, you can skip the Effects Rudiments table and go straight to rolling on any table you have training in.

Expert Specialist Archetypes

Adventurer

Choose this Archetype if you want a good range of skills and be able to help others in Skill Challenges.

Adventurers gain the most free skills of all Archetypes allowing for the largest breath of knowledge, however, they’re cheaper skills. They can also help others, giving them a chance to try again at Skill Checks they fail.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suites Two Different, non-Lore, non-Combat Skill Suites

Archetype Abilities

*Helping Hand The Adventurer is not just about knowing things but also giving others a helping hand in times of need. When another person uses an Adventurer’s help, either as an Asset, or by him Helping Out or Preparation, they receive an additional +1 to their roll at every odd level, including the first.

Middle Name: Danger For the Adventurer, danger is calming and straightforward and that’s what they crave. Whenever an Adventurer takes damage, they can heal half that amount in Stress, rounded up.

*Outthinking While they may not be the best with the blade, the Adventurer is often able to beat their opponents by outthinking them. Gain a use of your Grit Dice whenever you make a Check with a Str-, Con-, or Dex-based Skill that could change combat, successful or not. Gain an additional one for every 3 levels after that.

Skill Focus (Any < 7 CrP, 7) At every level, including first, Adventurers receive 7 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any non-combat Advanced Skills under 7 CrP in base cost (before Skill Adept). These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All non-Combat Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Primary Drawback

Adventurers tend to eschew book smarts for street smarts: the Lore Skill Suite and any Lore Advanced Skills cost double if he's purchasing them (so he can still pick up Lore Skills under 7 CrP as part of his Skill Focus).

Ranger

Choose this Archetype if you want to have a good range of movement skills, use your team to overcome Obstacles, but also move quicker on and off the battlefield.

The Ranger’s strengths rely mostly in their survival skills, making them excellent to have in the wilderness but they also gain a bonus to their movement that allows them to out-pace others during skirmishes.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suite Athletics

Archetypes Abilities

*Quick Footed The Ranger relies on being able to cross great swaths of land quickly, chasing down their prey. At every odd level, including the first, the Ranger increases his base movement rate categories by 2.5 foot + 2.5 per Movement Category above a Walk. He can't increase it again until all other movement categories have been increased in a similar manner.

The Ranger also gains a +1 to their ranged defenses for every 10 ft of additional movement they have.

For example, a first-level, human Ranger can choose Crawling, Walking, Jogging, Running, Sprinting. If he chooses Walking, his Walk becomes 7.5 ft / second (because it is +2.5 since Walk is 0 Movement Categories above Walk). At level 3, he can choose Crawling, Jogging, Running, or Sprinting, but not Walking. If he chooses Running, his Running speed becomes 20 ft / second (since Running is 2 Categories above Walk, adding that base +5).

After he's increased all, he can go back and increase his Walk again.

Put Your Heads Together For survival, communication is key and doing so quickly is often extremely important. When using each other as Assets, all allies are counted as 1 Organization category better. If they're already Good, they instead communicate their ideas in 1d4! Minutes.

*Hiking Conditioning A Ranger is used to running and jumping for hours on end with equipment on their back so gain a +1S to their Carry every odd level, including the first.

Skill Focus (Athletics, 5) At every level, including the first, Rangers receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Athletics Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All non-Combat Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Primary Drawback

A Ranger finds armor restricting and irritating: all Armor Advanced Skills are 2 CrP more expensive and the Armor Skill Suite is 10 CrP more expensive.

Sage

Choose this Archetype if you want to play an extremely knowledge person as well as use that knowledge to give bonuses to your company.

The Sage gets some of the most expensive and cerebral skills for free but also, because of his intense training, he has the ability to instantly reroll any skill he chooses.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suites Lore Twice

Archetype Abilities

*Study HardThe Sage’s training doesn’t just revolve around studying but also on how to study, allowing them better recall of information than those without such mental discipline. The Sage gains 1 Preparation per session that they can use on any Int, Wis, or Pre Check. This increases by 1 every odd level, not including 1st.

*Teacher The Sage doesn't just study to learn, he also studies to learn how to teach others. When he helps out with an Int, Will, or Pres Skill Check, he takes 2 less Stress.

*I Know This Even if the Sage doesn't know the answer, they might know some auxiliary information about the subject. On any Int, Wis, or Pre, you always get 1 Boon, even if you fail. You gain any additional Boon every 5 levels.

Skill Focus (Lore, 5) At every level, including the first, Sages receive 5 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skill from the Lore Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Focus (Any, 3) At every level, including the first, Sages receive 3 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skill from any Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level but are applied after Skills have been bought by the Character.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All non-Combat Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

Versatile Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All any Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts. This can't be taken as part of the Human's Skillful ability.

Primary Drawback

The Sage has spent most of his time buried in study and so may take a bit longer to react to reality: they always roll an initiative die 1 size larger.

Fighter Specialist Archetypes

Cateran

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a fighter that flanks their opponent, keeping them from moving or engaging others in combat.

The Cateran is all about flanking your opponent and keeping other fighters locked down. As such, they work best with other fighters.

Vitality Die

d6

Skills

Main Skill Suite Strategy Twice

Archetype Abilities

Baby-sitting While an individual Cateran may vary in his ability to take care of infants, they all share the ability to keep combatants locked down.

Whenever a person engaged with the Cateran tries to disengage, the Cateran gets an immediate free attack that, if it hits, deals half damage. This happens regardless of where they are on the Clock and doesn’t affect when their next attack happens.

Closed Openings Flanking is the art of dividing people’s attention and the Cateran has perfected that art. Anyone flanked by a Cateran lowers their attack die by 1 size, regardless of who they’re attacking.

*Mutieren A Cateran knows to move their blade to get into places their opponents wouldn't expect. If facing an enemy whose weapon has a base Parry or who is carrying a shield, the Cateran receives a bonus to Attack equal to halve their level, rounded up. This ability isn't effective against people who gained their Parry solely through training.

*Shadowing By keeping close to their opponent, the Cateran can negate people’s defensive posturing. By taking 7 Stress, a Cateran can negate any bonuses an opponent gets from moving, whether that’s shifting, stepping back, or whatever and also stops their opponent from moving.

They lower this Stress gain by 1 every 3 levels after that, down to 3.

Bum Rush A Cateran can also use his allies to more effectively flank their opponents. When flanking from the side, you and your allies lower your target’s defense die by 1, including the dice of those using shields (though they still get a chance to Parry). This doesn’t, however, give you any benefit for attacking from directly behind.

*Martial Focus (Melee Weapons, 2) At every level, including the first, Caterans receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Melee Combat Skill Suite. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Cateran is a group fighter and fighting alone can be unnerving for him. When engaged with enemies alone and not flanking, the Cateran is unable to use his Integrity but can still use his Grit Dice.

Knight

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a heavy armored tank that inspires others to fight hard.

The Knight focuses on shield and armor, and with their larger Vitality Die, they tend to be pretty tanky. Additionally, their Inheritance allows them to get the more expensive weapons for free.

Vitality Die

d10

Skills

Main Skill Suite Strategy

Archetype Abilities

*Chivalry From their strict code comes a strength and confidence that others can only dream of and envy. For the duration of a combat, you gain a number of Grit Dice equal to your level. You can only apply 1 of these bonus Grit Die to a single roll, though that doesn’t stop you adding Grit Dice that you’ve gotten from other sources.

*Call To Valor A Knight is a hero and inspires others to rise above. For the duration of a combat, all allies gain access to a pool of Grit Die equal to the Knight's level. They can only apply one of these bonus Grit Dice to a single roll, though that doesn’t stop them adding Grit Dice that they got from other sources. The Grit Dice is the size of the person’s Grit Dice that redeems it.

Inheritance This character may have had an extra leg up in life, allowing them the opportunity to start with better equipment than others. They gain an additional 250 sc that must be spent on one item that represents an heirloom passed down to them. They also gain 15 sc for each CrP cashed in at the end of Character Creation instead of the normal 10.

*Martial Focus (Special, 2) At every level, including the first, Knights receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Armor, Shield, or Melee Combat Skill Suites. These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Combat Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts.

Primary Drawback

The Knight is a creature of honor and respects Integrity where they find it. They may be killers, but they have a code, whether articulated or not. A Knight may never use a long-ranged weapon on a sentient creature.

Soldier

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a warrior that sticks close to the rest of the company and allows them to adapt more quickly to changing situations.

Bonfire characters are not meant to stand alone, and this goes double for the Soldier, who works the best with others. They’re able to shadow others around the battlefield and really focus on being good at one or two weapons.

Vitality Die

d8

Skills

Main Skill Suite Strategy

Archetype Abilities

Small Unit Tactics Soldiers work as a team. If they or anyone they’re adjacent (including enemies) uses a tactic that moves them (Shift, Step Back, etc.), the Soldier or their adjacent ally may also use that Tactic or Shift with no penalties.

*Buddy System Soldiers fight as a unit, moving and acting as one where possible. When an adjacent ally switches Tactics, the Soldier may immediately switch to that Tactic as well. On top of this, they, and adjacent allies, also receive a bonus to their Trauma Threshold equal to the Soldier’s level.

*Martial Focus (Any Combat Skill Suite Relating to Weapon Specialization, 2) At every even level, Soldiers receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills from the Combat Skill Suite relating to their weapon specialization (so if you picked Lucerne, this would be the Melee Skill Suite, for example). These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different Martial Focuses) but are applied after other Combat Skills have been bought by the Character.

Soldier's Martial Adept This character has a knack for combat. All Advanced Armor & Shield Skills have their cost reduced by 2 CrP. They also gain a 20% discount to all Armor & Shield Skill Suites. This stacks with other Martial Adepts. This can't be taken as part of the Human's Skillful ability.

Primary Drawback

A Soldier focuses so much on specializing that they often forget how to generally apply knowledge. All Ranged Weapon and Melee Skill Suites are 15 CrP more expensive.

d4

Skills

Archetype Abilities

Anatomy of the Divine

Covenants

Divines have spheres of power that represent their aspects. These are codified into Covenants, or promises they make with their servants in exchange for a portion of the Divine’s power.

Cults of that Divine typically pick one, maybe two Covenants, of their chosen Divine to focus on.

For instance, Wülder, a Gothic god, watches over Raiding, Industry and Trade. There could be 3 different priests to Wülder, one focusing on his role as the Bridge Between Men, one seeing him as primarily a watcher over people going on cattle raids, and one worshiping him as the greater crafter and source of inspiration.

While they all worship the same Divine, the Miracles they can invoke in his name are different.

This means that priests of different cults may not get along with each other and there can even be intra-faith wars to purge heretics (those that focus on different Covenant from them).

The main bonus of Covenants is that they make Miracles within them easier to invoke. The specifics are elaborated on in the Invoking Miracles in Chapter 1.5: The Divine.

Cosmic Links

The Higher Planes are those that exist above the crude and primitive world and so, naturally, the Divines are linked to the events and behaviors of the celestial bodies that make their homes in the heavens.

A Cosmic Link is the specific celestial body that the Divine is associated with, and it usually gives an insight to the personality of the god. The Cosmic Link doesn’t necessarily mean your Patron is primarily the Divine of that Cosmic thing. So, a Divine associated with the moon might not be primarily a moon god, in the same way that while Venus was associated with the Morning Star, she was primarily viewed as a love goddess by the Romans. Still, your holy symbols may incorporate the moon motif commonly to represent her.

For clarification’s sake: when something says “decrease” in this section’s explanations, you go down the chart by that many steps. If it says “increase” go up, so increase will always be a bonus, and decrease will always be bad.

Comet

This is associated with fervent but fleeting powers. Whenever you invoke a Miracle, you gain two Grit Dice with a +3 but lose an additional Favor.

Evening Star

The evening star is a source of strength and beauty: it's the first star to rise in the evening. When you invoke a Miracle, if you roll below the average, you gain the average instead. So, if you roll d8 and get a 3 or less, you get 4 instead.

Moon

The moon is a steady force, its effects are subtle but constant. When you invoke a Miracle, you gain a free reroll on any Grit Dice that roll below average but you have to keep the reroll.

Morning Star

The morning star is the last star in the heavens before day and is a lone watcher. When you invoke a Miracle and roll a 1, you don’t lose any Favor.

Sun

The sun is the lord of heaven, strong and unbending, its power unquestioned. When you invoke a Miracle and roll the max amount, you don’t lose any Favor.

Cyclical

Often hailing from older, more primitive times, the Cyclical deity represents earthly inevitabilities in a dichotomy, sometimes with themselves and sometimes with an opposing deity. This can be Spring and Summer, life and death, creation and destruction. They may be weak now, but they will wax unstoppable. When you invoke a Miracle, and roll under its average, your next Miracle gains a +1. This is cumulative.

If you roll above its average, this bonus is then removed, and you get a -1 to your next Miracle. Again, this is cumulative and resets when you roll below.

So, if you have a d6! Grit Dice, and you roll a 2, the next Miracle you invoke gets a +1. That next Miracle you roll, you get a 3 for a total of 4 (because of the +1), giving you a +2 the next roll. On that roll, you roll a 5 for a total of 7 ( 5 + 2 ). The next Miracle you then invoke gets a -1

Shepherd Of The People If someone else prays for a Miracle within sight of the Priest, the Priest can spend his own Favor to increase the power of that Miracle. This just treats the Favor he spent the same as Favor spent by the person praying except that he gains half, rounded down, of all the Favor spent this way back. If the person is Anointed in the Priest’s cult, the Priest also receives another use of their Grit Die.

Honor Of The Divines While invoking a Miracle, if you or someone you've spent Favor on rolls the maximum value on your Grit Die, you also gain an extra use of your Grit Die.

Favored Of The Divines The Priest’s mission isn’t a selfish one, but it does have some benefits and they’re loved for their devotion to their Divines beyond others. They add their total level to their Max Favor.

*Catechism By studying the holy texts, the Priest is about to discern the will of the Divines and better understand the Higher Realms. They receive a free purchase of the Religion (Divines) Advanced Skill at every level, including the first.

Symbol Of Faith The Priest continues to find mooring in their holy symbol. This symbol doesn't help channel their holy power but does help them keep the faith. When brought into a Confrontation that relates question their faith or going against their cult's tenets, they receive their Grit Dice instead of the standard d6!.

*Breath of Knowledge A Servant is a well-versed individual, regardless of their faith. At every level, including the first, Priests get either 2 free purchases of any Advanced Combat Skill, 4 free purchases of any Advanced non-Combat Skill, or they can increase their cult-related Devotions by 3.

Purchases of Skills can’t be doubled up on each other, even if they come from other sources (Skill Focus, Martial Focus, or Work on Your Personality).

Primary Drawback

A Priest has sworn themselves to a Higher Power and as a token of their Covenant, they must select a taboo; an action or thing they must never do, such as cut their hair or eat meat. This must be appropriate to the Divine and be approved by the GM before play begins.

Because of their Anointing, they're also unable to take another Servant Archetype as a Secondary, nor are they allowed to double down in this one.

Weird-adept Specialist Archetypes

Runegalder

Choose this Archetype if you want to play a weird-adept and are familiar with other games' spellcasters.

Unlike other magic-users, the Runegalder seals magical power in his bond, allowing him to not risk his body when working with supernatural forces. This allows him to cast quickly and consistently but limits the amount and flexibility of his spells.

Vitality Die

d4

Skills

Main Skill Suite Lore and Weirdcraft

Runegalders receive 3 free purchases of Literacy in their native language.

Archetype Abilities

Vancian Shaping The Runegalder chains the power of spells to various runes that he then invests, sealing them with his blood until he’s ready to release them.

Every 24 hours, the Runegalder can seal 3 spells. He can also bind an additional spell for every 3 total levels he’s obtained. This means that he may cast those spells at any time by simply speaking the rune they’re linked to, once prepared, which only takes 1 Second, however, the Weirding is weaker when Shaped this way: for every Boon a person gets on resisting the spell, they drop the Order by 2, instead of 1.

Speaking the rune releases it from its binding so speaking the same rune again before he’s resealed it will not recast the spell - the spell’s power is gone.

He may cast a spell multiple times per day, but he’ll need to bind each instance of the spell to a different rune.

He may also unseal a rune without actually shaping it, but he is not able to immediately reseal a new rune right after.

Arcane Components The Runegalder gains 8 random Rudiments and gains an additional 2 random Rudiments every level, not including the first.

Grimoire This is the Runegalder’s spell book and keeps the spells that he binds to runes. Unlike a normal spell book, however, the spells are scribed in meteorite ink and blood that seals their magic power in the text itself. This means that the Runegalder’s Grimoire does the channeling latently for him.

At first level, he constructs 3 spells using his known Rudiments and an additional 1 every total level after that. These spells can’t have a Drain greater than 7 + their total level (so at 1st level, his spells can only have a Drain of 8) when it is scribed into the Grimoire. A spell’s Rudiments cannot be redistributed and changed: spells in the Grimoire are immutable.

So, a 2nd level Runegalder scribes a new spell. He can max out at 7 Drain. He chooses to make it a Wall Shape, Near Origin (4 Drain) and for its Effect, he chooses Disorient (2 Drain), for a total of 7. He could have also made it Chain Shape instead for 1 Drain, and Stacked the Disorient Effect, if he wanted.

Note that spells cast by the Runegalder only last their Interval unless their Effect is stacked, in which case, it either increases that Interval by a like amount or gains the Stacking bonus but not both. This is decided when the spell is cast.

Spells inscribed at different levels count as completely different spells and must be sealed to different runes. So, if the Runegalder has a 8 SP Flash Grenade and a 11 SP Flash Grenade, he would need two runes to cast both. He couldn’t simply bind “Flash Grenade” to one rune and then choose which he wanted to use in the moment.

*Skill Focus (Weirdcraft, 1) Through the course of her career, a Runegalder seeks to further their knowledge of the occult. They receive 1 free purchase of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite at every odd level, including the first.

Primary Drawback

The Runegalder uses his body as a prison for latent magical energies which wears on a person over time. He ages at twice the normal rate.

They also find other types of Weirdshaping strange and so are unable to take another Weird-Adept Archetype as their Secondary, however, they are able to double specialize in their Primary.
