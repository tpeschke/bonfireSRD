Leveling Terminology

When an ability says "At first level and every 3rd level after that..." or "at every 3rd level, including the first...", it means the ability increases at 1st, 4th, 7th, 10th, etc. If an ability says "At every 3rd level", that means 3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th, etc.

Classes

Expert

Choose this Class if you want to use your wits to defeat your opponent or avoid combat altogether.

Experts are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines, magic, or their sword arm as a source of strength. They tend to rely on Intellect and Willpower, primarily, though there are Experts that focus on Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution for more physical skills.

Skill Adept This character has a knack for skills. All non-Combat Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. They also gain a 10% discount to all non-Combat Skill Suites. This stacks with other Skill Adepts.

These free purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level (unless from different skill focuses) but are applied after Skills have been bought by the character.

Fighter

Choose this Class if you want to kill monsters and worthy foes in mortal combat and like thinking on your feet.

War is a ladder by which capable people are able to climb the ranks of society. Fighters focus on combat and battle, either by training and discipline or by out maneuvering their opponent. Unsurprisingly, this Class focuses on Constitution, but how you approach combat may decide whether you favor Strength, Dexterity, or even Intellect or Willpower.

Socialite

Choose this Class if you want to talk your way out of problems and inspire others to give you your way.

Socialites are people people. They seem to get a real kick out of interacting with others, especially if it ends with them getting their way. While others' first weapons are swords, a Socialite's first weapon is their charm. Unsurprisingly, Socialites tend to rely on Intellect and Presence.

Weird-Adept

Choose this Class if you want an eclectic set of powers that you can apply to most situations in a creative manner but may not be helpful for all problems.

Through much study and/or natural talent, Weird-Adepts can channel the Painted World, using its power to alter reality. Weird-Adepts rely more on clever applications of spells rather than outright doing damage and so can often subtly shift the playing field in their favor. They rely mainly on Intellect and Willpower since they need a high Nerve as it is extremely stressful to shape Weird.

Applied Folkweird While anyone can shape a simple cantrip, the Weird-Adepts knows how to apply those lessons to shape a real spell. After 10 seconds of concentration (they can move up to a Jog and make Checks, although they do so on a d10!, including Attack and Defense Checks) with at least 1 hand free and the ability to speak, the Weird-Adept generates a number of Spell Points equal to their Weirdcraft Skill Suite Rank or the applicable Weirdcraft Advanced Skill's Rank which must be immediately spent to shape a spell.

Any SP remaining after the initial Shaping of a spell becomes fixed within it; these may only be used to extend its Interval per the normal rules, and each spell tracks its SP separately. The Weird-Adept can begin concentrating to gather more SP immediately after Shaping a spell, but these new SP can only be used to Shape a new spell, not added to an existing one.

Should the Weird-Adept not be able to get enough SP to even get 1 Interval, they can take Stress equal to the SP they're missing in Weirdburn to get at least 1 Interval of the spell up and running.

Bending reality and shaping primordial force is not an easy thing to do and nature doesnâ€™t like it. Acquiring Rudiments

At 1st level and as dictated by your Classes, youâ€™ll have the opportunity to acquire new Rudiments, which you can then use to shape spells. Youâ€™ll do so by picking a Rudiment table and then rolling on it. If you roll a Rudiment you already have, you can reroll for free until a new one comes up. For Effects, you roll on the Effects Rudiments table and then go to the corresponding sub-table.

On all tables, you can modify your roll by up to 1 for every 3 Ranks of the Weirdcraft Skill Suite you have. This does wrap around so, if you have Weirdcraft of 9 and roll a 11 on the Animism Effects Table, you can choose any Rudiment between 8 and 2.

Players that seek out the knowledge to gain a specific Rudiment during play can forgo this roll and simply gain that Rudiment.

At first level, it is required that you have at least 1 Rudiment from each type (so, 1 Shape Rudiment, 1 Origin, and at least 1 Effect) but once thatâ€™s satisfied, they can go back.

You can also purchase additional Rudiments for 15 Integrity a pop, although, if that's the case, you get to choose the Rudiment.

Roll on this the Effects Rudiments table to determine which Effects Table to roll on. If you have a Rank in a Weirdcraft Advanced Skill in a specific Tradition, you can skip the Effects Rudiments table and go straight to rolling on any table you have training in.
