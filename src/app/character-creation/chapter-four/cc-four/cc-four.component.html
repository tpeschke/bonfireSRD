Flaws

Every person has flaws; markers of an eventful life, good or bad. Oftentimes they’re artifacts of a hard life, like a soldier who is missing an arm.

Flaw Severity

Flaws have a Severity that denotes how serious they are.

What this specifically means will be unique to the Flaw as some are worse than others just as a base line (hence the variable CrP bonuses).

Flaw Rank

Flaws can sometimes be applied to a Check, sometimes as a bonus and sometimes as a penatly. Either way, you'll need to know what its Rank is..

Recording Flaws

When recording Flaws on your character sheet, you'll typically want to do it like so:

Deaf (3) 4

The number in parentheses is the Severity and the number to the side is the Rank, in case it comes up in a Confrontation.

Roleplaying Out Flaws

Flaws never give you permission to cross lines or make people uncomfortable, whatever side of the screen you're on.

On the flip side, don't take Flaws that you would be, on some level, uncomfortable roleplaying out. If you have any doubt, discuss with your GM and other players before the game starts and, if you're still unsure, spend the CrP to reroll.

Conflicting Flaws

It is perfectly fine to have conflicting Flaws because people are like that sometimes.

Starting Flaws

There are two ways to select Flaws: Rolling and Cherry Picking. Rolling gives you more CrP per Flaw but Cherry Picking gives you more control.

Rolling Flaws

Flaw Table

Roll on the following Flaw Table and then on the corresponding table.

You can spend 1 CrP to reroll at any time for either table but once you’ve committed to rolling for a Flaw, you have to roll.

Each Flaw after the first loses 5 CrP of its value per previous Flaw to a minimum of 0 CrP received. So, the 3rd Flaw you roll will be at a -10 CrP.

All Flaws start at Severity 1 but you can choose to increase them as much as you want, up to the Severity Cap, at the time of rolling, gaining its Per Severity bonus for each Severity past the first it increases.

If you roll the same Flaws twice, you have to increase its Severity by at least 1. You may choose to further increase its Severity at this time as normal. If you’ve already reached the cap of that Flaw, you reroll for free.

Cherry Picking Flaws

For the roleplayers among us with a specific character idea in mind, you may Cherry Pick your Flaws.

Each Flaw after the first loses 10 CrP of its value per Flaw to a minimum of 0 CrP received. So, the 3rd Flaw you pick will be at a -20 CrP.

All Flaws start at Severity 1 but you can choose to increase them at the time of selection, gaining its Per Severity bonus for each Severity past the first it increases.

Note that some Flaws have a Severity cap (you can only get so blind, afterall), which is noted.

Ageusia

CrP Bonus 10

Per Severity 5

Severity Cap 10

Rank 1 + 1 per 2 Severity

This means that the character is lacking the ability to taste.

While fairly minor, it means that this character won’t heal Stress from food the same way that others will: subtract this Flaw’s Severity from the Relaxation you would get from a meal to a minimum of 0.

Haunted

CrP Bonus 30

Per Severity 20

Severity Cap 5

Rank 3 + 2 per Severity

Most commonly, this character has done something to dishonor his clan and has since been haunted by one of their ancestors, displeased by their actions.

The spirit takes the corporeal form of a rotting corpse wearing its death mask, bothering the possessed at all hours to restore his family’s honor and stop being such a failure. The ancestor will do nothing to protect their descendant and often make life harder for them when they can.

Unfortunately, decades, if not centuries, in the grave have made the ancestor more than a bit senile. Even if the character is to obtain Great Honor, the ancestor doesn’t care; what it really wants is for the character to sacrifice themselves in a heroic way or perform suicide.

This character reduces the amount of Stress healed Taking a Breather by this Flaw’s Severity and reduces the Relaxation that others would by its Severity, both to a minimum of 0.

Finally, they lose 1 Honor at the end of every session.

This Flaw may be paid off for half cost if the character has lasted 2 sessions in Great Honor, however it goes back up to full price if they slip out of Great Honor later.

Playing The Honored Dead

The Honored Dead may have been a person once but they're no longer their full personalities but merely shards of what they once were: their strongest characteristics shine through but the subtlety of the once living person is often lost.

So, Honored Dead will often only have 2 Characteristics. Often this is just their 2 Strongest Convictions but it can also be their Devotions, Flaws, Temperament, or even Goals, or some combination, that are used to inform how they interact with their kin.

Players should be encouraged to fill this out and know at least something about the person that's currently haunting them, although the GM gets the final approval.

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap n/a

Rank 2 + 1 per Severity

This character has issues dealing with stress.

They take an additional Stress equal to their Severity every time they’re inflicted with Stress.

No Depth Perception

CrP Bonus 20

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 6

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

This character has a hard, if not impossible, time judging distances of all kinds.

This character’s Measure is treated as 1 ft shorter for every point of Severity and everything is counted as 1 range category further for ranged weaponry for every point of Severity.

Additionally, they shrink their dice size by 1 for every point of Severity for Checks involving estimating distances.

Orphan

CrP Bonus 15

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 20

Rank 1 + 1.5 per Severity

Orphans are those whose parents have abandoned them and have had to learn to survive on their own in a society that doesn’t know what to do with them.

Reduce the Honor cap of this character by 1 for every point of Severity but gain a discount to either the Streetwise or Trades Skill Suites.

The Skill Suite of your choice’s cost is reduced by 5 and all of its Advanced Skills’ costs are reduced by 1.

Additionally, orphans often don’t have the resources of others and, when selecting their starting equipment, they remove 1 item per 3 points of Severity, rounded up. If selecting the “Blank Slate Kit” they lose 30 sc per Severity instead.

CrP Bonus 15

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap n/a

Rank 1 + 1 per Severity

This character is unnaturally afraid of something, beyond all reason and ration.

When faced with their phobia, they gain Stress equal to their phobia’s Severity for each time they have to interact with it (which is normally represented by some kind of Check).

This Flaw can be taken multiple times for different Phobias, each counted as their own separate Flaw with its own Severity.

You can, of course, choose whatever fear you feel fits your character, but we’ve provided a table you can use to generate one for you.

Poor

CrP Bonus 15

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap 10

Rank 1 + 1 per Severity

Some characters don’t have the financial leg up that others have.

When selecting equipment, remove 45 sc worth of equipment and/or starting cash per Severity instead.

Refugee

CrP Bonus 30

Per Severity 0

Severity Cap 1

Rank 3

Fleeing from their homeland for either economic, religious, or conflict reasons, this character finds themselves in a foreign land.

Their flight has forced them to leave much of their belongings behind, halving their starting cash and removing any items that go over their carry.

Their native language is not the main one for this area and the Language and Law of the country they’re fleeing to costs double as they had to learn it under stressful circumstances.

Sensitive

CrP Bonus 20

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 10

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

Things just get to this character more than others.

When they gain Stress during a Confrontation, they take an additional amount of Stress equal to their Severity.

Shaky Hands

CrP Bonus 20

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 10

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

This character has a tremor in their hands that can be a huge pain to deal with.

When firing ranged weapons, they increase their Recovery by the Severity of this Flaw.

Additionally, when doing anything that requires fine movement with their hands and fingers they can either increase the time equal to their Severity or take a penalty equal to their Severity.

The specifics of the time increase are determined by how long the task usually takes and are left up to the GM.

Slow-witted

CrP Bonus 15

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap 10

Rank 1 + 1 per Severity

This character takes a little longer than others to think through things. This Flaw doesn’t dictate how smart a person is, simply how fast they think.

When making an Int- or Pre-base Check, they can either increase the time equal to their Severity or take a penalty equal to their Severity.

The specifics of the time increase are determined by how long the task usually takes and are left up to the GM.

Stutter

CrP Bonus 20

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 6

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

This character has trouble communicating, either finding the right words or pronouncing them and that can make talking a bit of a challenge.

Communicating an idea will take longer than it normally would: as an Asset, you double their Organization.

Additionally, anytime you need to make a Language Check to be understood while speaking, reduce the Check die by 1 size per point of Severity.

Characters with this Flaw cannot be Weird-Adept Archetypes that require speech of any kind.

Thrall

CrP Bonus 25

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 10

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

Thralls are those taken in raids or the children of slaves. Technically, they’re ‘free’ but they have a social stigma that follows them.

When selecting equipment, thralls remove 10 sc per point of Severity of the character’s choice and start with no extra starting cash. If selecting the “Blank Slate Kit” they lose 30 sc per Severity instead.

Additionally, they have to overcome their cultivated respect/fear for those of a higher social caste: they gain the Devotion “Afraid of those in a High Social Caste” equal to their Severity.

Trick Knee

CrP Bonus 15

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap 10

Rank 1 + 1 per Severity

Something about the character’s knee isn’t aligned right.

When Running or Sprinting, if the character gets above a 4 on their unmodified damage roll their knee gives out, forcing them to limp at a Strolling speed for a number of seconds equal to this Flaw’s Severity before they can resume normal movement.

Vexed

CrP Bonus 25

Per Severity 30

Severity Cap 2

Rank 2 + 3 per Severity

This character finds it hard to keep their stress under control and things can quickly escalate for them.

This character’s Stress explodes on 1 value lower for each point of Severity they have in this Flaw.

So, if they have Severity 1 and they roll a d4! for their Stress, that dice explodes on a 4 and 3.

Vow Of Poverty

CrP Bonus 35

Per Severity 10

Severity Cap 19

Rank 3 + 1 per Severity

The Middle Realm corrupts and those that wish to come closer to the Gods’ Realm sometimes eschew worldly possessions.

At any time, they can only keep a max of 200 sc - 10 sc per Severity worth of cash, goods, or equipment on them for their use.

All other wealth is donated to any local cults or the needy (which doesn’t include fellow adventurers).

This vow is rewarded though: their Max Favor is increased by 1, regardless of Severity.

Weak

CrP Bonus 20

Per Severity 15

Severity Cap 19

Rank 2 + 1.5 per Severity

This character runs out of steam faster than others, even if their capacity for work is just the same as others.

For every point of Severity, lower the character’s starting Fatigue Threshold by 1 step. Nothing can be done to reverse this except buying this Flaw down.
