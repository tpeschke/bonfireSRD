Every person has things that carry with them; markers of an eventful life, good or bad. Oftentimes they’re artifacts of a hard life, like a soldier who is missing an arm, while other times it’s something under the skin:

Burdens

Burdens largely represent things that are outside of the character’s control that are emotional or related to a character’s backstory, like being an orphan or being Fated. These can only be gained during character creation and will give you unique penalties and roleplaying opportunities in play.

Injuries

Injuries represent physical scars that your character has. Perhaps they lost a leg in a childhood accident, or an eye in battle, or perhaps they were just born epileptic. These will give you unique penalties.

CrP Bonus

All Burdens & Injuries give you CrP that you can turn around and use to make your character stronger in other ways. The total bonus is Base Crp + (Rank * Per Rank). If you increase its Ranks later in character creation, you gain the difference. So, taking the Fated Burden at Rank 3 gives you 43 CrP ( 13 + (10 * 3) ). Increasing it up to 5 later gives you an additional 20 CrP.

Rank

Burdens & Injuries can sometimes be applied to a Check, sometimes as a bonus and sometimes as a penatly. Either way, you'll need to know what its Rank is, which is added or removed from a Check.

Unsure of the Rank?

If you're not sure what your Burden or Injury's Rank should be, you can just roll a d3 and use that for your Rank. That will work in most cases, except for Missing Eye.

Starting Burdens & Injuries

There are two ways to select Burdens & Injuries: Rolling and Cherry Picking. Rolling gives you more CrP per Burdens or Injuries but Cherry Picking gives you more control. You must commit to one: you cannot start by rolling and then Cherry Pick. You cannot choose to do both and choose the one you like more, either - once you start down the path, you're on it.

Cherry Picking

For the roleplayers among us with a specific character idea in mind, you may Cherry Pick your Burdens and Injuries.

Each Burden and Injuries after the first loses 10 CrP of its value to a minimum of 0 CrP received. So, the 3rd Burden or Injury you pick will be at a -20 CrP.

Rank Cap 5

Playing The Honored Dead

The Honored Dead may have been a person once but they're no longer their full personalities but merely shards of what they once were: their strongest characteristics shine through but the subtlety of the once living person is often lost.

So, Honored Dead will often only have 2 Characteristics. Often this is just their 2 Strongest Convictions but it can also be their Devotions, Flaws, Temperament, or even Goals, or some combination, that are used to inform how they interact with their kin.

Players should be encouraged to fill this out and know at least something about the person that's currently haunting them, although the GM gets the final approval.
