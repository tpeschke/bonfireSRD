Every person has things that carry with them; markers of an eventful life, good or bad. Oftentimes they’re artifacts of a hard life, like a soldier who is missing an arm, while other times it’s something under the skin:

Burdens

Burdens largely represent things that are outside of the character’s control that are emotional or related to a character’s backstory, like being an orphan or being Fated. These can only be gained during character creation and will give you unique penalties and roleplaying opportunities in play.

Injuries

Injuries represent physical scars that your character has. Perhaps they lost a leg in a childhood accident, or an eye in battle, or perhaps they were just born epileptic. These will give you unique penalties.

Character Creation Bonus

At character creation, Burdens & Injuries give you a bonus either to your Max Favor or as a discount to any Weirdcraft Advanced Skills. This bonus is on top of any other bonus (such as being Anointed or having a Skill Adept). You only get to choose one or the other and it cannot be changed after this step.

Starting Burdens & Injuries

There are two ways to select Burdens & Injuries: Rolling and Cherry Picking. Rolling gives you bigger bonuses but Cherry Picking gives you more control. You must commit to one: you cannot start by rolling and then Cherry Pick. You cannot choose to do both and choose the one you like more, either - once you start down the path, you're on it.

Haunted

Most commonly, this character has done something to dishonor his clan and has since been haunted by one of their ancestors, displeased by their actions.

The spirit takes the corporeal form of a rotting corpse wearing its death mask, bothering the possessed at all hours to restore his family’s honor and stop being such a failure. The ancestor will do nothing to protect their descendant and often make life harder for them when they can.

Unfortunately, decades, if not centuries, in the grave have made the ancestor more than a bit senile. Even if the character is able to obtain Full of Integrity, the ancestor doesn’t care; what it really wants is for the character to sacrifice themselves in a heroic way or perform suicide.

Minor This character reduces the amount of Relaxation gained per day by 3 and reduces the Relaxation that others would by 1, both to a minimum of 0.

Major This character reduces the amount of Relaxation gained per day by 16 and reduces the Relaxation that others would by 3, both to a minimum of 0. They lose 1 Integrity at the end of every session.

Playing The Honored Dead

The Honored Dead may have been a person once but they're no longer their full personalities but merely shards of what they once were: their strongest characteristics shine through but the subtlety of the once living person is often lost.

So, Honored Dead will often only have 2 Characteristics. Often this is just their 2 Strongest Convictions but it can also be their Relationships, Flaws, or even Goals, or some combination, that are used to inform how they interact with their kin.

Players should be encouraged to fill this out and know at least something about the person that's currently haunting them, although the GM gets the final approval.
