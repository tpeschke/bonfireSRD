Which Ancestry Should I Choose?

Your Ancestry effects character creation in 3 ways: Stat Score Modifiers, Gameplay Rewards, and Class Costs. If you're new to RPGs in generally, go for an Ancestry that interests you based on the flavor.

Personality

Each Ancestry has its only unique personality that might appeal to you. You can read about each's in their Personality section and see a bullet-point summary beyond that in "Characteristics", which will become important later in Character Creation.

Note that these are default Characteristics that assume that there is only one culture per ancestry and that these cultures are very similar to the stereotypes of that ancestry in popular fiction but your GM might have a separate list from which to pull from so check with them. If you’re using the official campaign setting, you’ll find that information under the Nations folder in the World Codex found here.

Stat Score Modifiers

These modify the base stats that you roll. You can use it to balance out your character's weak spots or you can use it to maximize what your character is already good it. We recommend the latter as Bonfire is a game built around teamwork: you'll have friends who will be good at things your character isn't.

Note that these adjustments can't bring score above 23 or below 1.

Gameplay Rewards

Each Ancestry is meant to encourage a certain type of play. For instance, orcs are highly destructive, so they reward players who are aggressive, while elves get rewarded for being careful with how they engage with others. These are noted under the Ancestry Temperament and Abilities sections in their summary.

Class Costs

Some Ancestries have cultural practices that look down or favor certain Classes. This is reflected in the Class Costs.

We who were trapped in prisons of flesh look to our forebears. For as the Alwealda ascended to return to the Divines, we, their children, will also ascend when we too have perfected ourselves in their sight.

The Elves are the first born of the Divines. Originally, the inhabitants of the Painted World, the source of all magic, they now inhabit the Middle Realm as mentally ethereal beings.

Appearance

Elves are slender beings that are widely considered classically beautiful, though many find their features alien and off putting. They uniformly have long ears that taper to a point at the end.

Their eyes are pure white, with no iris or pupil visible, this matched with their sharp teeth, can give them a feline turn to their face.

Elves naturally survive off the ebb and flow of magic that drifts through the air of the Painted World. While still present in the Middle Realm, these winds are a sliver of their natural magnitude and so Elves need to find other sources; the main one being through the magic inherent in all living creatures. While vegetables do have magic in them, an Elf would have to consume 100s of pounds of lettuce each day to not die of starvation. The only realistic way of getting enough magic to survive is through the consumption of meat, making them omnivores in theory but carnivores in practice.

Personality

To describe an Elf as “careful” would be a huge understatement. They tend to live longer than other Ancestries, not because some supernaturally long lifespan, but because they're always careful about their actions. They see other Ancestries as brash and impetuous while they're in turn seen as slow and cowardly.

It's not they're incapable of great emotion or quick action but elves seem to understand that with every action comes a consequence, whether that action was well meaning or not, and that this karmic force is more powerful than any other law or power.

However, once set on a course, it is hard to dissuade an elf, for they do nothing lightly.

In the days of yore, the Divines and the Giants fought in a war unending. And from their struggles fell their blood and sweat to the earth. From that mixture arose the first man and women, born of blood, sweat, and earth; the prices we must pay for all things.

Humans are the first natives of the Middle Realm. They are creatures born from strife, who’ve carved for themselves a place in the world through fire, steel, and grit.

Appearance

The Humans of the Middle Realm look like you and me. Their skin, eye, and hair color range widely, adapted to the region of that Human’s origin, with darker skins more common towards the equator.

If you don’t know what a Human looks like, I don’t know what to tell you.

Personality

The truth is Humans are odd and quirky individuals that seem to have the defining trait of their curiosity and ambition.

Truthfully, you probably have your own opinion about how humans behave and why they behave the way they do and nothing I write in this little blurb is going to convince you otherwise.

Days of ashes and nights of fire; we were great warriors then. But our swords and shields were stolen from us and now we, the nomads, search for things that no hand can hold. Where must we go? We who search the wasteland for our better selves?

Heirs to a great empire long since lost to the ages, the orcs cling to tradition in the wake of uncertain and dark times. Some say that they value only strength but to truly known an orc is to know the soul of honor.

Appearance

Orcs have dark green or grey skin with porcine nose and tusks that jut up from their lower jaw. They tend to be stocky, with thick muscles and hunched shoulders.

Their hair is wiry, closer to steel wool than anything else and their eyes are completely black, with no pupils or irises, giving them an indescribable inhumanness to it.

Naturally, orcs are scavengers, able to survive on practically anything, no matter how rotten or foul.

Personality

An orc’s life is defined by struggle: from birth to death, they conceptualize the universe as having a vendetta against them. This can often give them a martyr complex and make them morose traveling companions at times because they feel the weight of the world.

They can also come across as aggressive, which some have pointed out is a defense mechanism, but orcs would simply scoff at that.

There’s nothing an orc values more than their honor and the chance to prove themselves against a worthy foe. This is often seen in their deities across orcish cultures, which are almost exclusively pantheons of their greatest foes. It is taken for granted that you are only as good as your enemies.
