Skill Suites Vs Advanced Skills

You’ll notice that there are two types of Skills in Bonfire: Skill Suites and Advanced Skills.

So, what’s the difference?

Skill Suites

These skills represent broad swathes of knowledge that cover a variety of subjects. Suites are hard to increase because there’s so much ground to cover but can be used if you don’t have the specific Advanced Skill.

An example would of this would be using the Survival Suite if you didn’t have the Signaling Advanced Skill.

Advanced Skills

These skills are more tightly focused, covering one intellectual discipline. They’re easier to master but may not be applicable in every case.

An example of this would be First Aid, Fire Building, and Scavenging are all Skills in the Survival Skill Suite, while Law, History, and Medicine are all Skills in the Lore Skill Suite.

Acquiring Skills

Throughout your character’s career, she’ll acquire and improve her skills. Both types of skills are purchased with CrP, that is spent either at Character Creation or when leveling up.

All skills have a unique cost that represents how easy or difficult they are to master. You pay the price as noted in the section below, you improve/purchase the skill.

Rank

If you haven't already calculated your starting Rank for your Skill Suites, you need to go back to Step 8 of Chapter 2.1: Step-by-Step Overview and do that before purchasing any additional Ranks in your Suites or Advanced Skills.

If you haven’t purchased an Advanced Skill before, its current Rank is equal to the Rank of the mother Skill Suite and increased by 1 from there.

When you buy a Skill Suite, if you've already bought Ranks in its children Advanced Skill, you increase those Ranks by 1 as well. This includes Skills you can buy multiple times with different subsets, like the Craft Advanced Skill and includes Training an Untrained Skill Suite.

Skill Suites cost their Base Cost - your full Int stat + (Current Rank * 10).

If you’re Untrained in that Skill Suite, “Current Rank”, the cost is just Base Cost - your full Int stat and gives you a Rank 0.

Advanced Skills cost their Base Cost + (Current Rank * 3).

You’re still able to purchase Ranks in Advanced Skills in Skill Suites you're Untrained in, with the initial purchase just being the Base Cost and putting that Skill at Rank 0, but it does mean you can roll the Check on a d20!.

If you have any discounts from your Archetypes (through Skill or Martial Focuses, for example), those discounts apply after all above calculations. Skill Costs have a minimum cost of 1 CrP.

Non-Combat Check Mods

For non-Combat Skills, each one is related to one or more applicable Stats, these are the stats who’s Check Mod you’re going to add to the roll when you make a Check.

If there is more than more than 1 Stat listed, for Skill Suite, use the lowest and, for Advanced Skills, use the highest.

Non-Check Check Mods & Bonuses

Most Skills also have a non-Check bonus (like Quarter Mastering) that you automatically get. Where these refer to "Rank", also add the applicable Check Mod to it to determine Bonuses.

So, for Endurance, your "Rank" is actually your Rank + your Check Mod and, for Combat Style, your bonus is equal to ( Rank + Check Mod ) / 2.

Combat Check Mods

Combat Skills behave identically to normal Skills except that their Check Mods are calculated a bit differently.

Instead of taking the highest stat, you’re going to refer to the following formulas and table below.

Attack (Atk): Dex Mod + Int Mod

Defense (Def): Dex Mod + Will Mod + Armor Def Mod + Shield Def Mod

Initiative (Init): Dex Mod + Will Mod + Armor Init Mod

Damage (Dam) [Melee & Thrown]: Weapon Damage + Str Mod

Damage (Dam) [Everything Else]: Weapon Damage

Recovery (Rec) [Melee & Bows]: Weapon Recovery + Armor Rec Mod + Str Mod * weapon size (1 for S, 2 for M, 3 for L, 4 for H), rounded down

Recovery (Rec) [Everything else]: Weapon Recovery + Armor Rec Mod

You'll also have Parry, which are both based on the weapon you used but Parry may be replaced by your shield's Parry.

Athletics Skill Suite

Base Cost 30

Stats Str/Con

This suite covers all things that require physical prowess and, to a lesser extent, pure manual dexterity.

Climbing

Base Cost 7

Stats Str

This Skill covers climbing and repelling up and down walls of various heights and textures.

Difficulty should be based on height, with about every 40 ft increasing the Difficulty by 1 dice size.

Textures should also play a major factor in not just how tough the Check is but also how many Checks need to be made for a given wall.

So, if there’s a wall, where the base is stone at a 45% slant for 20 ft, then it moves to close knit stones for another 40 ft, and then finally to a wood overhang for just 5 ft, and then to a wooden wall for 10 ft, there would be 4 Climbing Checks in this order: +d4!, +d10! (d8! for base but because it’s 40 ft, it gets bumped up 1), d10!, and +0.

Endurance

Base Cost 4

Stats Con

Through training, a character can push his body past the limits of his base physical ability. The Rank serves as DR against the damage you take while Running or Sprinting, although specifics are given in the Actions section of Chapter 1.3: Combat.

Escape Artist

Base Cost 7

Stats Dex

This allows the character to escape bonds and restrictions and is always rolled opposed, either against the Rope Use Skill of the person who tied them or against the quality of the manacles or binds the person was put in.

The character can make a straight +0 Check, not to break the bounds or get free, but to instead move his body to an advantageous position.

Jumping

Base Cost 5

Stats Str

When you need to cover a distance without touching the ground, jump.

Typically, a character can jump 16 inches vertically or 5 ft horizontally without a Check, however, if they want to go further, they’ll need to roll.

Each Boon adds 2 inches to the vertical jump and 6 inches to the horizontally. Starting at a run doubles this.

Small creatures half these numbers while Large creatures double them.

When you want to move silently, you’ll want to use this Skill. This involves being able to feel your way along and compensate for creaks.

Resistance (Poison/Disease)

Base Cost 1/5

Stats Con

Poisons, diseases, and toxins in general are a constant threat for adventures and some have found that they can build up a resistance to these troubles over time. When you take this Skill, you need to determine what specific poison, disease, or toxin you’re taking it for. When you’re exposed to that substance, for any reason, you gain this Skill’s Rank when making Checks against it.

You can take this Skill multiple times, each for different poisons, diseases, and toxins.

At character creation, this Skill has a base cost of 1 CrP instead of 5. If you want to take this Skill for a poison, disease, or toxin you’ve been exposed to during play, it drops down to 1 CrP again and the cost only increases by the Rank (instead of twice the Rank). You only get this discount once per exposure.

If you’ve bought a poison, you can expose yourself to small amounts to build up an immunity but, because you’re not at as much of a risk, the immunity builds up slower: there is no discount to the base cost but the price increases by the Rank (instead of thrice).

Sport (specific)

Base Cost 4

Stats Str/Con

This covers the actual playing of a specific sport as well as an in-depth knowledge of the game’s rules as well as famous players and techniques.

If it becomes a plot point, the GM should feel free to make this Skill apply to only certain positions, like pitching or shortstop but this a fantasy roleplaying game so I doubt that’ll happen.

Swimming

Base Cost 5

Stats Str/Con

This Skill allows a character to not drown in water.

The Difficulty of the Check is determined by the water (or other liquid, I don’t judge) you’re attempting to swim in, though simply trying to float on your back and letting the current take you gives you a +d8! to your roll, though it is risky because you could get pulled into an aquifer or get pulled over a waterfall but that’s the chance you take.

Vandalism

Base Cost 3

Stats Str

Vandalism is the art of destroying inanimate objects using non-weapon methods.

This could mean breaking down a door, bending bars, lifting gates, destroying pieces of art or a lock, or something more iconoclastic. It involves sussing out the weak points and targeting them for maximum damage.

A successful Check results in 1 Wear being applied to the object, with an additional Wear for each Boon.

Lore Skill Suite

Base Cost 30

Stats Int

This suite covers general knowledge, which is mostly academic in nature. Most of it is pretty heavy stuff but the basics are second nature or stuff that’s been picked up here and there.

Administration

Base Cost 7

Stats Int

While they’ll never inspire the awe or respect that other leaders do, people who oversee the day to day functions of an organization keep the lights on so that others can make the speeches.

Administrators can make a Check at +0 to gain a clue about an item the party might need on their adventure, or one to retroactively remember that they did buy an item back at the market for just such an occasion.

Each of these Checks they make increases the Difficulty of the next one by 1 step.

History

Base Cost 6

Stats Int

Everyone knows certain large events, but few can tie those events into an overarching narrative and understand their effects on later events.

The History Skill allows a character to not just recall events but also to link them to the present day, to certain traditions and rituals of a culture, or the behavior of certain members of a culture, giving insight on how to proceed.

Language (specific)

Base Cost 5

Stats Int/Will

A character receives this Skill for free in their native tongue with a Rank equal to their Int and it allows them to speak and be understood by others with Skill in that language. Like all Languages, this isn't increased with the Lore Skill.

Purchasing other languages can be done, however, if it a language completely unrelated to a language you already known, the Language's Rank starts at 0: otherwise use of that that Language's Rank as a starting Rank.

Checks are only made when timing is critical, such as communication in a battle, or when understanding is key, such as when debating foreign policy.

Additionally, when two speakers are in a Confrontation, the one with the higher Language Rank gains a +d4! to their roll (see Applicable Skills in Chapter 1.2: Confrontations). In case of tie, nothing happens.

The Lore Skill Suite can’t be used in place of a Language Check for tongues you don’t know unless you have the Language Skill in a related Language. Using a real-world example, someone with the Language (Swedish) Advanced Skill couldn’t use the Lore Skill Suite to understand a person with Language (French) but could use it when talking to someone with Language (Norwegian) or Language (Danish).

Law (culture)

Base Cost 5

Stats Int

This Skill covers the intricacies of the legal system, including landmark rulings and common punishments for common crimes. A character can make their case or find loopholes to exploit the legal system.

A character’s Lore Skill can only be used to substitute for their native culture but not others’. If the character does want to branch out, his mastery of Law for that new culture starts at 0, as he must learn everything from the ground up. Diving more into his own culture’s laws (buying this Advanced Skill for his culture) uses the Lore Skill as base, as per usual.

Literacy (alphabet)

Base Cost 7

Stats Int

This Skill allows a character to read and write in a language’s alphabet, giving them the power to pen books and poems, or passive aggressive notes. A character who's Trained in the Lore Skill Suite is considered to be literate in their native language's alphabet.

This Skill can be taken multiple times, each instance representing mastery of a new writing system. The scribe can then use that alphabet to pen any language they know, in the same way that we could use the Latin Alphabet to pen Arabic or the Korean Alphabet to write English.

To decipher a text, the person needs to know both the alphabet it's written in and the language it's using, which most of the time won't matter but can be used as a cheap way to do a cypher in a pinch.

Checks are only made when timing is critical, such as quickly scanning a letter, or when understanding is key, such as reading an extremely techinical work, or where basic competency is unsure, such as reading an early version of the language.

Mathematics

Base Cost 9

Stats Int/Will

While this may seem like just a Skill that allows you to be able to juggle figures and add totals and calculate the area of a circle like a huge nerd, mathematics has a more practical use: correctly guessing distances.

While you can try to eyeball how far away something is, a Mathematics Check vs +0 allows you to do rough calculations, giving you the ability to know exactly what penalty you’re going to get for using a ranged weapon or who's going to be affected by the Shape of a spell you’re going to cast.

Medicine

Base Cost 8

Stats Int

With proper treatment and nursing, a wound that would lay you out for weeks, may only keep you down for a couple of days. This Skill involves the knowledge of long-term care, when bandages should be changed, which poultices will have the greatest effect, and what minor surgeries need to be performed.

A character with this Skill can make a Check for a wounded person, adding the Severity of his largest Wound to the opposing roll.

So, if a person has a 5, 3, and 8 Severity Wound, he can make a Medicine Check vs d20+8.

Success means that the person can take 1 day off the healing time for every Wound every time it drops a Severity. They can only make this Check once per Wound, success or fail.

So, a success against a 5 Severity Wound means that it was treated properly and takes 10 days to completely heal ( 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 0 ) instead of the normal 15.

Every Boon you get reduces the number of days healed by an additional day.

Additionally, when resisting Poison, Toxin, or Diseases, you can add your Medicine Rank to each round of the Confrontation.

Monster Craft

Base Cost 8

Stats Int

This Advanced Skill is under both the Lore and Survival Skill Suite but still counts as 1 Skill, so you don’t have to buy it twice - they share their Rank. It simply represents the different ways that each Skill Suite approaches the craft.

When you first purchase this Skill, you need to choose which Skill Suite to base if off of: only purchases of that Skill Suite increase it. You cannot switch it.

Monster Craft gives you insight into the creatures that roam the Middle Realm. Simply put, each Boon gives you a (truthful) answer to a question.

Difficulty is based on how common the monster is, which is noted in the monster's entry.

Religion (cult)

Base Cost 4

Stats Int

This Skill acquaints the character with a knowledge of cults, their common Covenants and Miracles, as well as their hierarchies, means of advancement, and important members.

It also allows a character to gain a knowledge of their rituals and sacrifices, as well as the taboos of their clergy and how to appease their heavenly messengers and avenging angels.

The Religion Skill is taken for a single Covenant, but can be purchased independently for different Covenants.

A character adds the Rank of this Skill to their chance of gaining Favor when making certain sacrifices to the god of that cult.

World Craft

Base Cost 6

Stats Int

This area of study covers the topography of the Middle Realm. It’s forests and rock formations and biomes. It helps predict whether or not it’s going to rain or whether the next summer is going to be a hot one.

Predicting what’s coming in the next hour is a Check vs +0, with the Difficulty increasing by 1 dice size for each time you double the prediction window. So, detecting the weather in the next 2 hours is vs +d4!, while doing so in the next 16 hours is vs +d10!.

Streetwise Skill Suite

Base Cost 30

Stats Will/Pre

This suite covers knowledge that a character might pick on the street or in the school of hard knocks. While Lore is book smart, Streetwise is street smart.

Current Affairs

Base Cost 5

Stats Int

A character with this Skill is well informed about the day to day affairs of regional powers and men and women of importance, their dealings and alliances, as well as their hatreds. He can pick out celebrities and know how and why someone got rich and famous.

They can also use this Skill to guess the Integrity category of someone by watching how people react to them and letting them know about certain NPCs' Reputations.

This covers all sorts of deception, where you want the person to believe the opposite of the truth, or, to put it simply, when you want to lie to them.

Forgery

Base Cost 11

Stats Dex/Will

Mostly commonly used for creating fake currency, forgery is a Skill that allows a character to reproduce something effectively and fool others. This Skill acquaints the character with common forging techniques and knowledge with what possible agents will be looking for.

The forger must also have the appropriate corresponding Craft or Trade Skill, so he can’t forge coins if he doesn’t have the Metalworking Skill, though that’s within reason: a character forging an official passport with one at hand doesn’t need to have Literacy as he can just copy the letters, though he may also need to have Artistry (wood cutting) to create the heraldry on it, but someone trying to forge an official declaration from scratch does need to have the Literacy Skill in that language plus Artistry (wood cutting).

Forgery is opposed by the applicable Skill, or Forgery but only if the character looking has reason to suspect there’s something else. A lazy guard probably won’t Check your passport too closely right before lunch, but a particularly astute one who's on the lookout for a promotion will.

Gambling/Gaming

Base Cost 5

Stats Int

This Skill doesn’t give the character a gift for winning games, just that he knows the rules and knows the odds, which could in turn lead to him winning the game.

Intuition

Base Cost 5

Stats Will

The ability to understand people and get where they're coming from can help you figure out what will and won't work with them better.

It has 3 uses: to get a feel on how likely a Confrontation is, to confirm an NPC's Characteristic, and to discover an NPC's Characteristics.

To get a feel about the likelihood of a Confrontation, a Check is required with the Difficulty based on how well the person knows their target.

Boons can be used to determine what additional things might be dealbreakers or insights into why this tactic might work while Complications can result in a misread of the situation.

They can also use it to confirm their theories about what Characteristics a character has. The player needs to make a reasonable guess about the Characteristic first - so players who’re simply throwing out guesses automatically fail.

The Check is based on how much the person and the target have interacted with each other but typically start at Impossible and are lowered by 2 for each previous interaction, although the GM can further lower the Difficulty if the interaction was particularly meaningful or telling.

Finally, the GM will subtract double that Characteristic's Rank from their roll so that more powerful it is, the easier it'll be to confirm, although they may add their Deception Rank for free as well.

Boons can be spent for additional insight into that Characteristic (like learning it's general Rank) while Complications might result in the Characteristic being slightly off or leaving out a key detail.

Players don't need to exactly correct in their guesses, as long as they get the spirit. If they succeed, it's a good opportunity to just tell them what the Characteristic is directly.

A character can also use their Intuition Skill to 'read' someone: learn a Characteristic without making a guess at it.

The Check is based on how much the person and the target have interacted with each other but typically start at Impossible and are lowered by 1 for each previous interaction, although the GM can further lower the Difficulty if the interaction was particularly meaningful or telling.

Finally, the GM will subtract that Characteristic's Rank from their roll so that more powerful it is, the easier it'll be to guess, although they may add their Deception Rank for free as well.

Boons can be spent for additional insight into that Characteristic (like learning it's general Rank) while Complications might result in the Characteristic being slightly off or leaving out a key detail or being completely wrong.

Listening

Base Cost 6

Stats Int

Listening has to deal with your character’s auditory senses. This goes beyond simply hearing something: listening allows the character to know when an otherwise innocuous noise is actually something to pay attention to though it might not always be apparent why at first.

Survival Skill Suite

Base Cost 30

Stats Con/Will

This suite covers everything that a character might need to survive in the wild; Knowledge of what plants to eat and the Skills to hunt and breed animals.

Botany

Base Cost 7

Stats Int

This gives a knowledge of what the herbs used in cooking, alchemy, and medicine look like in their natural environment and it allows you to identify their common uses. It also helps find food in the wilderness.

If halving their overland travel speed, a person with Botany can make a Check every travel period: if they succeed, they can consider themselves 1 step up on the Long-Term Exhaustion table. So, if they had nothing, they could consider themselves Rationing.

If they choose to stay put, a success nets them 2 steps up.

4 or more Boons results in doubling their output, meaning they possibly found themselves enough food for the next day.

Mostly, this is used for detecting poisonous plants, which is Check vs +d4! but is a common Skill when Helping Out for both Medicine and Cooking/Baking.

Fire Building

Base Cost 5

Stats Dex

Against the cold, lonely nights out in the wild, there is only the fertility symbols you carve for yourself and the roar of the fire. This Skill allows you to build one of those things: the fire.

Under normal conditions, it takes about 30 minutes and a Check vs +0 to set up and light a fire, assuming you don’t have to go far out of your way to find the wood.

First Aid

Base Cost 8

Stats Dex/Int

This gives the character a crude understanding of battlefield medicine: making splints and staunching wounds.

A character with this Skill can make a Check for a person wounded in that last 30 minutes, adding half of the Severity of any Wounds that he's treating to the opposing roll (rounded down).

So, if a person has a 5, 3, and 8 Severity Wound, he can make a First Aid Check vs d20+8 or he can choose not to treat the 3 Severity Wound and make a vs d20!+6. Success means that the person can take 1 level of Severity off those Wounds. So, a 5 Severity Wound drops immediately to a 4 Severity Wound. You cannot go back and do a Check for a Wound that you skipped.

You have 30 minutes to perform First Aid Checks for a person, with each Wound taking 3 minutes, if applicable.

For every 2 Boons, you may increase the damage healed by 1.

Handle Animal (specific)

Base Cost 6

Stats Pre

This Skill deals with the handling and care of a specific animal, from birth to the slaughterhouse. It also deals with training and common illnesses to make sure the stock is healthy and virile.

While most Checks are handled like normal knowledge Checks or on a case-by-case basis (such as calming down your animal during a storm), using a question-and-answer style, this Skill is also used for training, with the base number of tricks you can teach an animal equal to their Int (note that carrying things and handling a bridle are two different tricks).

The animal needs to have both the basic temperament and physical ability to learn a trick (no sheep becoming pack animals and no dogs learning to pick locks). The GM may increase or decrease this for particularly smart/dumb and obedient/obstinate animals.

After a month of training, a roll is made vs +0. If successfully, the animal learned the trick. If not, another Check can be made in a month, although every failure increases the Difficulty to learn that trick by 1 dice size.

If players want to tame animals they can use the following Obstacle to do so.

Base Cost 7

Stats Will

Most of the time, you’re not going to down your quarry in one go, and so the hunt is on as your watch for subtle signs of their passing. While this Skill was originally used to hunt wildlife, it has been repurposed by wardens and bounty hunters trying to capture the most dangerous game.

The Hunting Skill can be used to track your prey, get a sense of their movements, and predict where they’re going. It can also give insight into common and uncommon natural creatures.

If you’re using it for food, hunting is a boom or bust strategy: at the end of each full day you spend hunting, roll a Check.

For each Boon you gain 2 days’ worth of food, but either way, you gain 1 damage from exhaustion and possibly more if you suffer a Complication.

Hunting is also used to harvest Pleroma from dead animals and monsters which can be sold or used to increase your spell power.

Base Cost 30

Stats Will/Pre

Combat Style (style)

Base Cost 9

Stats Con/Int

By studying the best tactics and how to fight in certain circumstances, a warrior can take advantage of his surroundings and combat styles emerge. Combat Styles can be anything but involved in parts: a situation and a metric.

This situation is when the Combat Style is most effective. For instance, it can be “in the forest”, “fighting on rooftops”, “being outnumbered”, “being in a duel”, “fighting while prone”, etc. Really anything (as long as your GM approves).

The second part of the Combat Style is the metric applied to. This can be anything from the following list: Fatigue, Initiative, Recovery, Attack, Damage, and Parry.

Both the situation and metric are selected at the time of purchase and cannot be changed.

If the situation is applicable, the character receives a +1 to that metric for every 2 Ranks he has.

So, a character with the Combat Skill (forest fighting, attack) at 5 with a +1 from Con who gets into a fight in a forest gets a +3 to attack ( ( 5 + 1 ) / 2 ).

Having a high Rank in Strategy doesn't automatically give you Combat Style bonuses under all circumstances. After the first time you get or buy this Skill, it starts it off at Rank 0, instead of the Strategy Suite's Rank.

Leadership

Base Cost 6

Stats Pre/Will

The difference between a leader and an administrator is that the latter gets stuff done and the former gets others to get stuff done but hooking into what they believe and how they view themselves.

Those with this Skill can 'distribute' their Ranks among their traveling companions, followers, and hirelings.

Each Rank 'given' to a person increases their Panic by 1 step.

These Ranks can only be 'redistributed' once a week and can't affect the person 'giving' them. In a group, only one person can 'give' out their Leadership Ranks (as you can only have 1 party captain) and they must be present, alive, and conscious for the Ranks to have an effect.

Additionally, during a Combat, Confrontation, or Challenge, whenever you could apply it, the party captain can transfer a Grit Die from one character to another up to their Leadership Rank. He can do this a number of times equal to his Leadership Rank, but he can only transfer 1 Grit Die and that Grit Die has to be used immediately. They also gain 1d4! Stress per die transferred this way.

And finally, assuming you have a willing audience, you may make a Leadership Skill Check vs +0 pointed at a Characteristic you think the person or group might have.

If you succeed the Check and they have the Characteristic you’re looking for (or close enough - you don’t have to be exact), you increase their Panic threshold by 1 step for the next d4! hours. Gaining 2 or more Boon increases it by 2 steps.

You can increase the number of Characteristics you’re aiming for by increasing the Difficulty dice size of the Check by a similar number.

Signaling

Base Cost 4

Stats Int

This is the knowledge of how to communicate over long distances, either using semaphore, smoke signals, signal lamps, or horn blowing.

Warfare

Base Cost 9

Stats Int/Will/Pre

The Warfare Skill handles conducting a war, preparing your troops and directing them in a battle.

A Check can also be made to retroactively apply an action. This has to be something that the character could have done previous to the Combat, Confrontation, or Challenge and can't retroactively affect or change other major events (i.e., no "I roll to have never come into this dungeon in the first place"). The GM will have the final say. This Check starts at +0 Difficulty but increases by 1 dice size each time they’ve been rolled. This resets when the next time they Relax.

Finally, a number of times per Confrontation, Combat, or Challenge, a person with this Skill can 'pause' what's going on and discuss, out of character, what's going on with their other party members (even if their characters aren't in the scene). This lasts up to 1 minute out of game.

Warfare also has additional benefits and uses that will be outlined in Crowns of Fire, the kingdom management and mass combat supplement for Bonfire.

From Rounds to Seconds

The Warfare Advanced Skill can help players transition from round-based combat to count-up combat as it can give them a chance to breath, which they might be used to. You might even consider giving them a starting 5 pauses + Warfare Rank and then slowly lower that number as they get adjusted.

Articulation

Base Cost 5

Stats Will/Pre

You may think that the truth is powerful enough to carry your argument, but you can be beat out by a master of deception. Articulation is the Skill of rhetoric and formulating the truth in such a way that makes it the most convincing.

The level of Difficulty to convince someone is how much evidence you can supply to back up your arguments. This is opposed by Deception or Articulation but only if someone is actively trying to deceive or convince the same person as you.

Note that the Difficulty of their Deception Check may add bonus dice to your Articulation Check.

Even if you succeed, this isn’t a permanent way of changing a person’s mind. Others may lie more effectively than you can tell the truth and that may cause them to rethink their opinion on what you tried to sell them.

Maintenance

Base Cost 6

Stats Dex/Int

The Skill of tinkers and adventurers, this gives the person a general knowledge of easy and ad hoc fixes that can be done on the fly.

A successful Check lowers the Wear on the item by 1 but you can lower the Wear by an additional point for every 2 Boons you get on your Check. The level of Difficulty in repairing something is equal to the Wear on the item, rounded up to the nearest dice size.

Weirdcraft Skill Suite

Base Cost 40

Stats Int/Will

The science of the Weird is an oft studied but little understood branch of knowledge that requires a deep knowledge of the nature of the Painted World.

With this Skill Suite, someone can identify a spell, point out needed regents and processes for ritual shapings, detect if something is touched by the Weird and what its properties are, or even detect if a spell has been cast in an area, and have a chance of finding out what it was.

This Skill Suite is also used to identify Pleroma from dead animals and monsters which can be sold or used to increase your spell power.

The final thing it does is allow you to shape cantrip versions of spells from any Tradition, regardless of Archetype. The ability to shape is inherit in all living things though it takes decades of practice, a strong family bloodline, and/or a natural gift to do what Weird-Adept Archetypes do, a form of practical "folkweird" is often passed down as part of the tools of the trade.

This doesn't, and will never, allow someone to shape the full version of a spell but it can give them a boost or an edge. The specific details should be left up to the GM and player, but they should always be appropriate to the Tradition and never be more powerful than a spell that does the same thing. Most commonly this will mean it will get used as a Skill while Helping Out or a Repurposed Skill. Regardless, it always takes 60 seconds - the Skill Rank to shape these effects.

All Advanced Weirdcraft Skills

Base Cost 12

Stats Int/Will

Unlike other Advanced Skilled, the Weirdcraft Advanced Skills pretty much work the same and so don't need their own section.

The first bonus that having an Advanced Weirdcraft Skill gives you is that it gives you further insight into a particular Weird Tradition, allowing you to detect it better. For more information on what each Tradition specializes in, head over to Chapter 1.6: The Weird but here is a small synopsis of each:

Animism This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the body, forcing it to endure beyond its normal limits and making it into a weapon.

Calling This Tradition focuses on the Realms beyond the Middle one we’re so fond of. It involves the summoning of demons, elementals, and spirits.

Charm This focuses on the manifestation of the Weird in its raw and primal form. This allows the Weird to interact with our world in a more physical way.

Glamour This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the senses, tricking the brain into seeing what the weird-adept wants you to see.

Investiture This focuses on inanimate objects and either imbuing them with power or manifesting the latent potential that is already in them.

Occultism This Tradition focuses on the hidden world and opening of the third mind: the divination of knowledge and the unseen.

Sortilege This Tradition focuses on fate, chance, and probability: more specifically, it focuses on messing with it so that things go in your favor.

Base Cost 7

Stat *

Damage This Skill allows you to kill your enemies faster and gives you a bonus to damage based on your Rank and the weapon you're using.

Crushing weapons gain a +1 per 2 Ranks (rounded up); For Slashing and piercing weapons, you start at a +d3! damage and increase it by 1 dice per 2 Ranks above that (rounded up), however slashing is capped at a +d4! and piercing is capped at a +d8!. So, if you have a Rank 5 in a slashing weapon, you're going to add +d3!+2d4! to your damage with that weapon. If you have Rank 5 with a piercing weapon, you instead add a +d3!+d8! instead.

Recovery This Skill allows you to attack quicker. You subtract 1 from the weapon's Recovery for every 2 Ranks (rounded up).
