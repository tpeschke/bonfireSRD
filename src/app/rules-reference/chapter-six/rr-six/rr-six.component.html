What is Weird?

Seems like a reasonable place to start. It is called a lot of things by different cultures: mana, magic, juju, but essentially, it’s the metaphysical power that allows people to reshape reality, which is why working with it is often called weirdshaping, or simply shaping, if you don’t want to sound pretentious.

Weird lies beneath the surface of all things, a hidden power that imbues life with those indescribable feelings. It is the reason you feel attracted to someone at first sight, are speechless when you see a beautiful view, or the reason why planets have healing properties. It is not that some things are inherently ‘magical’ or ‘non-magical’ - all things have weird that is waiting to be shaped.

Weird Traditions

Ways of shaping the Weird are split up into different “Traditions”, which with their own bent on ways the Weird can manifest. These are:

Animism

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the body, forcing it to endure beyond its normal limits and making it into a weapon.

Calling

This Tradition focuses on the Realms beyond the Middle one we’re so fond of. It involves the summoning of demons, elementals, and spirits.

Charm

This focuses on the manifestation of the Weird in its raw and primal form. This allows the Weird to interact with our world in a more physical way.

Glamour

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the senses, tricking the brain into seeing what the weird-adept wants you to see.

Investiture

This focuses on inanimate objects and either imbuing them with power or manifesting the latent potential that is already in them.

Occultism

This Tradition focuses on the hidden world and opening of the third mind: the divination of knowledge and the unseen.

Sortilege

This Tradition focuses on fate, chance, and probability: more specifically, it focuses on messing with it so that things go in your favor.

Rudiments & Spells

When you shape the weird, it manifests in the Middle Realm as a spell, which is broken up into different parts or “Rudiments”, which are split into types. More details on the specifics are found under the Acquiring Rudiments section below but here’s a brief overview:

Shape

This is the shape that the spell shows up in. This can be a circle, cone, or wall, for example.

Origin

This is where the spell comes into being: this can start as the weird-adept and emanate out, it can imminent from his touch, or it can manifest yards away from him.

Effect

This is where the spell usually gets its name and it’s what it actually does. A spell can cause Stress, remove friction, change appearances, and so on. Effects will also list the Interval, which we will talk about next, and the base Range for that spell.

Shaping a Spell

Step 1

The moment your Weird-Adept starts to shape a spell, it begins to manifest in the world. A spell manifests physically in two different ways: the first is when the Weird-Adept is gathering their spell points and the second is when the spell is actually active on the field. Both are largely informed by the type of Weird-Adept and their way of spell shaping but, they're all unpleasant to be in (this inflicts no Stress but even the blind will sense the spell and animals will try to escape it). Once a spell is active on the field, it continues to be visible - whether it’s a Circle, a Sphere, a Cone, or a Wall. The only real exception to this is Chain where you can see where it has leapt to but not necessarily who its next target is.

The first thing a Weird-Adept must do is select their Shape and Origin Rudiments, which appears on the field. You cannot start collecting SPs until that takes place. After which, you can select and exchange the other Rudiments but the Shape and Origin stays the same. The spell cannot move from where the Weird-Adept placed it relative to themselves until it is fully shaped. For Shapes with targets (Salvo, for example), the target needs to stay in the range of the spell until it is fully shaped. (So, if they move while shaping, the Shape stays in the same relative location, but, otherwise, it stays in its location).

For identifying monster spells, modify the Check by the monster's rarity difficulty.

Drain

Each of a spell’s Rudiments will have a Drain value, which added together, is how much that spell costs to cast. A spell must have at least 1 of each Rudiment type.

When you first shape a spell (on the second you receive your SP - although, if you don't have the spell Rudiments set, the GM is free to move the count along), you subtract its Drain from the number of SPs you have at that moment and the spell lasts for an amount of time equal to its Interval.

Effect & Order

Spells start with an Order of 1 (called a 1st Order spell) but Effects are also special because you can add multiple Effects (or even the same effect multiple times, thus making it more powerful) to the same spell, increasing the total Drain by the Effect’s base drain and the Order of that Effect by 1 each time you do so. If it’s not clear how an Effect would become more powerful when stacked with itself, there’s a “+Order” section for each Effect that should clear things up.

Overlapping spells with the same Effect default to the highest Order, unless the spells were shaped by different Weird-Adepts, in which case, their Orders add together. So, if you shape two Magic Circles at Orders 3 and 4, where they intersect counts as a 4th Order Magic Circle. If, however, you have a buddy help you out and shape one, where they intersect count as a 7th Order Magic Circle.

Interval

However, if you have SP left over at the end of that Interval, you can keep the spell up. This time, you only subtract the Effect's Drain * the spell's Order from your remaining Drain.

If you have at least 1 SP when the Interval comes up but don't have enough to cover the new Drain cost, you can continue to keep the spell up for 1 more Interval if you take the amount of SP you're missing * spell's Order in Stress. This is called "Weirdburn". You can only do it once per spell.

If you choose to stop the spell at the Interval, any leftover SP are lost.

You cannot stop a spell that has been shaped until it reaches its Interval unless you die, in which case, the spell automatically ends. Additionally, if you've gained SP since shaping a spell, that SP has to be put into a new spell; it cannot be used to renew a spell at its Interval that's already been shaped.

Stacking Different Effects & Interval If you're stacking multiple, different Effects, you use the shortest Interval to determine what the Interval for the spell is. If you're using two Effects from different Traditions and are shaping via Applied Folkweird, you use the lowest of any of the Ranks. Additionally, stacking adds an additional 1d4! Drain, rolled when you first shape the spell and at its Interval (which replaced the first roll). For each additional Effect you add, increase this die by 1 size.

Changing Range

It is possible to change the Range of a spell: you can either double it or reduce it to 5 ft. In either case, you need to double the total Drain of the spell, including what you have to pay at the Interval. Once a change has been modified (or not modified), it cannot be changed for the duration of the spell. Doubling the range can only be done once per spell.

Stacking Different Effects & Range

If you're stacking multiple, different Effects, you use the smallest Range to determine what the Range for the spell.

Monster Pleroma

The Weird is in everything and cunning Weird-Adepts can harass it to make themselves more power. Every monster has Weird Pleroma, parts of their body that, when sacrificed when you shape a spell or use a Weirdcraft Advanced Skill, will give you a bonus to your SP or that Check. You discover these by identifying and then harvesting them.

But even if you're not a Weird-Adept or use Weirdcraft Skills, Pleroma are worth cold, hard cash and there is always a buyer for them.

Identifying Effects

Once a spell is active, there’s two ways of identifying what the Effect is. You (or someone you know) could get affected by it or you could try to identify it. This is partly for watching the subtleties and signs in its physical manifestation but it’s also partly listening to the waves and weave of the Weird and how they bend and twist.

Mechanically, this is represented by a (typically Weirdcraft) Check vs +0 with a Failure Threshold of 2. Success means that you’re able to identify the Effect correctly. Getting any number of Complications means that you only identify the Tradition and failing the Check means that you get nothing - you have no idea what it is.

Resisting Effects

If your character gets hit with a spell, unless they specifically try to resist its effects, the spell affects them. What ‘resisting’ an effect means varies by the Effect Rudiment but can be overridden by the specific Shape Rudiment in some cases so, if there is a conflict between the two, go for what the Shape says.

Each Effect will note the type of Effect it is: Mental or Physical. Physical Effects affect the body while mental Effects affect the brain.

When you resist an Effect you’re going to explain what your character does to overcome it. For a physical effect, for example, you could either dodge out of the way of the spell or perhaps just sit there and take it like a champ. For a mental effect, you could use your Characteristics to overcome it. So, if someone is trying to charm you into harming one of your allies, you could use your Devotion to dig deep and try to overcome it (automatically getting 1 Boon for every 5 Ranks in that Devotion).

In either case, this is treated like a Skill Check (even if you’re using Characteristics) - with all that implies. The target is going to use whatever Skill best applies to their approach to resisting the spell while the Weird-Adept is going to use a d20! + The Tradition of the Effect (or the Weirdcraft Suite) + their Will Mod.

For every Boon you get, lower the Order of the spell by 1 for just yourself. Bringing the Order to 0 or less means your character isn’t affected by the spell at all. For every 2 Complications, increase the Order by 1 against your character. If the spell is beneficial, reverse this; lower the Order by every 2 Complications, raise it for every Boon.

The character has to continue to resist the effects of the spell for the entire Interval and so is considered Multitasking. That means, that if she uses a d20! on her Check to resist, she’s going to be rolling a d0 for all her other Checks. You can stop resisting at any point but then you’re immediately affected by the full force of the spell again unless you got Complications, in which case, those are still in play.

Resisting on a full d20! resets your weapon count.

Types of Resistance

Dodge represents your character leaping out of the way of the spell and is handled via the Weird-Adept’s Attack Check vs any target’s Defense Check as if it were a ranged weapon (including natural Cover but not Cover from your shield if your shield is a viable target). If hit by a spell that can be resisted this way, the target is automatically affected by the spell for the first Interval and cannot make any attempt to resist it during that time.

At the start of the next Interval, they can resist it using the Effect’s normal method.

Mental represents your character overcoming the spell through force of will and personality. This Check uses your Characteristics but is still considered a Skill Check for the sake of Boons and Tactics.

Physical represents your character overcoming the spell through use of brawn or dexterity - either by jumping out of the way of the worst parts of the spell or simply gritting and bearing it. This is just a normal Skill Check based on how the opposing character is facing the situation. Note that this is different than Dodge spells as those are resolved through Attack and Defense Checks and result in binary outcomes (you either dodge or you don’t) while physical spells are resolved through other skills and have a gradient of outcomes.

Exiting & Entering The Shape

If you exit and enter the Shape of the spell, you need to make a new Check to resist. This does ‘clear’ Complications but also Boons as well.

If you’re exiting the Shape on the second it is shaped, you don’t need to make a Check - you’re unaffected by the spell.

Interval

At the beginning of each Interval, everyone affected by the spell who wants to resist it needs to make another Check. This clears all Boons but not Complications.

Resisting Multiple Effects

If a spell has multiple Effects, the Weird-Adept will roll an opposing Check for each Effect and compare them separately to the single Check rolled by the person who is resisting to determine how many Boons and/or Complications they get. This isn’t considered Multitasking for the Weird-Adept.

Shape Rudiment Descrptions

Unless otherwise noted, walls block shapes, including walls you can see through.

Circle

Resistance As Effect Rudiment

The Circle is the most basic Shape. It extends from a point within the Origin with a Radius equal to the Range of the Effect. Anyone in the Circle is affected by the spell only as long as they stay within it. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape.

Chain

Resistance Mental to redirect or as Effect Rudiment

This is the most primal Shape a spell can come in. The weird-adept casts the spell and it chooses 1 viable, random target within the Range of the Spell. After half the spell’s Interval, it then leaps to the next. Anyone hit by the spell suffers its Effects for the entire time the spell is cast. If there is only 1 viable target, it doesn't jump until there's a second.

People may be hit multiple times, which simply counts as the spell increasing the Order of the spell for that person (so a person hit with a 2nd Order Spell twice now counts as being affected by a 4th Order version of that spell). The weird-adept may try and redirect the spell, causing it to skip over a person. This means that the target is randomly determined again, although everyone in the Shape of the spell is still an option. They take Stress equal to the number of times they’ve redirected the Chain each time they do so.

Those who succeed at mentally resisting the spell can spend Boons to try and redirect the spell. The person who resisted the spell can force a reroll to determine who the new target is once per Boon they want to spend. The Weird-Adept cannot intervene in these types of redirects.

Cone

Resistance As Effect Rudiment

The Cone starts with its tip at a point within the Origin and then extends in a 45-degree arch from it up to the spell’s Range. Anyone within the Cone is affected by the spell for as long they're in its Shape. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape.

The Cone can be rotated around its Origin, moving a facing every 3 seconds. This takes no effort or concentration from the Weird-Adept.

Personal

Resistance As Effect Rudiment

This makes the Shape of a spell just a single individual. Starting at a point within the Origin, it affects the first viable target (determined by the Effect Rudiment) and then stops. If there are multiple viable targets that could all be first, roll randomly to determine which is affected. The spell continues to affect that person after that point, wherever they move.

Salvo

Resistance Dodge or as Effect Rudiment

The Salvo is the spell chucked up to 3 * its Range from a point within the spell’s Origin. It requires a ranged attack or Mathematics Check (which is treated as an Attack Check, not a Skill Check) to hit (though both types ignore non-shield Cover) a single viable target. Anyone that is hit, suffers the full effects of the spell for the duration of the spell. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval.

If a shield wouldn't be a viable target for the spell, the defender can use its cover value.

On a miss, it follows the normal ranged miss rules.

This Shape can create additional Salvos that can hit different viable targets. In this case, your considered to be Multitasking - so you divide the dice between any/all viable targets. You create a single salvo got each target you want to hit.

Salvo & Ray Ranged Attack Skill

While they're not weapons, both of these Shapes require a roll to hit, and it is something that Weird-Adepts can get better at.

These to-hit rolls can either be a Mathematics Check or they can be a ranged attack, using the Ranged Skill Suite or the Ranged Attack (Shape Rudiment) Advanced Skill. This is Advanced Skill works just like any other.

Sphere

Resistance Dodge

The spell takes on the shape of a 2 ft diameter sphere that flies around, coming into being at a point within the spell’s Origin. While the weird-adept has complete control over the direction with only minimal effort (so it doesn’t affect what else he’s doing), it still has some limitations. The first is that it only goes 10 ft / second, no faster, no slower, and the weird-adept may only turn it 45 degrees every 3 seconds. It also travels along the ground. The Sphere can pass through anything that air can pass through.

The second is that the sphere needs to stay in the shaper's eyeline. If the eyeline is broken for most than 1 second, the sphere stops. Regaining view of the sphere will give the shaper control again. Otherwise, the distance at which the sphere is controls is unlimited.

However, those struck with the Sphere (those who don't dodge out of the way) the first time, are affected by the spell, forfeiting their ability to resist in other ways, for the rest of the spell's life. Further hits with the Sphere don't do anything else.

Ray

Resistance Dodge

The ray is a thin, straight line of magic that extends from a point within the spell’s Origin up to the spell’s Range. It manifests in a single instant, requiring a ranged attack or Mathematics Check to hit. This cannot be blocked by shields if it's a valid target. Those that it hits suffer the full Effect of the spell for the duration of the spell.

If no one was hit, the weird-adept can manifest another ray on each following second, however, this is considered snapshotting, otherwise, they need to wait 4 seconds. They can continue to do this until either a target is hit or the spell ends. Note that the Interval is always ticking; a target struck on a follow-up attempt will be affected for a shorter duration than one struck on the initial attempt.

The shaper can hit multiple viable targets with one Ray if they're in line with each other but he has to do so simultaneously. Non-viable targets, besides equipment that the viable targets are wearing, block the Ray or provide Cover: otherwise the Ray passes right through everything else. The shaper only make one Attack Check which all targets make their separate Defense Checks against.

Wall

Resistance Dodge initially but anyone passing through the Wall forfeits their right to resist the Effect for the first Interval

The wall is a 1-ft-thick line that starts at a point within the spell’s Origin and extends up to its Range, but also into the air and below the ground up to the spell's Base Range. It also doesn’t have to be a straight line, weaving, twisting and turning, however, no part of it can get within 5 ft of another part. It must also be anchored to the ground. The wall cannot be moved once placed unless it has an Aura Origin. There is no way to increase that height or thickness.

Anyone passing through the wall is considered to be within the Shape of the spell for the rest of the duration of the spell. Those that are in the wall’s way when it is shaped, get a defense roll vs a ranged attack to get out of the way.

Origin Rudiment Descriptions

Aura

Drain 8

This makes it so the Origin starts at the weird-adept (and any equipment he carries) and then extends out from them, moving as they move.

Zone

Drain 3

This makes it so the Origin starts at the weird-adept (and any equipment he carries) and then extends out from them, but stays where they initially shaped the spell.

Touch

Drain 4

This makes it so the Origin starts at whatever the weird-adept touches and then extends from there.

If the target is trying to resist being touched, the weird-adept needs to make an Unarmed attack against them: the weird-adept is considered armed. This attack cannot be Parried and any equipment is considered a viable target, forcing the defender to defend on 1 dice size lower. Additionally, if the shaper is hit with an attack, the spell automatically hits the attacker; they receive no defense roll.

This doesn’t replace the normal way to resist the Effect, however, the person touched by the Weird-Adept using this Origin who does resist makes their Check on 1 dice smaller (usually a d12!) for the entire length of the spell.

Burst

Drain 3

This makes the Origin of the spell from 0 ft up to the Base Range of the spell, although it never affects the shaper and stays where the Weird-Adept initially shaped the spell.

Shield

Drain 5

This makes the Origin of the spell from 0 ft up to the Base Range of the spell, although it never affects the shaper and stays with the Weird-Adept, moving as they move.

Near

Drain 3

This makes the Origin of the spell from the Effect's Base Range to up to double the Effect’s Base Range.

Far

Drain 6

This makes the Origin of the spell from double the Effect’s Base Range to up to triple the Base Range.

Bind Elemental Heart

Tradition Calling

Resistance None

Base Drain 3

Base Range 20 ft

Interval 30 seconds

Effect This spell either conjures an elemental heart of the shaper's choosing from its realm or binds one that has gone rogue. The choices for what you can summon are found here.

Once in this realm, the elemental heart robes itself in nearby sympathetic elements, giving a form with which it can then move. Without its dead kin around, the heart becomes inert and defenseless. So, while there are Order and Chaos Elementals, neither of those two things have a physical manifestation in the Middle Realm and so they simply lay there.

A conjured elemental with a Weird of d4! that's rolled when the spell is initial shaped. The higher the Weird, the more powerful the elemental. Stats for elementals are found in the Bonfire Bestiary.

Elementals are ‘liquid’ and can shift and change their form. Their Weird can be invested to increase their baseline stats and special abilities. How much this costs is determined by the type of heart summoned. Weird can assigned at the time of summoning but also rearranged after, though this takes 1 second per point shift

A summoned elemental heart appears in a random location within the Shape of the spell, determined by d12 for direction (12 being where the weird-adept is facing and 6 being directly behind him) and d20 for distance, and rolls a d12 initiative dice. During this time, the elemental is getting its bearings and forming itself proper.

Elementals are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change an elemental’s command after the first one given, the elemental rolls a d10! + its Weird (and the Weird of any other elementals controlled by the weird-adept) vs a d20! + the shaper's Will Check Mod in a one round Confrontation. The weird-adept can sacrifice SP to gain a bonus to their Check on a one-to-one basis, however, any SPs they sacrifice applies to any further Checks vs that Elemental going forward. If it wins, the weird-adept loses control and the elemental goes rogue, first seeking out the weird-adept to try and kill them as quickly as possible.

The weird-adept can try to cast Bind Elemental Heart again to try and regain control. If the Weird you could have summoned is greater than the rogue elemental’s Weird, you regain control... for now.

If this spell is cast in any other Shape than Circle, the elemental is forced into that shape and unless the Shape can be moved, the elemental is rooted to the spot, only able to attack people who get within their Measure of that spot.

Shapes that require a roll to hit (Salvo and Ray) and do count that as the Elemental attack and thus include the Elementals damage or special attack.

+Order Increase the summoned Weird of the Elemental by d4!.

Elementals in Challenges

Elementals are intelligent beings, even if they're unable to effectively communicate with creatures of the Middle Realm so there might be cases where they can help in a Challenge.

In this case, you can have them either Give a Boost, or add a Skill equal to half their Weird (rolling on either a d20! or d12! for how effective the Elemental can be) or just grant a free reroll in other cases (a Water Elemental might be able to catch you if you fall while climbing), however, these bonuses only apply to physical Checks.
