What is Weird?

Seems like a reasonable place to start. It is called a lot of things by different cultures: mana, magic, juju, but essentially, it’s the metaphysical power that allows people to reshape reality, which is why working with it is often called weirdshaping, or simply shaping, if you don’t want to sound pretentious.

Weird lies beneath the surface of all things, a hidden power that imbues life with those indescribable feelings. It is the reason you feel attracted to someone at first sight, are speechless when you see a beautiful view, or the reason why planets have healing properties. It is not that some things are inherently ‘magical’ or ‘non-magical’ - all things have weird that is waiting to be shaped.

Weird Traditions

Ways of shaping the Weird are split up into different “Traditions”, which with their own bent on ways the Weird can manifest. These are:

Animism

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the body, forcing it to endure beyond its normal limits and making it into a weapon.

Calling

This Tradition focuses on the Realms beyond the Middle one we’re so fond of. It involves the summoning of demons, elementals, and spirits.

Charm

This focuses on the manifestation of the Weird in its raw and primal form. This allows the Weird to interact with our world in a more physical way.

Glamour

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the senses, tricking the brain into seeing what the weird-adept wants you to see.

Investiture

This focuses on inanimate objects and either imbuing them with power or manifesting the latent potential that is already in them.

Occultism

This Tradition focuses on the hidden world and opening of the third mind: the divination of knowledge and the unseen.

Sortilege

This Tradition focuses on fate, chance, and probability: more specifically, it focuses on messing with it so that things go in your favor.

Rudiments & Spells

When you shape the weird, it manifests in the Middle Realm as a spell, which is broken up into different parts or “Rudiments”, which are split into types. More details on the specifics are found under the Acquiring Rudiments section below but here’s a brief overview:

Shape

This is the shape that the spell shows up in. This can be a circle, cone, or wall, for example.

Range

This depends on the Shape of the spell but generally it’s the reach of the spell: how long is the cone or how far can you launch a salvo shaped spell.

Origin

This is where the spell comes into being: this can start as the weird-adept and emanate out, it can imminent from his touch, or it can manifest yards away from him.

Effect

This is where the spell usually gets its name and it’s what it actually does. A spell can cause Stress, remove friction, change appearances, and so on. Effects will also list the Interval, which will talk about next, and the base Range for that spell.

Effects are also special because you can add multiple Effects (or even the same effect multiple times, thus making it more powerful) to the same spell but increasing the Drain in the same amount. If it isn’t clear how an Effect would work if Stacked with itself, there’s a Stacking section for each Effect that should clear things up.

Shaping a Spell

Each of a spell’s Rudiments will have a Drain value, which added together, is how much that spell costs to cast. A spell must have at least 1 of each Rudiment type.

When you first shape a spell (on the second you receive your SP - although, if you don't have the spell Rudiments set, the GM is free to move the count along), you subtract its Drain from the number of SPs you have at that moment and the spell lasts for an amount of time equal to its Interval.

Interval

However, if you have SP left over at the end of that Interval, you can keep the spell up. Again subtracting the Drain from the number of SP you have.

If you have at least 1 SP when the Interval comes up but don't have enough to cover the entire Drain cost, you can continue to keep the spell up for 1 more Interval if you take the amount of SP you're missing in Stress. This is called "Weirdburn". You can only do it once per spell.

If you choose to stop the spell at the Interval, any left over SP are lost.

You cannot stop a spell that has been shaped until it reaches its Interval. Additionally, if you've gained SP since shaping a spell, that SP has to be put into a new spell; it cannot be used to renew a spell at its Interval that's already been shaped.

Stacking Different Effects & Interval If you're stacking multiple, different Effects, you use the shortest Interval to determine what the Interval for the spell is.

Shape Rudiment Descrptions

Circle

Drain 2

The Circle is the most basic Shape. It extends from a point within the Origin with a Radius equal to the modified Range of the Effect. Anyone in the Circle is affected by the spell only as long as they stay within it. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape.

Chain

Drain 0

This is the most primal Shape a spell can come in. The weird-adept casts the spell and it chooses 1 viable, random target within the Range of the Spell. After half the spell’s Interval, it then leaps to the next. Anyone hit by the spell suffers its Effects for the entire time the spell is cast.

People may be hit multiple times, which simply counts as the spell Stacking on them. The weird-adept may try and redirect the spell, causing it to skip over a person. This means that the target is randomly deteremined again, although everyone in the Shape of the spell is still an option. They take Stress equal to the number of times they’ve redirected the Chain each time they do so.

Cone

Drain 3

The Cone starts with its tip at a point within the Origin and then extends in a 45-degree arch from it up to the spell’s Range. Anyone within the Cone is affected by the spell for as long they're in its Shape. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape.

The Cone can be rotated around its Origin, moving a facing every 3 seconds. This takes no effort or concentration from the Weird-Adept.

Personal

Drain 2

This makes the Shape of a spell just a single individual. Starting at a point within the Origin, it effects the first viable target (deteremined by the Effect Rudiment) and then stops. The spell continues to affect that person after that point, wherever they move.

Salvo

Drain 5

The Salvo is the spell chucked up to 3 * its unmodified Range from a point within the spell’s Origin. It requires a ranged attack to hit (though ignoring Cover) a single viable target. The shaper gains a bonus based on the Range Rudiment they're using: +0 for Personal, +1 for Half, +3 for Full, +5 for Double. Anyone that is hit, suffers the full effects of the spell for the duration of the spell. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval.

If a shield wouldn't be a viable target for the spell, the defender can use its cover value.

On a miss, it follows the normal ranged miss rules.

This Shape can be stacked with itself to fire additional Salvos that can target different targets. If targeting the same target, they target needs to make multiple defense rolls.

Sphere

Drain 5

The spell takes on the shape of a 2 ft diameter sphere that flies around, coming into being at a point within the spell’s Origin. While the weird-adept has complete control over the direction with only minimal effort (so it doesn’t affect what else he’s doing), it still has some limitations. The first is that it only goes 10 ft / second, no faster, no slower, and the weird-adept may only turn it 45 degrees every 3 seconds. It also travels alone the ground.

The second is that the sphere needs to stay in the shaper's eyeline. If the eyeline is broken for most than 1 second, the sphere stops. Regaining view of the sphere will give the shaper control again. Otherwise, the distance at which the sphere is controls is unlimited.

Ray

Drain 3

The ray is a thin, straight line of magic that extends from a point within the spell’s Origin up to the spell’s Range. This is modified by Range Rudiments. It manifests in a single instant, requiring a roll to hit. This cannot be Parried and equipment is also part of the target. Those that it hits, suffer the full Effect of the spell for the duration of the spell.

If no one was hit, the weird-adept can manifest another ray on each following second until either a target is hit or the spell ends. Note that the Interval is always ticking; a target struck on a follow-up attempt will be affected for a shorter duration than one struck on the initial attempt.

The shaper can hit multiple viable targets with one Ray if they're in line with each other but he has to do so simultaneously. Non-viable targets, besides equipment that the viable targets are wearing, block the Ray or provide Cover: otherwise the Ray passes right through everything else.

Wall

Drain 2

The wall is a 1-ft-thick line that starts at a point within the spell’s Origin and extends up to its Range, but also into the air and below the ground up to 50 ft. It also doesn’t have to be a straight line, weaving, twisting and turning, however, no part of it can get within 5 ft of another part. It must also be anchored to the ground. The wall cannot be moved once placed unless it has an Aura Origin. There is no way to increase that height or thickness.

Anyone passing through the wall is affected by the Spell for the rest of the duration of the spell. Those that are in the wall’s way when it is shaped, get a defense roll vs a ranged attack to get out of the way.

Range Rudiment Descriptions

Double

Drain 1

This makes the Range of a spell twice its Base Range.

Full

Drain 0

This makes the Range of a spell just its Base Range.

Half

Drain 1

This makes the Range of a spell half its Base Range.

Localized

Drain 3

This makes the Range of the spell 5ft, regardless of the Base Range of the Effect Rudiment.

Origin Rudiment Descriptions

Aura

Drain 5

This makes it so the Origin starts at the weird-adept (and any equipment he carries) and then extends out from them, moving as they move.

Touch

Drain 2

This makes it so the Origin starts at whatever the weird-adept touches and then extends from there. If the target is trying to resist being touched, the weird-adept needs to make an Unarmed attack against them: the weird-adept is considered armed. This attack cannot be Parried and any equipment is considered a viable target, forcing the defender to defender on 1 dice size lower. Additionally, if the shaper is hit with an attack, the spell automatically hits the attacker; they receive no defense roll.

Burst

Drain 1

This makes the Origin of the spell from 0ft up to the Base Range of the spell, although it never affects the shaper.

Near

Drain 1

This makes the Origin of the spell from the Effect's Base Range to up to double the Effect’s Base Range.

Far

Drain 3

This makes the Origin of the spell from double the Effect’s Base Range to up to triple the Base Range.

Bind Elemental Heart

Tradition Calling

Base Drain 3

Base Range 20 ft

Interval 30 seconds

Effect This spell either conjures an elemental heart from its realm or binds one that has gone rogue.

Once in this realm, the elemental heart robes itself in nearby sympathetic elements, giving a form with which it can then move. Without its dead kin around, the heart becomes inert and defenseless. So, while there are Order and Chaos Elementals, neither of those two things have a physical manifestation in the Middle Realm and so they simply lay there.

A conjured elemental with a Weird of d4!. The higher the Weird, the more powerful the elemental. Stats for elementals are found in the Bonfire Bestiary.

Elementals are ‘liquid’ and can shift and change their form. Their Weird can be invested to increase their baseline stats and special abilities. How much this costs is determined by the type of heart summoned. Weird can assigned at the time of summoning but also rearranged after, though this takes 1 second per point shift

A summoned elemental heart appears in a random location within the Shape of the spell, determined by d12 for direction (12 being where the weird-adept is facing and 6 being directly behind him) and d20 for distance, and rolls a d12 initiative dice. During this time, the elemental is getting its bearings and forming itself proper.

Elementals are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change an elemental’s command after the first one given, the elemental rolls a d8! + its Weird (and the Weird of any other elementals controlled by the weird-adept) in a one round Confrontation. If it wins, the weird-adept loses control and the elemental goes rogue, first seeking out the weird-adept to try and kill them as quickly as possible

The weird-adept can try to cast Bind Elemental Heart again to try and regain control. If the Weird you could have summoned is greater than the rogue elemental’s Weird, you regain control... for now.

If this spell is cast in any other Shape than Circle, the elemental is forced into that shape and unless the Shape can be moved, the elemental is rooted to the spot, only able to attack people who get within their Measure of that spot.

Shapes that require a roll to hit (Salvo and Ray) and do count that as the Elemental attack and thus include the Elementals damage or special attack.

Stack Increase the summoned Weird of the Elemental by d4!.

Elementals in Challenges

Elementals are intelligent beings, even if they're unable to effectively communicate with creatures of the Middle Realm so there might be cases where they can help in a Challenge.

In this case, you can have them either function as adding a Helper Skill equal to half their Weird or just grant a free reroll in other cases (a Water Elemental might be able to catch you if you fall while climbing), however either case only applies to physical Skills.

Force Bind Spirit

Tradition Calling

Base Drain 10

Base Range 50 ft

Interval A Year and a Day

Effect By sheer force of will, the Caller binds the escaped spirit to its body, forcing a horrible bastardization of life.

This affects all the dead bodies in the Shape of the spell, regardless of how long it’s been dead for. This can bring them back as a skeleton or zombie based on how much meat is left, although it can't bind a spirit whose body has completely decayed.

The body continues to deteriorate, however, subtracting 10 from the max Vitality of the Bound Spirit for every month the spell wears on.

But it’s not all bad: the bound spirit is immune to the effects of Fatigue.

Improbable Stair

Tradition Sortilege

Components Something that could be seen as a stair

Base Drain 5

Base Range 5 ft

Interval 3 Seconds

Effect Constructed by ancient wizards, the Improbable Stair was a means for the magi of ancient times to travel great distances instantly, however, since the deaths of the old masters, the secrets of how to use the properly Improbable Stair effectively have disappeared.

The Stair is accessed by seeing a stair in an unlikely place but within the Shape of the spell; a pile of crates or a sketch on a wall. It only has to suggest that a stair is there to work. The weird-adept then steps on to the ‘stair’ and onto the Stair. As the weird-adept travels up the stairs the world around them twists and distorts as they enter the liminal stair space. While on the stair, interaction of any kind with the real world is impossible.

Each interval of travel allows the weird-adept to travel d12! yards if walking, d12! miles if jogging, or d12! days’ travel if running, rolled by the GM after the weird-adept has left the stairs. The Stair forces you to keep moving up the Stair at the same rate you were at when you entered.

The direction they travel in the real world is determined by the weird-adept when they enter the Stair and cannot be changed after that as the Stair exists outside of our conception of space.

At the end of each Interval the weird-adept may choose to exit the stairs or keep going. Once they leave the Stair, the GM reveals how far they’ve traveled and the weird-adept can adjust that based on how many times they Stacked the Effect.

The Stair will not place them in an area that is already occupied by anything other than air and will find the nearest place that meets that qualification: if the weird-adept would end up inside a mountain, they instead materialize on the ground on top of it or in a cave, or if they would end up in a wall, they instead end up on a random side of it.

Choosing to travel down is possible when the weird-adept first enters the Stair but will result in them emerging in a cave system below the ground or where they left. Choosing up will result in the weird-adept exiting in the sky or in space, either falling to their death or suffocating in the void.

Others can be brought along by holding onto the weird-adept directly or vice versa. You can be brought into the Stair against your will.

If for some reason the weird-adept loses consciousness inside the stairs, everyone is immediately thrown off the Stairs having traveled 2d12! days in a random direction. The weird-adept has no control over this.

Stack You gain more control over where you end up: modify the travel distance rolled by up to 1 in either direction. Each additional Stack adds a +1 to that maximum.
