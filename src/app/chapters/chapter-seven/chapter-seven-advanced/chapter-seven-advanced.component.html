Mastery Limitations

Masteries are sometimes natural gifts that either have been around since the character’s childhood or emerge during their adventuring career, or they’re specific area that your character has trained to develop.

In any case, unless stated otherwise, you may only take a Mastery once.

ARCANE TRAINING

CrP: *

Mages, Speakers, and Wizards may increase their magical might through study and understanding in a way that other Magical Archetypes cannot. This is represented by Arcane Training which these Magical Archetypes can purchase.

When you purchase an Arcane Training, you select one of the Orders of magic. The price is 8 + 2 * (Current Ranks + number of previous Orders trained in). Each purchase increases the Rank you have in that Order. Going back and purchasing more Ranks in an Order you’re already Trained in doesn’t “shift” it around for purposes of “number of previous Orders trained in”

So, if you’re a Mage, and you train to become Rank 1 in Mysticism, which costs you 8 CrP ( 8 + 2 * ( 0 + 0 ) ), and then train to become Rank 1 in Conjuration, which costs you 10 CrP ( 8 + 2 * ( 0 + 1 ) ), going back and becoming Rank 2 in Mysticism would only cost you 12 ( 8 + 2 * ( 2 + 0 ) ).

When casting a spell of that Order, you gain a bonus of SP equal to your Rank in that Order. If you have a spell that has 1 or more Orders assigned to it, you only get to apply one Order’s training to it, so if a spell is noted as Alteration | Invocation, and you have training in both, you may only apply your Alteration training or your Invocation training.

ELEMENTAL TRAINING You can choose to train in the Elemental Order itself but you can also choose to specialize further into specific elements with one caveat:

Once you purchase a training in a particular element, you’re unable to purchase any training in that element’s opposite.

ARMOR BASIC TRAINING

CrP: *

This basic training allows a character to more effectively utilize armor of different types based on their level of familiarity with them. It’s split into 3 categories:

LIGHT (15 CrP) This covers armor that tends to not to restrict the wearer so is often favored by skill centric characters.

MEDIUM (10 CrP) This armor is a bit heavier than Light armor and offers a tradeoff between protection and mobility.

HEAVY (10 CrP) The armor in this category is durable, thick, and heavy, able to withstand blows but at the cost of being hard to move in.

You have to buy armor training in sequence, starting at Light and moving all the way to Heavy.

You can further your training in armor to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training are further training in those particular fields. They can only be taken for things that you already have Basic Training in. So, a character can’t take any masteries for any Medium Armor if he doesn’t have the Medium Armor Basic Training.

Every type will have categories you can specialize in. Each specialization in that area gives them a +1 for every Rank they obtain in it. This can't bring a negative bonus above 0

The price for increasing your Rank in a category is the Tier’s Base Cost + twice the current Rank.

You can’t buy up a Rank again until you’ve either filled out all the rest of the category or you have two categories at that Rank. This is true all the way down so your Advanced Training for a weird looking triangle (as shown below). This is true even if you have reached the maximum benefit from a category.

For example, let’s say that you want to increase your training in Scale Armor specifically, which have the stats of Def 0, Init 3, Speed 1, and Encumbrance of 8. If you want to get a Rank 5 in Encumbrance (lowering it by 4 points), you would have to also buy a Rank 4 in one category, Rank 3 in another, and Rank 2 in a final thing.

Ideally, that means that you bought Rank 4 in Init, Rank 3 in Speed, and Rank 2 in Def. Notice that that additional 2 Ranks in Speed and that Rank 1 in Def doesn’t give you anything besides allowing you to reach Rank 4 in Encumbrance.

UNARMED COMBAT MASTERIES

While you can gain Masteries in Unarmed like other weapons, because it’s not treated exactly like a weapon, you’ll have different areas in which you can become specialized in it.

AREAS Attack, Defense, Speed, Effectiveness

Effectiveness is applied to the difference between the Attack and Defense for additional benefits if the attack hits. Every purchase adds +3 times the Tier to the total. So, Tier 3 in Effectiveness gives you a +9 to your total.

Additionally, regardless of the number of Masteries in Unarmed Combat, you’re never treated as if you have a weapon.

Ranged Weapon Firing Break Down

If you’re wondering what you’re doing at any given second when firing a ranged weapon: here’s a breakdown for you.

So, if you already loading and cranked (drawing) your crossbow, it would only take 5 seconds to fire it.

MISSILE RETRIEVAL This is drawing the missile from its quiver or pouch. For firearms, that means drawing out the powder and bullets.

FUSE This is the process of lighting the fuse. This can be dropped to 1 second but increases the chance of backfiring from 1 to 5.

LOADING This is knocking the arrow or loading the bullet and powder in the barrel.

DRAW This is drawing back the bow, cranking the crossbow, or cocking your arm for the throw. This can be prepped ahead of time for the belly- and crossbows but not the composite, long, or war bows

AIMING This is actually sighting your target and lining up the shot. This can be skipped, making it so you fire faster, but you roll attack on a d12! instead of the standard d20!. This can't bring a weapon below it's minimum weapon speed.

FIRING This is pulling the trigger or loosing the arrow.

Masteries that lower speed take from Missile Retrieval, Fuse, Loading, Drawing, and Aiming evenly but they can never reduce a single category to below 1 second. So, the minimum firing speed for a Composite Bow is 5 seconds.

The key here is that often the two professionals don’t know who the other is, so an overtly familiar greeting from a strange is usually a give away they’re a master of the many trades.

The most common use of Thieves Cant is gathering information. In this case, after the confirmation they ask if you’d like to play cards.

The new thief then pays money into the pot for each card played, paying for the information he’s getting, as he asked about the other’s estates, friends, and family.

DEFENSIVE FIGHTER

CrP: 25

Between the options of retreating and attacking, this person retreats, knowing, if there's any doubt, that the safest option is always to fall back and regroup. In a Morale Conflict, this character can't be forced to take aggressive actions.

This Mastery costs 5 CrP if you have a Trait that could be defined as Defensive. You can't take this Mastery if you've taken the Aggressive Fighter Mastery.

GOAL ORIENTED

CrP: 15

Where others are directed by their goals, this person directs them. If they have a goal that relates to the Conflict, they can steal the Advantage Dice once per Conflict for free.

GOAL SYNERGY

CrP: 15

Goals working together is a beautiful thing. Increase the Stress bonus you get by 2 for each Goal that applies beyond the first. So, having two Goals apply now gives you a +10 when Stressing.

IDIOSYNCRATIC

CrP: 20

Some people don’t see the point of going against what they see as their true nature. A person with this Mastery increases the dice they get from their Flaws by 1 size.

HARDENED RESOLVE

CrP: 15

When it comes down to the wire, this character doesn’t give up. When Revisiting the Point in a Morale Conflict, this character increases the size of the dice they bring in by 1.

NEVERMIND, FORGET IT

CrP: 30

Sometimes you know you’re beaten and the only solution is to get out of there with at least some of your dignity intact. When you lose a Conflict, either by Folding or just simply backing out, you only lose half the Honor (rounded down) you otherwise would have.

RENAISSANCE MAN

CrP: 10

A true Renaissance man is a man (or woman) of many and powerful interests and they know how to use them to charm others. When you purchase this Mastery, designate a Passion; you can use it twice in a Conflict without using Revisiting the Point

SPECIAL BOND

CrP: 10*

The battlefield can forge unlikely friendships, healing old scars by inflicting new ones on other people. These characters have put racial amenity aside and have grown to respect each other.

This Mastery can only be taken by two characters who’s races don’t have a racial preference above Tolerate for each other. Their race is considered Preferred for each other, though this doesn’t extend to others of the same race.

So, an elf and an orc that have gotten over their distrust for the other won’t cause each other Stress but that elf traveling with another orc will.

Both characters must pay the Mastery cost for it to take effect. Each person subtracts the max size of their Devotion die for each other from the price. So, a human who has a d12 Devotion to an orc can purchase this Mastery for 18 CrP.

STRONG OPENER

CrP: 25

A good opening salvo can really put you ahead and this character has a sense for it. If you have Advantage on the first round of a Conflict, temporarily increase its size by 1 size for that round.
