Mastery Limitations

Masteries are sometimes natural gifts that either have been around since the character’s childhood or emerge during their adventuring career, or they’re specific area that your character has trained to develop.

In any case, unless stated otherwise, you may only take a Mastery once.

General Masteries

A NOTCH ABOVE the REST

CrP: 30

Even in a crowd of kings, this person would stick out. They often carry themselves with the perfect blend of confidence and humility. Those with this Mastery max out their Honor at 30, instead of 25, however, they gain the additional Honor Dice once they reach 25 and then again at 30, though going above 25 doesn’t increase the size (in case you were wondering).

AMBIDEXTROUS

CrP: 30

With this mastery, a character is just as proficient with one hand as with the other, which may become helpful if they end up suffering an amputation. Mostly it means that they can better fight with a weapon in each hand. Rules for this are found in Chapter 10: Combat.

ANTI-MAGICAL BLANK

CrP: 30

There’s just something about this character that refuses to give when spells are slung around her. She increases the Drain of any spell that affects her by 3. Weird-Adept Archetypes are barred from taking this Mastery.

ANTIQUARIAN

CrP: 25

You may invoke a minor Relic’s power once per session for free and are considered 1 Honor Category higher for medium Relics.

ARCANE GRIP

CrP: 25

By sheer force of will and a natural connection to the Painted World, a character only loses 1 SP per 12 seconds while not channeling.

BELOVED

CrP: 20

Some are just favored by the Divines, the teacher's pets of the celestial world. Increase your Max Favor by 1.

CHARLATAN

CrP: 40

Sometimes people have a gift for certain things and it allows to reach beyond their own training and adapt other styles into their own. This Mastery allows a character to choose a class ability from another Archetype and incorporate it into their class, however, this ability starts at first level and stays there, only “leveling up” if they invest 20 CrP into at their level up.

CONCERT CASTER

CrP: 25

This character serves as an effective conduit to help others channel the Painted World and is considered an additional person when lowering the channeling time while Concert Shaping.

EARDSTAPA

CrP: 10

Created by the rangers of Yr Alban, this is an ad hoc language of marks and symbols sometimes called “Wander Signs”, used to communicate things to other people who might go the same way.

Often carved into trees or marked on stones, they provide useful information about the area in very simple, broad terms. Below are the most common signs:

ELDRITCH ADEPT

CrP: 10

While they don’t exactly revel in it, a person can adjust to the sickening waves that result from spells. With this Mastery, this character gains 1 less Stress from weirdburn around them. Weird-Adepts may also pick up this Mastery as well.

EXTRA SPECIAL MIX

CrP: 25

Playing with guns and gunpowder is dangerous, but a little tinker may help you learn how to refine your powders so that your barker is way worse than your bite. With this Mastery, use the larger of the two damage dice to determine Flinch. For firelance, always use the highest rolled of the two damage dice.

LINKED SOUL

CrP: 25

This character has an exceptionally special link with the lead weird-adept while concert shaping where they feel like one soul instead of two touching. Their channeling dice isn’t reduced one size if they’re directly touching the lead weird-adept.

HEIRLOOM

CrP: 35

This character has received a special gift from their family or their community: a Minor Relic. This Minor Relic needs to either be noted in the character's backstory as receiving one of these is a pretty big deal.

Servant characters receive a discount of 15 CrP and those with at least one Archetype with Inheritance receive a 10 CrP discount for this Mastery. Servant and Inheritance does stack with each other but two Inheritances don't. It can be picked up after Character Creation but only at double the price and there needs to be reason (such as a family member dying).

HEROIC/VILLAIN SPIRIT

CrP: 20

When the chips are down, this character comes in swinging. Once per session, when they redeem an Honor Die, they can receive its full value automatically.

TEACHER

CrP: 20

A great teacher can be worth their weight in gold: Any time an ally levels up, you and your companion may choose to train an extra day, if you do your ally may take a 5 CrP discount on any one Skill or Mastery you currently have. You must have a higher degree of mastery than your ally in order for them to receive the discount.

THAUMATURGE

CrP: 20

This character finds that certain Miracles are just easier to invoke than others. When you purchase this Mastery, select a specific Miracle. Increase your Honor Dice one size when invoking that specific Miracle. It still needs to follow the normal restrictions (so be sure to check with the GM to make sure the Miracle falls within your Covenant).

Combat Masteries

ACUTE ALERTNESS

CrP: 25

This a character can sometimes start acting before they even consciously know that something is wrong, either through a natural survival instinct or years of training. This Mastery lowers the Initiative Die of the character by 1 size.

ATTACK on the RUN

CrP: 25

Those with this Mastery have a special gift when it comes to aiming and moving. Instead of gaining a lowering their Attack Dice 2 per Movement Category when using ranged weapons, they lower it by 1 per Movement Category.

BLIND FIGHTING

CrP: 30

By relying on other senses, a character can somewhat make up for the loss of their sight. They increase their attack and defense die by 1 size when in dim or dark lightning conditions.

BORN GUNNER

CrP: 5

For some people, there is nothing sweeter than the smell of blackpowder and the horribly loud bang? A symphony. This Mastery lowers the delay suffered by the person from firearms going off by 1 second. This can be purchased multiple times but the cost doubles each time (5, 10, 20, etc.).

Being around firearms tends to make you less susceptible to the pure terror of firearms and so the cost of this master is discounted by 5 for every time you flinch because of a firearm going off.

COVER CHAMPION

CrP: 25

Increase your Cover dice by 1 size or +10% if it's flat cover.

DASHER

CrP: 15

One might describe this character as having a certain spring in their step and they use it to get up and going quicker than those around them. A person with this Mastery can increase their speed category by 2, allowing them to move from a Walk to a Run in a single second, for example.

DEADEYE

CrP: 25

You can see the whites of your enemy’s eyes; you can also get a good sense of where their vital organs are. When at -4 range or lower, add a d4! to your damage.

DEADLY FLANKER

CrP: 30

You’ve trained yourself to seize openings when they flash by, jumping on every lapse in your opponent’s judgement. When flanking from the side, you lower your target’s defense die by 1, including the dice of those using shields (though they still get a chance to Parry). This doesn’t, however, give you any benefit for attacking from directly behind.

DECEITFUL MEASURE

CrP: 20

Carefully positioning and moving offline can give you the edge to strike first or keep your opponent at bay. This Mastery increases the Measure of any weapon you wield by a 1/2 foot for determining who hits first but not for whether a person can Engage or get In Your Guard.

MARATHONER

CrP: 15

Through training and breathing techniques, this character can fight and sprint longer than others. They receive a -1 to their Encumbrance.

You can buy this Mastery multiple times, but the price increases by 2 each time. So, the second time, you gain a -2 for 17 CrP, the third -3 for 19, and so on.

OPPORTUNISTIC FIGHTER (ATTACK)

CrP: 30

By watching for openings and waiting to strike, a character can take advantage of any opening. This doubles the range for Near Perfect Attacks.

OPPORTUNISTIC FIGHTER (CQC)

CrP: 30

By watching for openings and waiting to strike, a character can take advantage of any opening and slip instead their guard. On a Near Perfect Attack or Defense, this character can close in or retreat one range position.

OPPORTUNISTIC FIGHTER (DEFENSE)

CrP: 30

By watching for openings and waiting to strike, a character can take advantage of any opening. This doubles the range for Near Perfect Defenses.

FLEET FOOTED

CrP: 25

A fleet-footed character tends to win foot races as he just goes flat out faster than those around him. His Running speed is increased by 2.5 feet / second and his Sprinting speed is increased by 5 feet / second.

HEALTHY

CrP: 20

Nothing can stop this juggernaut, especially not weapons. This Mastery gives the character a one time, but permanent +1d8 to their Vitality. This dice is affected by the Min Vitality of that character's Con.

IMPROVED DEFENSE

CrP: 25

Through muscle training or by simply having a talent for placing their shield in the right place at the right time, the character is able to move effectively parry blows that would otherwise land. Each purchase of this Mastery allows the character to increase their defense dice by 1 size verses one attacker up to their standard defense die. Each additional purchase doubles the cost of this Mastery.

IRON SIDES

CrP: 35

This is a nickname often given to these characters. Whether through ritual scarification or just simply having thick skin, this character has a natural +1 DR.

QUICK HEALING

CrP: 15

Some people just bounce back from wounds that would have put others down for months. With this Mastery, a character heals 1 day quicker for each level of wound severity. So, a 5 Severity Wound would heal in 10 days ( 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 0) instead of 15 ( 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1).

SHARPSHOOTER

CrP: 35

Careful aim can get you so far; sometimes, some people are just born with an eye for distance. The penalties for long-ranged shots are lowered from 16 to 12, and from 32 to 24.

SHIELD BREAKER

CrP: 25

With an innate understanding on resonance and timing, this character can target the weak spot of a shield, causing it to break more easily. This character rerolls the Shield and Weapon Breakage Threshold by 5 when dealing out Shield Damage.

This bonus does apply to the Strike the Weapon Combat Tactic, however, weapons with no Shield Damage don’t gain any benefit from this Mastery.

SURE FOOTED

CrP: 20

A solid stance or a dense physique, this character is harder to move. This Mastery increase your character’s Vitality Size Modifier for Knock-Backs by 5 and gives you a +5 to defense against Pushes and Throws.

UNYIELDING

CrP: 30

Like a warrior of old, this guy could take a spear right through the gut and keep fighting. For Trauma checks, this character rolls a 1d12, instead of a d20.

WINDAGE

CrP: 20

By compensating for wind, range, and other factors, an archer can take some control over a bad situation. This Mastery allows you to roll 2d4s on a ranged missed and keep whichever one you want.

Skill Masteries

DANGER SENSE

CrP: 20

There are people who have an ability to know when things are up and guess at how difficult something is going to be. Even if they can’t put a finger on, they can get a sense of the danger (Get it? That’s the name of the Mastery).

Those with this Mastery get to know how many Obstacles are in play during a Skill Challenge; when they’re added and when they’re removed. This doesn’t tell them what they are, why type, or how difficult, but it does give them a good sense of how complex the situation is.

DILIGENT RESEARCHER

CrP: 10

Those that have trained themselves to study, listen, and watch, can make more out of resources as their fingers than others. When using an Asset, those with this Mastery half the Time Investment.

EASY OPERATOR

CrP: 15

There’s no need to worry when your character is around because they know what they’re doing. Gain a point of Relaxation for every point of Stress you buy down when you make a Skill Check.

GRACE UNDER PRESSURE

CrP: 15

Like turning coal into diamonds, some people can turn stress into grace. When making a Skill Check, if you don’t buy down any of the Stress you take, you gain 3 Honor.

GROUNDED MIND

CrP: 30

Hobbies keep you grounded and can keep you from snapping annoying people’s necks when the pressure is on. When recovering Stress through hobbies, this Mastery increases the amount they recover by 1.

KNACK

CrP: 10

Sometimes people have a gift for certain things. When buying this Mastery, the character selects a Skill Suite. They get one free reroll per session for this Skill Suite or any of its Advanced Skills. This can be for a Skill Check or it can be for any Leverage you brought in because of that Skill.

They can buy Knack multiple times, selecting the same or different skill, however each purchase costs 10 CrP more each time.

MULTITASKER

CrP: 25

Some people can keep their minds in two places. When Multitasking, those with this Mastery gain an extra d4! to one of their Skill Checks.

OLD FRIEND

CrP: 15*

Your character didn’t start in a vacuum and he’s got resources he can draw on when he needs a helping hand. When you purchase this Mastery, you gain an Asset; it can actually be a friend but it can also be a membership to a club, a parent with a library, or whatever else you can think of. The Time Investment starts at 2 Hours and their Rank in a Skill of your choosing starts at 5.

Players subtract their Charisma from the price and don’t have to redeem it right away, however, once redeemed (and defined), however, it can’t be changed.

This Mastery can be bought multiple times, each time gaining a new Asset they can use, but each purchase adds 5 to the price.

ON YOUR TOES

CrP: 20

If you think quickly and stay on your toes, you can get away with what others can’t. One you purchase this Mastery, choose an Advanced Skill. Once per session, when using that Skill in connection with one of the Strategies, you forgo rolling or gaining a Stress Die.

POLYGLOT

CrP: 15

This character has the gift of gab and can pick up on other languages quicker than most, learning the nuances of phrases and meanings. Any language that the polyglot gains Rank 4 or above in is considered a native tongue for her.

PRODIGY

CrP: *

Some characters have just a flair or talent for a certain skill set that makes learning the subject a breeze. Characters with this Mastery get a discount of 3 CrP for 1 Advanced Skill, chosen at the time they purchase this Mastery.

The Skill you choose also determines the price. Take the unmodified cost of that Skill and multiply it by 2. You can take this Mastery multiple times but each time increases the price by the unmodified cost of the Skill, however, you can't pick the same Skill twice.

REAL HELPER

CrP: 15

When times get tough, sometimes the tough get helping. Those with this Mastery give others an additional +3 when someone else uses Preparation that they made. This is in addition to the other benefits of Preparation.

SHORT SLEEPER

CrP: 35

Some weirdos don’t need to sleep as much as the rest of us. What they do with that time will forever remain a mystery but we can assume it’s nothing good. That’s people with this Mastery, who half each threshold when it comes to Long-Term Exhaustion from sleep.

SURVIVAL TRAINING

CrP: 20

While others suffer from lack of sleep or water, this character keeps going. When you buy this Mastery you must select Food, or Water. For Long-Term Exhaustion, you half each threshold, allowing you to survive (and indulge) yourself on less.

So, if you selected Food, you can Indulge on 2.5 lbs of food, go on Maintenance on 1.5 lbs, and suffer from Deprivation under .5 lb.

You can buy this Mastery twice, the first for the base price, the second time for 35 CrP. The second time you purchase it, you get the other option so you can’t pick the same thing the second time, sorry.

TEAMWORK TRAINING

CrP: 15

While helping another person in a Skill Challenge by using the Helper Skill, only one of you takes the Stress Die.

THIEVES CANT

CrP: 35

Much like Eardstapa, Thieves Cant is an ad hoc language used for members of the many trades, but instead of marks, they use clothing and turns of phrase to communicate their intentions to others. Thieves Cant gives the ability to say things without saying them.

Thieves Cant is based on call and response, with confirmation and steps. The examples below are taken from the I Duchi Selvaggi version of the Cant, which is the most common and from which other Cants draw their lineage from.

APPROACH & CONFIRMATION Now, because it’s entirely possible that someone wears two necklaces or two rings on their right index finger, the agent has to do an approach. This is usually a code phrase disguised as an innocuous sentence followed by an innocuous reply.

The key here is that often the two professionals don’t know who the other is, so an overtly familiar greeting from a strange is usually a giveaway they’re a master of the many trades.

The most common use of Thieves Cant is gathering information. In this case, after the confirmation they ask if you’d like to play cards.

The new thief then pays money into the pot for each card played, paying for the information he’s getting, as he asked about the other’s estates, friends, and family.

Hearts is safe, meaning he’s a friend and can be trusted, Diamond means you asked about a good mark, Clubs is heavily guard, and Spades means the person in question is looking for work.

The dealer may also talk about people, places, or things; the card he deals when he brings them up applying to them.

Here’s some common Cant phrases:

"You settled down yet?"
Are you working with a guild or are you solo?
"How's your mother's health?"
How difficult is the job?
"I think that tower's at least 14 spans high"
You'll give me 14 sc for the job
"No, you're crazy, I'm sure it's only 10"
No, I won't. You'll get 10
"Aren't you a sight for sore eyes"
Hide me
"Haven't seen you in a while"
Lay low

This should be roleplayed out, with the player with Thieves Cant getting a Wisdom check to clarify anything that didn’t quite come through.

TOOL ADEPT

CrP: 15

When applying equipment to a Skill Check, you increase the dice it gives you by 1 size. If you're using equipment in a Conflict, you increase its Leverage dice size by 1 as well.

Social Masteries

AGGRESSIVE FIGHTER

CrP: 25

Between the options of retreating and attacking, this person attacks, knowing, if there's any doubt, any hesitation on their part could make it worse. In a Morale Conflict, this character can't be forced to take defensive actions.

This Mastery costs 5 CrP if you have a Trait that could be defined as Aggressive. You can't take this Mastery if you've taken the Defensive Fighter Mastery.

ANIMAL MAGNETISM

CrP: 15

Some people have that inner force that comes with total confidence, allowing them to quickly put others on the back foot. In a Conflict, if you have higher Honor than your opponent, you automatically gain a point.

CRONY

CrP: 15

Some people don't like the spotlight and are happy to help others. During a Conflict, if you rolled the least amount of dice in a round and your side still won the Appeal, you gain 1 Honor.

DEFENSIVE FIGHTER

CrP: 25

Between the options of retreating and attacking, this person retreats, knowing, if there's any doubt, that the safest option is always to fall back and regroup. In a Morale Conflict, this character can't be forced to take aggressive actions.

This Mastery costs 5 CrP if you have a Trait that could be defined as Defensive. You can't take this Mastery if you've taken the Aggressive Fighter Mastery.

GOAL ORIENTED

CrP: 15

Where others are directed by their goals, this person directs them. If they have a Goal that relates to the Conflict, you gain 1 point for each Goal.

GOAL SYNERGY

CrP: 15

Goals working together is a beautiful thing and makes you more sure of your path. Reduce the Repercussion dice by 1 size per Goal that applies to the Conflict. If it would reduce it below a d3!, you’re automatically considered to have rolled a 1 on your Repercussions though this effect doesn’t stack.

IDIOSYNCRATIC

CrP: 20

Some people don’t see the point of going against what they see as their true nature. A person with this Mastery gains an automatic +5 to their roll whenever they make an Appeal with a Flaw.

HARDENED RESOLVE

CrP: 15

When it comes down to the wire, this character doesn’t give up. Increase your Stress Threshold by 5.

NEVER MIND, FORGET IT

CrP: 30

Sometimes you know you’re beaten and the only solution is to get out of there with at least some of your dignity intact. When you lose a Conflict, either by Folding or just simply backing out, you only lose half the Honor (rounded down) or gain half the Stress you otherwise would have.

RENAISSANCE MAN

CrP: 10

A true Renaissance man is a man (or woman) of many and powerful interests and they know how to use them to charm others. When you purchase this Mastery, designate a Passion; you can use it twice in a Conflict.

SPECIAL BOND

CrP: 10*

The battlefield can forge unlikely friendships, healing old scars by inflicting new ones on other people. These characters have put racial amenity aside and have grown to respect each other.

This Mastery can only be taken by two characters whose races don’t have a racial preference above Tolerate for each other. Their race is considered Preferred for each other, though this doesn’t extend to others of the same race.

So, an elf and an orc that have gotten over their distrust for the other won’t cause each other Stress but that elf traveling with another orc will.

Both characters must pay the Mastery cost for it to take effect. Each person subtracts the max size of their Devotion die for each other from the price. So, a human who has a d12 Devotion to an orc can purchase this Mastery for 18 CrP.

STRONG OPENER

CrP: 25

A good opening salvo can really put you ahead and this character has a sense for it. If you beat (but not tie) your opponent in the first round, you gain 2 points.
