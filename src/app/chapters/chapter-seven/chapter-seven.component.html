Mastery Limitations

Masteries are sometimes natural gifts that either have been around since the character’s childhood or emerge during their adventuring career, or they’re specific area that your character has trained to develop.

In any case, unless stated otherwise, you may only take a Mastery once.

ACUTE ALERTNESS

CrP: 25

This a character can sometimes start acting before they even consciously know that something is wrong, either through a natural survival instinct or years of training. This Mastery lowers the Initiative Die of the character by 1 size.

ANTI-MAGICAL BLANK

CrP: 60

There’s just something about this character that refuses to give when spells are slung around her. Whenever she makes a saving throw, she adds twice her level to the Ability Check. Magical Archetypes are barred from taking this Mastery.

ARMOR BASIC TRAINING

CrP: *

This basic training allows a character to more effectively utilize armor of different types based on their level of familiarity with them. It’s split into 3 categories:

LIGHT (15 CrP) This covers armor that tends to not to restrict the wearer so is often favored by skill centric characters.

MEDIUM (10 CrP) This armor is a bit heavier than Light armor and offers a tradeoff between protection and mobility.

HEAVY (10 CrP) The armor in this category is durable, thick, and heavy, able to withstand blows but at the cost of being hard to move in.

You have to buy armor training in sequence, starting at Light and moving all the way to Heavy.

You can always try and use armor without the baseline knowledge of how to properly wear and don it, but the Defense penalty and Encumbrance are both doubled if that’s the case.

You can further your training in armor to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training are further training in those particular fields. They are a refining of the basics that allow your character to take their knowledge to new heights. They can only be taken for items that you already have training in. So, a character can’t take any masteries for any Medium Armor if he doesn’t have the Medium Armor Training. Every category will have areas that you can specialize in. Each specialization in that area gives them a +1 for every rank they obtain. This can't bring a negative bonus above 0.

The base price for a rank is based on the Tier your specializing in times the rank you’re obtaining. So, to get a +3 in something at Tier 1 (8 CrP), it’s going to cost you 24 CrP (8 x 3), with the following caveat:

You have to also purchase all the ranks below that for every area you can specialize in.

For example, if I wanted to gain a +2 in Defense for Kite Shields, I’d have to buy the +1 for Defense, Encumbrance, Parry, and Breakage, before buying it. Meaning that that +2 actually costs you 98 CrP.

Tiers don't stack with each other: you gain all the bonuses from a single Tier of your choice. If you have a +1 Attack and Damage in all weapons (Tier 5) and a +1 to Defence and -1 to Speed in Polearms (Tier 2), while wielding a pike, you either gain the +1 Attack and Damage with it or +1 Defense and -1 Speed, but not +1 Attack, Damage, Defense, and -1 Speed.

SHIELDS

AREAS Defense, Parry, FEncumbrance, Breakage

Breakage increases each Shield Breakage Threshold by 2 points for every rank obtained. So, wielding a shield with 2 ranks in this area of Mastery means that your shield can take 19 points of damage before saving vs d20!+10, 24 before saving vs d20!, and 29 before saving vs d20!-10. This mastery doesn't effect weapon breakage.

AREAS Attack, Damage, Defense, Speed

BELOVED

CrP: 20

Some are just favored by the Divines, the teacher's pets of the celestial world. Increase your Max Favor by 1.

BLIND FIGHTING

CrP: 30

By relying on other senses, a character can somewhat make up for the loss of their sight. They increase their attack and defense die by 1 size when in dim or dark lightning conditions.

DASHER

CrP: 15

One might describe this character as having a certain spring in their step and they use it to get up and going quicker than those around them. A person with this Mastery can increase their speed category by 2, allowing them to move from a Walk to a Run in a single second, for example.

DEADLY FLANKER

CrP: 30

You’ve trained yourself to seize openings when they flash by, jumping on every lapse in your opponent’s judgement. When flanking from the side, you lower your target’s defense die by 1, including the dice of those using shields (though they still get a chance to Parry). This doesn’t, however, give you any benefit for attacking from directly behind.

DECEITFUL MEASURE

CrP: 20

Carefully positioning and moving offline can give you the edge to strike first or keep your opponent at bay. This Mastery increases the Measure of any weapon you wield by a 1/2 foot for determining who hits first but not for whether a person can Engage or get In Your Guard.

ELDRITCH ADEPT

CrP: 30

While they don’t exactly revel in it, a person can adjust to the sickening waves that result from spells. With this Mastery, this character doesn’t gain Stress from other’s casting spells around them.

People who're constantly around magic quickly develop this trait. This costs 1 CrP less for everytime they suffer from magical fallout from a spell being cast.

MARATHONER

CrP: 15

Through training and breathing techniques, this character can fight and sprint longer than others. They receive a -4 to their Encumbrance.

FLEET FOOTED

CrP: 25

A fleet-footed character tends to win foot races as he just goes flat out faster than those around him. His Running speed is increased by 2.5 feet / second and his Sprinting speed is increased by 5 feet / second.

GRACE UNDER PRESSURE

CrP: 20

This character doesn't sweat anything in a competition. When making an opposed Skill Check, they increase their dice by 1 size.

GROUNDED MIND

CrP: 30

Hobbies keep you grounded and can keep you from snapping annoying people’s necks when the pressure is on. When recovering Stress through hobbies, this Mastery character doubles the number dice she rolls.

HEALTHY

CrP: 20

Nothing can stop this juggernaut, especially not weapons. This Mastery gives the character a one time, but permanent +1d8 to their Vitality. This dice is affected by the Min Vitality of that character's Con.

IMPROVED DEFENSE

CrP: 25

Through muscle training or by simply having a talent for placing their shield in the right place at the right time, the character is able to move effectively parry blows that would otherwise land. Each purchase of this Mastery allows the character to increase their defense dice by 1 size verses one attacker up to their standard defense die. Each additional purchase doubles the cost of this Mastery.

IRON SIDES

CrP: 35

This is a nickname often given to these characters. Whether through ritual scarification or just simply having thick skin, this character has a natural +1 DR.

KNACK

CrP: 10

Sometimes people have a gift for certain things. When buying this Mastery, the character selects an Advanced Skill. They get one free reroll per session for this skill.

They can buy Knack multiple times, selecting the same or different skill, however each purchase costs 10 CrP more each time.

POLYGLOT

CrP: 15

This character has the gift of gab and can pick up on other languages quicker than most, learning the nuances of phrases and meanings. Any language that the polyglot gains Rank 4 or above in is considered a native tongue for her.

QUICK HEALING

CrP: 15

Some people just bounce back from wounds that would have put others down for months. With this Mastery, a character heals 1 day quicker for each level of wound severity. So, a 5 Severity Wound would heal in 10 days ( 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 0) instead of 15 ( 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1).

SHIELD BASIC TRAINING

CrP: 10

This teaches a character the basic understanding of how to properly use and employ shields in combat. This training covers all types of shields.

You can always try to use a shield without the training, however, you gain a -4 to your defense when doing so.

You can further your training in shields to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

THAUMATURGE

CrP: 20

This character finds that certain Miracles are just easier to invoke than others. When you purchase this Mastery, select a Miracle. Decrease the Invocation Die of that Miracle by 1 dice size, down to a d2, once per session.

APPROACH & CONFIRMATION Now, because it’s entirely possible that someone wears two necklaces or two rings on their right index finger, the agent has to do an approach. This is usually a code phrase disguised as an innocuous sentence followed by an innocuous reply.

The key here is that often the two professionals don’t know who the other is, so an overtly familiar greeting from a strange is usually a give away they’re a master of the many trades.

The most common use of Thieves Cant is gathering information. In this case, after the confirmation they ask if you’d like to play cards.

The new thief then pays money into the pot for each card played, paying for the information he’s getting, as he asked about the other’s estates, friends, and family.

Hearts is safe, meaning he’s a friend and can be trusted, Diamond means you asked about a good mark, Clubs is heavily guard, and Spades means the person in question is looking for work.

The dealer may also talk about people, places, or things; the card he deals when he brings them up applying to them.

WEAPON BASIC TRAINING

CrP: 10

By gaining the basic understanding of how a weapon is wielded, used and taken care of, you not only expand its longevity, but also your longevity on the battlefield.

Weapon training is taken for individual weapons, however its benefits extend to weapons of that share similar features.

A person completely untrained in weapons both Attacks and Defends with that weapon using a d12!.

However, one trained uses a d20! Attack and Defense (depending on whether or not they use a shield).

Additionally, this is extended to any weapon that shares similar qualities as defined by the Weapon Tiers, up to Tier 4.

For example, a character with training only in the Dusack extends that training to all melee weapons.

A character with training in both a Melee and Ranged weapons, gains that training to all weapons.

So, why would you take training in multiple weapons once you have one melee and one ranged? Because this training allows you to further master that weapon, increasing its usefulness.

You can further your training in weapons to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.
