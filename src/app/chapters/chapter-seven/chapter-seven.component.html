Mastery Limitations

Masteries are sometimes natural gifts that either have been around since the character’s childhood or emerge during their adventuring career, or they’re specific area that your character has trained to develop.

In any case, unless stated otherwise, you may only take a Mastery once.

Even in a crowd of kings, this person would stick out. They often carry themselves with the perfect blend of confidence and humility. Those with this Mastery max out their Honor at 30, instead of 25, however, they gain the additional Honor Dice once they reach 25 and then again at 30, though going above 25 doesn’t increase the size (in case you were wondering).

While they don’t exactly revel in it, a person can adjust to the sickening waves that result from spells. With this Mastery, this character doesn’t gain Stress from other’s casting spells around them.

This character finds that certain Miracles are just easier to invoke than others. When you purchase this Mastery, select a Miracle. Decrease the Invocation Die of that Miracle by 1 dice size, down to a d2, once per session.

COMBAT MASTERIES

ACUTE ALERTNESS

CrP: 25

This a character can sometimes start acting before they even consciously know that something is wrong, either through a natural survival instinct or years of training. This Mastery lowers the Initiative Die of the character by 1 size.

ARMOR BASIC TRAINING

CrP: *

This basic training allows a character to more effectively utilize armor of different types based on their level of familiarity with them. It’s split into 3 categories:

LIGHT (15 CrP) This covers armor that tends to not to restrict the wearer so is often favored by skill centric characters.

MEDIUM (10 CrP) This armor is a bit heavier than Light armor and offers a tradeoff between protection and mobility.

HEAVY (10 CrP) The armor in this category is durable, thick, and heavy, able to withstand blows but at the cost of being hard to move in.

You have to buy armor training in sequence, starting at Light and moving all the way to Heavy.

You can always try and use armor without the baseline knowledge of how to properly wear and don it, but the Defense penalty and Encumbrance are both doubled if that’s the case.

You can further your training in armor to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training

Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training are further training in those particular fields. They can only be taken for things that you already have Basic Training in. So, a character can’t take any masteries for any Medium Armor if he doesn’t have the Medium Armor Basic Training.

Every type will have categories you can specialize in. Each specialization in that area gives them a +1 for every Rank they obtain in it. This can't bring a negative bonus above 0

The price for increasing your Rank in a category is the Tier’s Base Cost + twice the current Rank.

You can’t buy up a Rank again until you’ve either filled out all the rest of the category or you have two categories at that Rank. This is true all the way down so your Advanced Training for a weird looking triangle (as shown below). This is true even if you have reached the maximum benefit from a category.

For example, let’s say that you want to increase your training in Scale Armor specifically, which have the stats of Def 0, Init 3, Recovery 1, and Encumbrance of 8. If you want to get a Rank 5 in Encumbrance (lowering it by 4 points), you would have to also buy a Rank 4 in one category, Rank 3 in another, and Rank 2 in a final thing.

Ideally, that means that you bought Rank 4 in Init, Rank 3 in Recovery, and Rank 2 in Def. Notice that that additional 2 Ranks in Recovery and that Rank 1 in Def doesn’t give you anything besides allowing you to reach Rank 4 in Encumbrance.

Tiers don't stack with each other: you gain all the bonuses from a single Tier of your choice. If you have a +1 Attack and Damage in all weapons (Tier 5) and a +1 to Parry and -1 to Recovery in Polearms (Tier 2), while wielding a pike, you either gain the +1 Attack and Damage with it or +1 Parry and -1 Recovery, but not +1 Attack, Damage, Parry, and -1 Recovery.

ARMOR

AREAS Defense, Recovery Modifier, Encumbrance, Initiative

Encumbrance lowers that armor's Encumbrance by 2 for every plus you have in it.

SHIELDS

AREAS Defense, Parry, Encumbrance, Breakage

Encumbrance lowers that shield's Encumbrance by 2 for every plus you have in it.

Breakage increases each Shield Breakage Threshold by 2 points for every rank obtained. So, wielding a shield with 2 ranks in this area of Mastery means that your shield can take 19 points of damage before saving vs d20!+10, 24 before saving vs d20!, and 29 before saving vs d20!-10. This mastery doesn't effect weapon breakage.

WEAPONS

AREAS Attack, Damage, Parry, Recovery

BLIND FIGHTING

CrP: 30

By relying on other senses, a character can somewhat make up for the loss of their sight. They increase their attack and defense die by 1 size when in dim or dark lightning conditions.

DASHER

CrP: 15

One might describe this character as having a certain spring in their step and they use it to get up and going quicker than those around them. A person with this Mastery can increase their speed category by 2, allowing them to move from a Walk to a Run in a single second, for example.

DEADEYE

CrP: 25

You can see the whites of your enemy’s eyes, you can also get a good sense of where their vital organs are. When at -4 range or lower, add a d4! to your damage.

DEADLY FLANKER

CrP: 30

You’ve trained yourself to seize openings when they flash by, jumping on every lapse in your opponent’s judgement. When flanking from the side, you lower your target’s defense die by 1, including the dice of those using shields (though they still get a chance to Parry). This doesn’t, however, give you any benefit for attacking from directly behind.

DECEITFUL MEASURE

CrP: 20

Carefully positioning and moving offline can give you the edge to strike first or keep your opponent at bay. This Mastery increases the Measure of any weapon you wield by a 1/2 foot for determining who hits first but not for whether a person can Engage or get In Your Guard.

MARATHONER

CrP: 15

Through training and breathing techniques, this character can fight and sprint longer than others. They receive a -1 to their Encumbrance.

You can buy this Mastery multiple times, but the price increases by 2 each time. So, the second time, you gain a -2 for 17 CrP, the third -3 for 19, and so on.

FLEET FOOTED

CrP: 25

A fleet-footed character tends to win foot races as he just goes flat out faster than those around him. His Running speed is increased by 2.5 feet / second and his Sprinting speed is increased by 5 feet / second.

HEALTHY

CrP: 20

Nothing can stop this juggernaut, especially not weapons. This Mastery gives the character a one time, but permanent +1d8 to their Vitality. This dice is affected by the Min Vitality of that character's Con.

IMPROVED DEFENSE

CrP: 25

Through muscle training or by simply having a talent for placing their shield in the right place at the right time, the character is able to move effectively parry blows that would otherwise land. Each purchase of this Mastery allows the character to increase their defense dice by 1 size verses one attacker up to their standard defense die. Each additional purchase doubles the cost of this Mastery.

IRON SIDES

CrP: 35

This is a nickname often given to these characters. Whether through ritual scarification or just simply having thick skin, this character has a natural +1 DR.

QUICK HEALING

CrP: 15

Some people just bounce back from wounds that would have put others down for months. With this Mastery, a character heals 1 day quicker for each level of wound severity. So, a 5 Severity Wound would heal in 10 days ( 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 0) instead of 15 ( 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1).

SHARP SHOOTER

CrP: 35

Careful aim can get you so far; sometimes, some people are just born with an eye for distance. The penalties for long ranged shots are lowered from 16 to 12, and from 32 to 24.

SHIELD BASIC TRAINING

CrP: 10

This teaches a character the basic understanding of how to properly use and employ shields in combat. This training covers all types of shields.

You can always try to use a shield without the training, however, you gain a -4 to your defense when doing so.

You can further your training in shields to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

WEAPON BASIC TRAINING

CrP: 10

By gaining the basic understanding of how a weapon is wielded, used and taken care of, you not only expand its longevity, but also your longevity on the battlefield.

Weapon training is taken for individual weapons, however its benefits extend to weapons of that share similar features.

A person completely untrained in weapons both Attacks and Defends with that weapon using a d12!.

However, one trained uses a d20! Attack and Defense (depending on whether or not they use a shield).

Additionally, this is extended to any weapon that shares similar qualities as defined by the Weapon Tiers, up to Tier 4.

For example, a character with training only in the Dusack extends that training to all melee weapons.

A character with training in both a Melee and Ranged weapons, gains that training to all weapons.

So, why would you take training in multiple weapons once you have one melee and one ranged? Because this training allows you to further master that weapon, increasing its usefulness.

You can further your training in weapons to increase the benefits. See the Weapon, Shield, & Armor Advanced Training Sidebar for more details.

SKILL MASTERIES

DILIGENT RESEARCHER

Those that have trained themselves to study, listen, and watch, can make more out of resources as their fingers than others. When using an Asset, those with this Mastery half the Time Investment.

EASY OPERATOR

CrP: 30

Some people come alive under pressure: some people are alive to begin with. When making a Skill Check in a Skill Challenge vs Passive Opposition, they gain a +3 to their rolls. This Mastery also increases the price of the Grace Under Pressure Mastey by 30.

When Multitasking, this bonus is only applied to one dice.

GRACE UNDER PRESSURE

CrP: 20

This character doesn't sweat anything in a competition. When making a Skill Check in a Skill Challenge vs Active Opposition, they gain a +3 to their rolls. This Mastery also increases the price of the Easy Operator Mastery by 30.

When Multitasking, this bonus is only applied to one dice.

GROUNDED MIND

CrP: 30

Hobbies keep you grounded and can keep you from snapping annoying people’s necks when the pressure is on. When recovering Stress through hobbies, this Mastery increases the amount they recover by 1.

KNACK

CrP: 10

Sometimes people have a gift for certain things. When buying this Mastery, the character selects a Skill Suite. They get one free reroll per session for this Skill Suite or any of its Advanced Skills. This can be for a Skill Check or it can be for any Leverage you brought in because of that Skill.

They can buy Knack multiple times, selecting the same or different Skill Suite, however each purchase costs 10 CrP more each time.

MULTITASKER

CrP: 25

Some people can keep their minds in two places. When Multitasking, those with this Mastery gain an extra d4! to one of their Skill Checks.

OLD FRIEND

CrP: 15*

Your character didn’t start in a vacuum and he’s got resources he can draw on when he needs a helping hand. When you purchase this Mastery, you gain an Asset; it can actually be a friend but it can also be a membership to a club, a parent with a library, or whatever else you can think of. The Time Investment starts at 2 Hours and their Rank in a Skill of your choosing starts at 5.

Players subtract their Charisma from the price and don’t have to redeem it right away, however, once redeemed (and defined), however, it can’t be changed.

This Mastery can be bought multiple times, each time gaining a new Asset they can use, but each purchase adds 5 to the price.

POLYGLOT

CrP: 15

This character has the gift of gab and can pick up on other languages quicker than most, learning the nuances of phrases and meanings. Any language that the polyglot gains Rank 4 or above in is considered a native tongue for her.

REAL HELPER

CrP: 15

When times get tough, sometimes the tough get helping. Those with this Mastery give others an additional +3 when someone else uses Preparation that they made. This is in addition to the other benefits of Preparation.

SHORT SLEEPER

CrP: 35

Some weirdos don’t need to sleep as much as the rest of us. What they do with that time will forever remain a mystery but we can assume it’s nothing good. That’s people with this Mastery, who half the each threshold when it comes to Long Term Exhaustion from sleep.

SURVIVAL TRAINING

CrP: 20

While others suffer from lack of sleep or water, this character keeps going. When you buy this Mastery you must select Food, or Water. For Long Term Exhaustion, you half each threshold, allowing you to survive (and indulge) yourself on less.

So, if you selected Food, you can Indulge on 2.5 lbs of food, go on Maintenance on 1.5 lbs, and suffer from Deprivation under .5 lb.

You can buy this Mastery twice, the first for the base price, the second time for 35 CrP. The second time you purchase it, you get the other option so you can’t pick the same thing the second time, sorry.

THIEVES CANT

CrP: 35

Much like Eardstapa, Thieves Cant is an ad hoc language used for members of the many trades, but instead of marks, they use clothing and turns of phrase to communicate their intentions to others. Thieves Cant gives the ability to say things without saying them.

Thieves Cant is based on call and response, with confirmation and steps. The examples below are taken from the I Duchi Selvaggi version of the Cant, which is the most common and from which other Cants draw their lineage from.

LAY OF THE LAND This is before contact is made. The agent is looking for an initial contact. He’s look specifically for two of something; two rings, two scarves, two knives, etc.. The place the twins are in signifies what line of work the person is in.

APPROACH & CONFIRMATION Now, because it’s entirely possible that someone wears two necklaces or two rings on their right index finger, the agent has to do an approach. This is usually a code phrase disguised as an innocuous sentence followed by an innocuous reply.

The key here is that often the two professionals don’t know who the other is, so an overtly familiar greeting from a strange is usually a give away they’re a master of the many trades.

The most common use of Thieves Cant is gathering information. In this case, after the confirmation they ask if you’d like to play cards.

The new thief then pays money into the pot for each card played, paying for the information he’s getting, as he asked about the other’s estates, friends, and family.

Hearts is safe, meaning he’s a friend and can be trusted, Diamond means you asked about a good mark, Clubs is heavily guard, and Spades means the person in question is looking for work.

The dealer may also talk about people, places, or things; the card he deals when he brings them up applying to them.

Here’s some common Cant phrases:

"You settled down yet?"
Are you working with a guild or are you solo?
"How's your mother's health?"
How difficult is the job?
"I think that tower's at least 14 spans high"

"No, you're crazy, I'm sure it's only 10"
No, I won't. You'll get 10
"Aren't you a sight for sore eyes"

"Haven't seen you in awhile"

This should be roleplayed out, with the player with Thieves Cant getting a Wisdom check to clarify anything that didn’t quite come through.

SOCIAL MASTERIES

ANIMAL MAGNETISM

CrP: 15

Some people have that inner force that comes with total confidence, allowing them to quickly put others on the back foot. In a Conflict, if you have higher Honor than your opponent, you gain Advantage the first round.

CRONY

CrP: 15

Some people don't like the spotlight and are happy to help others. During a Conflict, if you’re not in Position 1, once per round you can increase the size of your dice by 1. Additionally, if your side wins (and you haven’t moved up to Position 1), you gain 1 Honor, regardless if Position 1 shares Honor with you.

GOAL ORIENTED

CrP: 15

Where others are directed by their goals, this person directs them. If they have a goal that relates to the Conflict, they can steal the Advantage Dice once per Conflict for free.

GOAL SYNERGY

CrP: 15

Goals working together is a beautiful thing. Increase the Stress bonus you get by 2 for each Goal that applies beyond the first. So, having two Goals apply now gives you a +10 when Stressing.

IDIOSYNCRATIC

CrP: 20

Some people don’t see the point of going against what they see as their true nature. A person with this Mastery increases the dice they get from their Flaws by 1 size.

NEVERMIND, FORGET IT

CrP: 30

Sometimes you know you’re beaten and the only solution is to get out of there with at least some of your dignity intact. When you lose a Conflict, either by Folding or just simply backing out, you only lose half the Honor (rounded down) you otherwise would have.

RENAISSANCE MAN

CrP: 10

A true Renaissance man is a man (or woman) of many and powerful interests and they know how to use them to charm others. When you purchase this Mastery, designate a Passion; you can use it twice in a Conflict without using Revisiting the Point

STRONG OPENER

CrP: 25

A good opening salvo can really put you ahead and this character has a sense for it. If you have Advantage on the first round of a Conflict, temporarily increase its size by 1 size for that round.
