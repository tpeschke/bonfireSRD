What Is Weird?

Seems like a reasonable place to start. It is called a lot of things by different cultures: mana, magic, juju, but essentially, it’s the metaphysical power that allows people to reshape reality, which is why working with it is often called weirdshaping, or simply shaping, if you don’t want to sound pretentious.

Weird lies beneath the surface of all things, a hidden power that imbues life with those indescribable feelings. It is the reason you feel attracted to someone at first sight, are speechless when you see a beautiful view, or the reason why planets have healing properties. It is not that some things are inherently ‘magical’ or ‘non-magical’ - all things have weird that is waiting to be shaped.

Anatomy Of A Spell

When you shape the weird, it manifests in the Middle Realm as a spell, which is broken up into different parts or “Rudiments”, which are split into types. More details on the specifics is found under the Acquiring Rudiments section below but here’s a brief overview:

SHAPE This is the shape that the spell shows up in. This can be a circle, cone, or wall, for example.

RANGE This depends on the Shape of the spell but generally it’s the reach of the spell: how long is the cone or how far can you launch a salvo shaped spell.

ORIGIN This is where the spell comes into being: this can start as the weird-adept and emanate out, it can imminent from his touch, or it can manifest yards away from him.

EFFECT This is where the spell usually gets its name and it’s what it actually does. A spell can cause Stress, remove friction, change appearances, and so on. Effects will also list the Interval, which will talk about next, and the base Range for that spell.

Effects are also special because you can add multiple Effects (or even the same effect multiple times, thus making it more powerful) to the same spell but increasing the Drain in the same amount. If it isn’t clear how an Effect would work if Stacked with itself, there’s a Stacking section for each Effect that should clear things up.

SHAPING A SPELL

Each of a spell’s Rudiments will have a Drain value, which added together, is how much that spell costs to cast. A spell must have at least 1 of each Rudiment type.

FALLOUT

Bending reality and shaping primordial force is not an easy thing to do and nature doesn’t like it. When you first shape a spell, you take Stress equal to your Encumbrance. This is a one time cost so continuing to shape the same spell doesn't further Stress you out (as long as you don't suffer weirdburn, of course).

Weird Traditions

Ways of shaping the Weird are split up into different “Traditions”, which with their own bent on ways the Weird can manifest. These are:

ANIMISM

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the body, forcing it to endure beyond its normal limits and making it into a weapon.

CALLING

This Tradition focuses on the Realms beyond the Middle one we’re so fond of. It involves the summoning of demons, elementals, and spirits.

CHARM

This focuses on the manifestation of the Weird in its raw and primal form. This allows the Weird to interact with our world in a more physical way.

GLAMOUR

This Tradition focuses on the manipulation of the senses, tricking the brain into seeing what the weird-adept wants you to see.

INVESTITURE

This focuses on inanimate objects and either imbuing them with power or manifesting the latent potential that is already in them.

OCCULTISM

This Tradition focuses on the hidden world and opening of the third mind: the divination of knowledge and the unseen.

SORTILEGE

This Tradition focuses on fate, chance, and probability: more specifically, it focuses on messing with it so that things go in your favor.

Alternative Ways To Cast

🜂 Advanced Rule

SIMULSHAPING

If you have enough SP, you may cast more than one spell in the same second, however you gain 1 Stress for each additional spell cast this way.

CONCERT SHAPING

For Wild Magic-Users, it is possible to unite your spirits and channel together, thus improving your channeling overall.

When Concert Shaping, there is a ‘lead’ Magic User who casts the spell. Each person added either their channeling dice reduced by 1 size for each additional person (down to a d4!) whose also adding dice or reduces the channeling time, though it takes 1 person to reduce it by 1 second, 2 additional people for the next second (so 3 total), and so on.

To effectively Concert Cast, all Magic Users must remain linked through physical contact, though not all of them need to touch the lead Magic User you must be able to be able to trace from that channeler to the lead, but beyond that, they must have some connection with each other. Each channeler needs to have a Devotion to the lead Magic User or the shaping will simply not work.

The lead Magic User takes all the SP from the other and uses it to cast his spell, though he’s still affected by his SP limit and may gain Stress because of it.

Acquiring Rudiments

At 1st level and as dedicated by your Archetypes, you’ll have the opportunity to acquire new Rudiments, which you can then use to shape spells. You’ll do so by picking a Rudiment table and then rolling on it. If you roll a Rudiment you already have, you may select the Rudiment on either side of it. For Effects, you roll on the Effects By Tradition table and then go to the corresponding sub-table. You may spend a CrP to reroll as per the norm, even during level up.

Players that seek out the knowledge to gain a specific Rudiment during play can forgo this roll and simply gain that Rudiment, however, they can’t learn more Rudiments than their Archetype and level dictates.

At first level, it is required that you have at least 1 Rudiment from each type (so, 1 Shape Rudiment, 1 Origin Rudiment, and so on) but once that’s satisfied they can go back.

TEACHING YOUR SPELLS TO OTHERS

🜂 Advanced Rule

Magic spells are complex things with more layers than an onion, so it’s not a simple task to teach another a spell you know, even if they’re using the same magic system.

The first step is to be taught the basic of the spells. This is an Weirdcraft Skill Check with the penalty being set by which type of magic is doing the teaching and learning.

For instance, a Sourcerer’s understanding of magic is innate, making it hard to articulate how they’re able to do something to someone who understands that same thing through study. In the same way, Wizards and Mages shape magic completely differently than Speakers who shape spells using the Divine Language.

This takes a 1d4! days of study and teaching, as well as ‘dry’ runs, after which the Check is made.

If it’s successful, the person has the basics and may gain that spell as part of their normal level up (instead of rolling a random spell) or they may learn is instantly, but this takes additional work, costing them CrP to the tune of 25 - the learner’s Int score, as they have to devote additional time to finally cementing it in memory, time that might be taking up with other things.

A failure indicates that more time and study must be taken, increasing the days of study by 1 dice size (d6! days of study for the second try, d10! for the fourth attempt and so on).

Shape Rudiment Descriptions

Note that all shapes glow slightly with strange luminesce, alerting people where the spell is.

CIRCLE

DRAIN 2

The Circle is the most basic Shape. It extends from the Origin with a Radius equal to the Base Range of the Effect. Anyone within the Circle is affected by the spell. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape. If not cast far enough away, this could also include the weird-adept.

CHAIN

DRAIN 0

This is the most primal Shape a spell can come in. The weird-adept casts the spell and it chooses 1 viable, random target within the Range of the Spell. After half the spell’s Interval, it then leaps to the next. Anyone hit by the spell suffers its Effects for the entire time the spell is cast.

People may be hit multiple times, which simply counts as the spell Stacking on them, but the weird-adept may try and redirect the spell, causing it to skip over a person, but they take Stress equal to the number of times they’ve done so.

CONE

DRAIN 3

The Cone starts with its tip at the Origin and then extends in a 45 degree arch from it up to the spell’s Range. Anyone within the Cone is affected by the spell. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval or if a person enters/re-enters the shape.

The Cone can also be moved and shifted, rotating around its Origin, moving a facing every 3 seconds.

SALVO

DRAIN 5

The Salvo is the spell chucked up to its Range from the spell’s Origin. It requires a ranged attack to hit (though ignoring Cover). On a miss, it follows the normal ranged miss rules. Anyone that is hit, suffers the full effects of the spell. If it deals damage or inflicts Stress, this occurs on the Interval.

Those within 10 ft of the Salvo’s touchdown location (even on a total miss) suffer the effects of the spell for half its Interval.

SPHERE

DRAIN 5

The spell takes on the shape of a 2 ft diameter sphere that flies around, coming into being at the spell’s Origin. While the weird-adept has complete control over the direction with only minimal effort (so it doesn’t affect what else he’s doing), it still has some limitations. The first is that it only goes 10 ft / second, no faster, no slower, and the weird-adept may only turn it 45 degrees every 3 seconds.

Anyone touched by the sphere suffers the Effects of the spell.

RAY

DRAIN 2

The ray is a thin line of magic that extends from the spell’s Origin up the 3 times the spell’s Range. It manifests in a single instant, requiring a roll to hit, but those that it hits, suffer the full Effect of the spell.

If no one was hit and the spell is still being cast, the weird-adept can manifest another ray and try again until he hits or he decasts the spell.

WALL

DRAIN 2

The wall is a line that starts at the spell’s Origin and extends up to its Range, but also into the air and below the ground up to 50 ft. It also doesn’t have to be a straight line, weaving, twisting and turning, however, no part of it can get within 5 ft of another part. It must also be anchored to the ground. The wall cannot be moved once placed.

Anyone passing through the wall is affected by the Spell. Those that are in the wall’s way when it is cast, get a defense roll vs a ranged attack to get out of the way.

Origin Rudiment Descriptions

AURA

DRAIN 5

This makes it so the Origin starts at the weird-adept (and any equipment he carries) and then extends out from them.

TOUCH

DRAIN 2

This makes it so the Origin starts at whatever the weird-adept touches and then extends from there. If the target is trying to resist being touched, the weird-adept needs to make an Unarmed attack against them: the weird-adept is considered armed.

NEAR

DRAIN 1

This makes the Origin of the spell up to the Effect’s Base Range.

FAR

DRAIN 3

This makes the Origin of the spell up to double the Effect’s Base Range.

Demons are notorious shapeshifters and beguilers but through this spell, the summoner is able to force them (somewhat) into a single shape, though this is often a grotesque and twiced form. From there, the weird-adept can order the demon to do their bidding.

There are different types and categories of demons, each with different abilities that can get a specific job. Some are brutish and stupid, while others are cunning and skillful. Some come linked with certain symbols, such as gateways or the birth of children, that give them powers related to them.

The basic spell conjures just a random demon type, which Circle value is 1, or the lowest rung of the aetherial world that exists between the other realms.

The summoner can also weigh the odds in his favor when it comes to what categories of demon he summons. He can modify the dice roll by his level in either direction. He may also use his SP in the same way though this does increase the Drain by a like amount.

A demon appears in a random location within the Shape of the spell and rolls a d3 initiative dice.

Demons, even relatively dumb ones, are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change a demon’s command, the demon rolls a d20 + its Circle (and the Circle of any other demons controlled by the weird-adept) in a one round Conflict. If it wins, the weird-adept loses control and the demon goes rogue, first seeking out the weird-adept to try and kill them as quickly as possible. If it can’t do that, it will do as much damage as possible to what’s around it and then try to kill itself.

The weird-adept can try to cast Bind Demon again to try and regain control. If the Circle you could have summoned is greater than the rogue demon’s Circle, you regain control... for now.

Demons are forced to stay in the area they were summoned in, following it wherever it goes if the Shape can be moved. If it breaks its binding, it may, of course, run free.

Demons are wily creatures, always trying to find loopholes in their orders and while there are implicit bindings in the summon that prevent obvious loophole breaching, they’ll do the bare minimum of what’s asked of them.

This binding does make them more susceptible to suggestion as well and others may enter into a Conflict with the demon to get it to do or reveal things, on one condition: it can’t go against their summoner’s orders.

So, a demon ordered to patrol a hall can be forced to reveal information about its master or how many people are in each room, but it can’t be forced by others to stop patrolling (or even not attack the players, should it be told to kill anyone in the hall).

STACK Increase the Circle of the demon by 1.

BIND ELEMENTAL HEART

TRADITION Calling

BASE DRAIN 3

BASE RANGE 20 ft

INTERVAL 30 seconds

EFFECT This spell either conjures an elemental heart from it’s realm or binds one that has gone rogue.

Once in this realm, the elemental heart robes itself in nearby sympathetic elements, giving a form with which it can then move. Without it’s dead kin around, the heart becomes inert and defenseless. So, while there are Order and Chaos Elementals, neither of those two things have a physical manifestation in the Middle Realm and so they simply lay there.

A conjured elemental with a Weird of d4!. The higher the Weird, the more powerful the elemental. Stats for elementals are found in the Bonfire Bestiary.

Elementals are ‘liquid’ and can shift and change their form. Their Weird can be invested to increase their baseline stats and special abilities. How much this costs is determined by the type of heart summoned. Weird can assigned at the time of summoning but also rearranged after, though this takes 1 second per point shift

A summoned elemental heart appears in a random location within the Shape of the spell, determined by d12 for direction (12 being where the weird-adept is facing and 6 being directly behind him) and d20 for distance, and rolls a d12 initiative dice. During this time, the elemental is getting its barings and forming itself proper.

Elementals are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change an elemental’s command, the elemental rolls a d8! + its Weird (and the Weird of any other elementals controlled by the weird-adept) in a one round Conflict. If it wins, the weird-adept loses control and the elemental goes rogue, first seeking out the weird-adept to try and kill them as quickly as possible

The weird-adept can try to cast Bind Elemental Heart again to try and regain control. If the Weird you could have summoned is greater than the rogue elemental’s Weird, you regain control... for now.

If this spell is cast in any other Shape than Circle, the elemental is forced into that shape and unless the Shape can be moved, the elemental is rooted to the spot, only able to attack people who get within their Measure of that spot.

Shapes that require a roll to hit (Salvo and Ray) and do count that as the Elemental attack and thus include the Elementals damage or special attack.

STACK Increase the summoned Weird of the Elemental by d4!.
