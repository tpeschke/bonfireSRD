SPELLCRAFTING

Crafting spells is a rather simple process with a lot of steps as it allows you to alter almost every aspect of what you cast to fit your needs in the moment. If you're a Runegalder, you'll use this steps whenever you gain a new spell (Ignoring Step 0).

STEP 0: FOR WILD MAGIC CASTERS ONLY: DETERMINE LOCATION

When Channeling as a Wild Magic caster (which is in the Advanced Rules), you'll pick a location that begins to shimmer with arcane energy, alerting everyone that something funky is going on. The default area this shimmer covers is equal to the Full Area of Effect and will grow and shrink as the magic user hones in the Area of Effect in Step 3.

You may change this location but for every 10 ft off of the original location, the caster gains 1 Stress.

STEP 1: CHANGE MAGNITUDE

You can increase the power of spell at this step. The specifics are determined by the spell but for the most part you'll replace Xs in the spell's description with the Magnitude.

Increasing the Magnitude costs the Base Cost of the spell and increases both the Positive and Negative effects.

You can choose to buy down the Negative and Positive effects of spell once for every time you pay the Base Cost again but you do so separately. This buy down subtracts from any X. If there is no X in the effect, buying down once simply eliminates the effect entirely. This can be done until one is zeroed out.

The caster is always affected by the Negative Effects as if they were Magnitude + 1.

Keep track of the cost of your Magnitude increases and your buy downs for the final step.

STEP 2: ALTAR AREA OF EFFECT (AOE)

Now you can grow, shrink, or move the Area of Effect of your spell. How easy or hard the spell is to move is based on its size. The +X by each section is the cost.

Anyone caught in the AoE of a spell suffers the full penalty of the spell for the Duration: the only way to dodge is to get out before the spell goes off.

Spells always start centered on the caster. Additionally, if the Caster is still in the AoE when the spell is cast, it affects him first, regardless of where he is. If dies or goes unconscious, the spell immediately dispels.

Full +0

This is just the base AoE and is a circle with a radius equal to the spell's Area of Effect. It can be treated as anywhere in size between the full radius and just above half that, rounded up. So, a 5 ft spell can be shrunk down to a circle with a radius of 5.1 ft.

Half Size +2

At this point, the spell may be shaped and made more directed in its focus.

ZONE: This is a circle with a radius of between half the AoE, rounded up, and 5 ft in radius.

RAY: this is a thin beam that shoots from the cast in a given direction. The spell affects the first person hit by the ray. The ray is blocked by any opaque matter.

If the ray doesn't initial hit someone, the magic user by manipulate it by turning or walking until it does. This does bite into the Duration of the spell.

CONE: This affects anyone within a 90 deg arche in front of the caster. The caster can manipulate this area by walking or turning.

SPHERE: This is a hovering ball, 1 ft in diameter, that's generated within the Full AoE of the spell.

It can then be directed by the caster remotely to move around at a Jogging speed. If the caster loses sight of it, the Sphere keeps moving in the same direction. This sphere can move through solid objects.

WALL: This lays an unmoving line around the battlefield. If the spell is not Elemental, this wall appears as a semi-translucent shimmer, that people can pass through freely. Anyone doing so is affected by the spell. Shooting ranged weaponry through it gives the target an extra d4 of Cover.

The wall must be continuously laid with no breaks but it doesn't have to be in a straight line though it can never have pieces of itself touch itself. Its total length is equal to the Area of Effect or shorter and its width is 2.5 ft and it can't be moved or shifted once placed.

Individual +3

This affects one person at a time and can take the following shapes:

PERSONAL: This affects only the caster. Increasing the Magnitude and buying down the Negative or Positive effects costs half.

TOUCH: This affects anyone the caster touches. If the person is trying to dodge, the caster will need to make an attack roll. Increasing the Magnitude and buying down the Positive or Negative effects costs half.

CHAIN: The spells starts at the caster (but doesn't affect him) and then jumps to the nearest person within the Full AoE of the person, who is then affected by the spell. It then leaps to the next closest person within the Full AoE from the first person, etc. with each person who get hits feeling the effect of the spell. If no one is found within the Full AoE of a person, the spell simply fizzles out.

Each time it leaps, the spell loses 1 Magnitude. If its Magnitude drops to 0 or it runs out of valid targets, the spell fizzles out.

A person can't be hit twice with the same Chained spell, so someone that's already been hit doesn't count as the "next closest person".

The spell's Duration is tracked individually for each person hit; the Duration starts when that person is hit.

TARGET: This spell affects one person as long as they're in "sight" of the caster, meaning nothing solid is between them and the caster (illumination, therefore, doesn't affect whether or not you can target someone) and within the Full AoE when the spell is cast.

STEP 3: CALCULATE RANGE

This step moves the AoE of the spell in relation to the caster. How expensive it is is determined by the AoE chosen in the last step and the base unit is found under the spell's Base Range.

The Base Cost is still the Base Cost and doesn't factor in anything else.

FULL Base Cost x Units Moved x 2

HALF Base Cost x Units Moved

INDIVIDUAL Base Cost x Units Moved / 2

STEP 4: CALCULATE DURATION

This is where you increase the Duration of your spell. You multiply the Base Cost by the number of times you want to increase the Base Duration.

So, tripling the time Duration will cost triple your Base Cost.

You can theoretically go into percentages here (only increasing a 20 second spell to 25 seconds), but the cost is always rounded up, so that would still be double your Base Cost.

STEP 5: ALL TOGETHER NOW

Now add all the individual costs from each step together like so:

Final Cost = Magnitude Adjustment total + AoE Cost + Range Cost + Duration

CANTRIPS

Cantrips are just severally reduced version of the spell. They always cost 1 SP and are limited to how creative you can be with your spells. So, if you want to scratch your back with the Telekinesis spell, be my guest. They can’t do direct damage, or really affect others but besides that, go nuts.

MAGICAL FALLOUT

Making the primal forces of the cosmos your bitch is tiring work. All casters automatically gain a level of Fatigue after they cast their spell.

On top of this, being around a gate of the eldritch is unsettling for those unused to it. It's the equivalent of hearing feedback from a microphone, only reality is the speaker. Everyone who’s not a caster of the same magical training who’s within 200 ft of where the spell goes off gains 1 point of Stress.

As stated, this is not a pleasant experiance for anyone and so enemies will try and payback the magic user for this unkindness, especially beasts and non-intelligent monsters who will make the magic user their main target until distracted again. Everyone will also innately known where the epicenter of the spell was so if the magic user is there attempting to hide, he should move with all haste.

ORDERS of MAGIC

Some that study magic wish to categorize it so that they may better understand it.

There are technically 7 different Disciplines of Magic: Animancy, Alteration, Conjuration, Elemental, Illusion, Invocation, Mysticism, though the Elemental Order is further subdivided into the individual elements: Air, Chaos, Earth, Fire, Order, Water.

ANIMANCY This Order focuses on the study of life and death. Its spells focus on the manipulation of the body and spirit.

ALTERATION This Order focuses on manipulation of magic into crude matter. It focuses on wards and barriers.

CONJURATION This Order focuses on summoning things from the other planes of existence and forcing them into your control. It focuses on summoning and banishing.

ELEMENTAL This Order focuses on the primitive forces on the Middle Realm; the building blocks of creation. It focuses on conjuring elementals or channeling them into different forms.

ILLUSION This Order focuses on fooling the senses and the hijacking of the brain to make things appear and disappear. Its spells focus on making hallucinations of all kinds.

INVOCATION This Order focuses on the shifting of kinetic and potential energy in an area. Its spells can Fatigue enemies or change the direction they’re heading.

MYSTICISM This Order is more subtle. It’s the art of tapping into the secrets of the Painted World and shifting the unseen winds of fate. It’s spells focuses on scrying.

BIND ELEMENTAL HEART

ORDERS Elemental | Conjuration

BASE COST 3

COMPONENTS None

DURATION 30 Seconds

RADIUS 20 ft

POSITIVE This spell either conjures an elemental heart from it’s realm or binds one that has gone rogue.

Once in this realm, the elemental heart robes itself in nearby sympathetic elements, giving a form where it can then move. Without it’s dead kin around, the heart becomes inert and defenseless. So, while there are Order and Chaos Elementals, neither of those two things have a physical manifestation in the Middle Realm and so they simply lay there.

This spell conjures an elemental with a Weird of Xd4. The higher the Weird, the more powerful the elemental. Stats for elementals are found in the Bestiary.

Elementals are ‘liquid’ and can shift and change their form. Their Weird can be invested to increase their baseline stats and special abilities. How much this costs is determined by the type of heart summoned. Weird can assigned at the time of summoning but also rearranged after, though this takes 1 second per point shift

A summoned elemental heart appears in a random location, determined by d12 for direction (12 being where the caster is facing and 6 being directly behind him) and d20 for distance, within the Area of Effect and rolls a d12 initiative dice. During this time, the elemental is getting its barings and forming itself proper

Elementals are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change an elemental’s command, the elemental rolls a d8! + its Weird (and the Weird of any other elementals controlled by the caster) vs d8! + caster level x 2. If it wins, the caster loses control and the elemental goes rogue, first seeking out the caster to try and kill them as quickly as possible

The caster can try cast Bind Elemental Heart again to try and regain control. If the Weird you could have summoned is greater than the rogue elemental’s Weird, you regain control... for now.

Elementals cast in specialized Area of Effects (Sphere, Ray, Wall, etc.) are forced into that shape and unless the Area of Effect is allowed to move, the elemental is rooted to the spot, only able to attack people who get within their Measure of that spot.

NEGATIVE This gives all elementals within the Area of Effect a +X chance to break their bindings and go berserk. Additionally, casting this spell triggers this check.
