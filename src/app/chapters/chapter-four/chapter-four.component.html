Archetypes represent your character’s background training and starting skills. Your character’s class is a gestalt of two Archetypes of your choosing.

Archetypes are divided into Archetype Categories: Expert, Magical, Servant, Socialite, and Warrior. These represent the overall theme of the individual Archetype. For instance, Warrior Archetypes tend to be good at things related to combat. So, if you’re looking to make a combat focused character, you can look there.

You choose a Primary and a Secondary Archetype and pay the price in CrP for both based on your race.

You use the Vitality Die, the Skills & Masteries, and the Primary Drawback sections from the Primary, and combine both Archetype Abilities section.

Each Archetype also has a Primary Boon, which modifies the Archetype Abilities of your Primary and is based on the Secondary's Archetype category.

While you may want to double down into the exact same Archetype but Magical Archetypes are the only ones that you can do that with. You may choose two Archetypes from the same Category, however.

Your GM may choose to reject a Class based on the fact that the two Archetypes don’t fit together thematically. So, while a Thief/Priest (Beast Covenant) might be really powerful because of the Beast Covenant’s Shape Changing ability, if there’s not a Patron of Thieves and Animals in the campaign world, you’re out of luck.

Expert Archetypes are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines or magic as a source of strength.

Expert Archetypes tend to rely on Intellect and Wisdom, primarily.

RUNEGALDER

Unlike other magic-users, the Runegalder seals magical power in his bond, allowing him to not risk his body when working with supernatural forces. This allows him to cast quickly and consistently but limits the amount and flexibility of his spells.

VITALITY DIE

d6

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Runegalders receive 1 free purchase of the Lore Skill Suite as well as an additional free purchase of Literacy in their native language and the Arcana skill.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

VANCIAN CASTING The Runegalder chains the power of spells to various runes that he then invests, sealing them with his blood until he’s ready to release them.

Every 24 hours, the Runegalder can seal 3 spells. He can also bind an additional spell for every 4 levels he’s obtained. This means that he may cast those spells at any time by simply speaking the rune they’re linked to, once prepared.

Speaking the rune releases it from it’s binding so speaking the same rune again before he’s resealed it will not recast the spell - the spell’s power is gone.

He may cast a spell multiple times per day but he’ll need to bind each instance of the spell to a different rune.

Speaking a rune in this way doesn’t affect the Runegalder and so he ignores Magical Fallout to himself.

He may also unseal a rune without actually casting it but he is not able to immediately reseal a new rune right after.

GRIMOIRE This is the Runegalder’s spell book and keeps the spells that he binds to runes. Unlike a normal spell book, however, the spells are scribed in meteorite ink and blood that seals their magic power in the text itself. Essentially, the Runegalder’s Grimoire does the channeling latently for him.

This Grimoire starts with 3 random spells and gains an additional spell every level. New spell’s are equal to the Runegalder’s level.

A spell has a number of SP equal to 5 + their level (so at 1st level, you would get three that each have 6 SP) that are invested in it when it is scribed into the Grimoire. These SP cannot be redistributed and changed: spells in the Grimoire are immutable.

So, a 2nd level Runegalder scribes Disorient. He gains 7 SP. He chooses to make it a Sphere spell (3 SP), increase the Magnitude to 2 and buy down the Negative effect 2 times. Now, whenever he speaks a rune sealed to that spell, he casts it with an Area of Effect of a Sphere, with a Magnitude of 2, and no Negative effects to him.

Spells inscribed at different levels count as completely different spells and must be sealed to different runes. So if the Runegalder has a 6 SP Flash Grenade and a 11 SP Flash Grenade, he would need two runes to cast both. He couldn’t simply bind “Flash Grenade” to one rune and then choose which he wanted to use in the moment.

SKILL FOCUS (Arcana, 1) Through the course of her career, a Runegalder seeks to further their knowledge of the occult. They receive 1 free purchase of the Arcana Advanced Skill at every other level up.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Your Grimoire starts at 4 spells and new spells have 7 + level SP. They also gain 2 new spells per level. You can also seal a new spell every 3 levels.

MAGICAL The only magical secondary archetype available for this archetype is the Runegalder. See the Runegalder Doubling Down Benefits Sidebar below.

SERVANT Your Grimoire starts at 4 spells and new spells have 7 + level SP. They also gain 2 new spells per level. You can also seal a new spell every 3 levels.

SOCIALITE Your Grimoire starts at 4 spells and new spells have 7 + level SP. They also gain 2 new spells per level. You can also seal a new spell every 3 levels.

WARRIOR Your Grimoire starts at 4 spells and new spells have 7 + level SP. They also gain 2 new spells per level. You can also seal a new spell every 3 levels.

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

The Runegalder uses his body as a prison for latent magical energies which wears on a person over time. He ages at twice the normal rate.

Additionally, the Runegalder's magic is 'dead' and so is less potent than other types of magic, thus allowing some to shrug off their spells.

When casting a spell, the Runegalder rolls a d20! + 10 + their level. All those that would be affected by the spell roll a d20! + their level. If their total is above the Runegalder's, they're unaffected by the his magic. This roll is made regardless of whether or not the person wants to be affected by the spell.

Runegalder Doubling Down Benefits

VANCIAN CASTING

You can seal a new spell every level.

GRIMOIRE

Your Grimoire starts at 6 spells and new spells have 8 + ( level x 2 ) SP. They gain 3 spells per level.

Additionally, they don’t have to roll a new spell and instead may choose to select a spell they already know and inscribe it anew. If the spell is the same Degree as the copy, it counts as a new spell and may have its SP invested differently. This means that you can have two 1st Degree Earth Shifts that have different Area of Effects, for example.

Choosing to ‘upgrade’ a spell in this way doesn’t destroy the original.

Covenants change which Miracles the Priest has access to while Cosmic Links change the ways you gain and spend Favor.

DIPLOMAT

CAREFUL WORDING A Diplomat has the grace and tact to be able to reword an argument in the most effective way possible. At first level and for every additional 2 levels obtained, the Diplomat receives 1 free reroll of all the dice in that round in a Social Conflict. RASCAL

The Rascal gains a few skills but their main strength is their cunning ability to avert danger, though any brush with death leaves him shaken. On top of this, in Social Conflict, the Rascal wins because he can leverage circumstances in his favor.

VITALITY DIE

d6

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Rascals receive 1 free purchase of the Trades Skill Suite

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

LUCKY The Rascal is naturally blessed with the ability get out of trouble and using that adrenaline that comes as death comes near to dodge the grim reaper and keep running. By taking an amount of Stress up to half their level (rounded up), they can modify a dice roll by that number. This differs from normal Stress because you can take this Stress after you roll, even if you didn't declare you were Stressing on that roll and it applies to all rolls, not just Skill Checks, as per standard Stress rules, but it can only be used under the following conditions:

🜂

They can never be used to make you a better Man-at-Arms. Stress gained this way can only be used to dodge death or escape a bad situation but never to make your character a superman.

🜂

The character must think of a situation that saves his rear end, something that happens to him that’s to his advantage. The key here is that it can’t be impossible but it can be highly improbable. The chandelier rope breaking right at that moment separating him from a blood thirsty guard, a pigeon flying across an archer’s view intercepting an arrow, etc., are all acceptable. It can’t be the result of skill. Him deflecting the blade with his hand like a badass is right out.

Should the character be unable to think of a scenario that fits both of these criteria, he’s unable to gain Stress to save himself.

A Rascal can only use this ability once per Combat, Conflict, or Challenge at first level but can use it an additional time for every 4 levels they've achieved.

GREASE PAINT & GLITTER If there's one thing that a Rascal knows, it’s how to position themselves in the most advantageous light. Any Leverage in Social Dice (besides those from Flaws) that the Rascal brings in are increased by 1 size.

SKILL FOCUS (Any, 2) At first level and every level other up, Rascals receive 2 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skill. Purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level.

War is a ladder by which capable people are able to climb the ranks of society. Warrior Archetypes focus on combat and battle, either by training and discipline or by out maneuvering their opponent.

Unsurprisingly, these Archetypes focus on Constitution, but how you approach combat may decide whether you favor Strength, Dexterity, or even Intellect or Wisdom.

CATERAN

The Cateran is all about flanking your opponent and keeping other fighters locked down. As such, they work best with other fighters.

VITALITY DIE

d8

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Caterans receives the Deadly Flanker Mastery, as well as free Light Armor Training and 1 Weapon Training.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

BABY-SITTING While an individual Cateran may vary in his ability to take care of infants, they all share the ability to keep combatants locked down.

Whenever a person engaged with the Cateran tries to disengage, the Cateran gets an immediate free attack that, if it hits, deals half damage. This happens regardless of where they are on the Clock and doesn’t effect when their next attack happens.

CLOSED OPENINGS Flanking is the art of dividing people’s attention and the Cateran has perfected that art. Anyone flanked by a Cateran lowers their attack die by 1 size, regardless of who they’re attacking.

SHADOWING By keeping close to their opponent, the Cateran can negate people’s defensive posturing.

Once at first level and an additional time for every 3 levels they obtain, a Cateran can negate any bonuses an opponent gets from moving, whether that’s shifting, stepping back, or whatever.

This also stops their opponent from moving.

BUM RUSH A Cateran can also use his allies to more effectively flank their opponents. Once at first level and an additional time for every 2 levels they’ve obtained, the Cateran can give another ally flanking the same enemy the benefits of the Deadly Flank Mastery if they don’t have it.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Bum Rush is increased to every level

MAGICAL Shadowing is increased to every other level

SERVANT Shadowing is increased to every other level or Bum Rush is increased to every level

SOCIALITE Bum Rush is increased to every level

WARRIOR Shadowing is increased to every other level or Bum Rush is increased to every level and Bum Rush is reduced to every 4 levels

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

The Cateran is a group fighter and fighting alone can be unnerving for him. When engaged with enemies alone and not flanking, the Cateran is unable to use his Honor.

Knights receive the Armor Training (Heavy), Shield Training, and 1 Weapon Training Mastery for free.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

IMPROVED MASTERIES A Knight is a master of armor and defense, spending years conditioning himself to be able to wear armor and use a shield without breaking a sweat. Knights can purchase all Tiers of Armor and Shield Masteries for half cost.

INHERITANCE This character may have had an extra leg up in life, allowing them the opportunity to start with better equipment than others. They gain a free weapon of their choice and also gain 20 sc for each CrP cashed in at the end of Character Creation.

CHIVALRY From their strict code comes a strength and confidence that others can only dream of and envy. For the duration of a combat you gain 1 free use of your Honor Die. You gain an additional free Honor Die for every 2 levels you obtain. You can only apply 1 of these bonus Honor Die to a single roll, though that doesn’t stop you adding Honor Dice that you’ve gotten from other sources.

MARTIAL TRAINING A Knight receives a free Tier 3 Armor or Shield Mastery for every 5 levels she’s obtained.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Chivalry is increased to every level

MAGICAL Chivalry is increased to every level

SERVANT Chivalry is increased to every level and Martial Training is increased to every level

SOCIALITE Chivalry is increased to every level

WARRIOR Chivalry is increased to every level and Martial Training is increased to every level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

The Knight is a creature of Honor and respects Honor where they find it. They may be killers but they have a code, whether articulated or not. A Knight may never use a long ranged weapon on a foe that has greater than Average Honor.

SOLDIER

🜂 Advanced Rule

Bonfire character are not meant to stand alone and this goes double for the Soldier, who work the best with others. They’re able to shadow others around the battlefield and really focus on being good at one or two weapons.

VITALITY DIE

d8

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Soldiers receive 1 free purchase of the Tactics Skill Suite.

Soldiers receive either the Armor Training (Light) and Shield Training or Armor Training (Medium). On top of this, they gain 1 Weapon Training Mastery for free as well.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

SMALL UNIT TACTICS Soldiers work as a team. If they or anyone they’re adjacent uses a tactic that moves them (Shift, Step Back, etc.), the Soldier or their adjacent ally may also use that Tactic with no penalties.

IMPROVED MASTERIES Soldiers rely on others to make up for their weaknesses, as such, that gives them greater freedom to really specialize in what they’re good at. Soldiers can purchase Tier 1 - 2 Weapon Masteries for half cost.

BUDDY SYSTEM Soldiers fight as a unit, moving and acting as one where possible. Once per combat at first level and an additional time for every 2 levels they obtain, when an adjacent ally switches Tactics, the Soldier may immediately switch to that Tactics as well.

MARTIAL TRAINING A Soldier receives a free Tier 1 Weapon Mastery for every 2 levels he’s obtained.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Buddy System is increased to every level

MAGICAL Buddy System is increased to every level

SERVANT Buddy System is increased to every level or Martial Training lowered to every other level

SOCIALITE Buddy System is increased to every level

WARRIOR Buddy System is increased to every level or Martial Training lowered to every other level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

A Soldier focuses so much time drilling with his preferred weapon that he can often forget that principles with one weapon can apply to another. All Tier 4 and above Weapon Masteries are 2 CrP more expensive.

THUG

The Thug is designed to hit hard and make each strike count. As such they have abilities that remove an enemy’s damage reduction and increase the chance to knock someone out.

VITALITY DIE

d6

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Thugs receive 2 free purchase of the Streetwise Skill Suite.

Thugs receive the Armor Training (Light) Mastery and 1 Weapon Training for free.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

CRITICAL STRIKE If you deal enough damage to move your opponent into the next Wound Category, you trigger a Trauma check with the target rolling a d12!, instead of a d20.

RUN 'EM RAGGED At the beginning of combat, the Thug gains a pool of Stress equal to 5 times his level. On a successfully hit, he may substract an amount of Stress from that pool. If he does so, he inflicts that much Stress on his target, which is healed when the person Takes a Breather.

PRECISION ATTACK The Thug is an expert at making every attack count, he lowers the DR of his opponent by 1 per 3 levels. For /d DR, this is subtracted from the final total.

Example, a level 12 Thug with a dagger (3 dice) vs Scale (2/D), would reduce the enemy’s DR to 4 (6 total DR - 2 from Precision Attack), while one using a club (1 dice) would reduce it to 0 (2 total DR - 2 from Precision Attack).

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT The Stress pool for Run 'Em Ragged is increased to 10 * your level

MAGICAL Precision Attack lowered to every other level

SERVANT Precision Attack lowered to every other level or The Stress pool for Run 'Em Ragged is increased to 10 * your level

SOCIALITE The Stress pool for Run 'Em Ragged is increased 10 * to your level

WARRIOR Precision Attack lowered to every other level or The Stress pool for Run 'Em Ragged is increased to 10 * your level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

The Thug never really rises above his street rat origins: the Leadership, Administration, Rally, and Recruiting Advanced Skills all cost double.

VANGUARD

The Vanguard is the first in the fray, usually engaging multiple enemies at the same time and trying to do as much damage as possible to soften them up for their allies to follow.

VITALITY DIE

d10

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Vanguards receive 1 free purchase of the Tactics Skill Suite.

They also receive the Armor Training (Medium) Training and 1 Weapon Training for free.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

AGAINST THEM ALL When they’re in the thick of it is when the Vanguard thrives. The Vanguard gains their Honor Dice to their Attack rolls against 1 opponent when engaged with 2 or more enemies.

IN & OUT & AROUND Knowing how to slip and out of your opponent’s guards allow the Vanguard to get in and out of a jam quickly.

Once at first level and an additional time for every 2 levels they’ve obtained per combat, the Vanguard may change their engagement zone with one opponent, without any associated penalties.

So, if they’re Threatening their opponent, they could move to Engaged or out to Not Engaged without giving their enemy that free attack.

Additionally, if their enemy has successfully employed the In Their Guard Tactic, the Vanguard can move them back out to simply Engaged.

CROWD CONTROL Surrounded by enemies, the Vanguard learns to use the crowd against themselves, forcing his enemies to get in each other’s way.

A Vanguard can use his full defense against an additional opponent if the two opponents are adjacent to each other

FIRST STRIKE The Vanguard hits hard and hits first. Or at least, they try to.

At the beginning of combat, Vanguards receive a pool of Measure equal to 1 ft + 1 per every 2 levels they’ve obtained. When they first become Engaged with an opponent, they may spend ft from that pool to temporarily increase their Measure to determine who strikes first.

So, a level 6 Vanguard would have a pool of 4 ft to work with. If wielding a Stiletto (Measure 1.5 ft) and closing in to Engage with an enemy wielding a Rapier (Measure 4 ft), he could spend 3 ft of it to allow him to get his hit off first, perhaps crippling his enemy before they have change to respond. Doing so would leave him with 1 ft in his pool for the rest of combat.

After that, combat resumes as normal, so he wouldn’t be able to attack the Rapier wielder until he got In Their Guard.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT In & Out & Around is increased to every level

MAGICAL In & Out & Around is increased to every level

SERVANT In & Out & Around is increased to every level or First Strike is increased to every level

SOCIALITE In & Out & Around is increased to every level

WARRIOR In & Out & Around is increased to every level and First Strike is increased to every level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

Vanguards are a position of Honor and they don’t take charging into battle lightly. A Vanguard loses 1 Honor for every battle they’re not the first on either side to land a melee attack.
