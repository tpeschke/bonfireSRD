Archetypes represent your character’s background training and starting skills. Your character’s class is a gestalt of two Archetypes of your choosing.

Archetypes are divided into Archetype Categories: Expert, Magical, Servant, and Warrior. These represent the overall theme of the individual Archetype. For instance, Warrior Archetypes tend to be good at things related to combat. So, if you’re looking to make a combat focused character, you can look there.

You choose a Primary and a Secondary Archetype and pay the price in CrP for both based on your race.

You use the Vitality Die, the Skills & Masteries, and the Primary Drawback sections from the Primary, and combine both Archetype Abilities section.

Each Archetype also has a Primary Boon, which modifies the Archetype Abilities of your Primary and is based on the Secondary's Archetype category.

While you may want to double down into the exact same Archetype but Magical Archetypes are the only ones that you can do that with. You may choose two Archetypes from the same Category, however.

Your GM may choose to reject a Class based on the fact that the two Archetypes don’t fit together thematically. So, while a Thief/Priest (Beast Covenant) might be really powerful because of the Beast Covenant’s Shape Changing ability, if there’s not a Patron of Thieves and Animals in the campaign world, you’re out of luck.

Expert Archetypes are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines or magic as a source of strength.

Expert Archetypes tend to rely on Intellect, Wisdom, and Charisma, primarily.

ADVENTURER

Adventures gain the most free skills of all Archetypes allowing for the largest breathe of knowledge, however, they’re cheaper skills. They can also help others, giving them a chance to try again at Skill Checks they fail.

VITALITY DIE

d8

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Adventurers receive 2 free purchases of any two different, non-Lore Skill Suites.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

SKILL FOCUS (Any < 16 CrP, 6) At first level and every other level up, Adventurers receive 6 free skill purchases. These can be distributed between any Advanced Skills under 16 CrP in base cost (before Skill Adept) within either of the two Skill Suites he picked at first level. Purchases may not be doubled up on in the same level.

Adventurer As A Secondary

If you choose Adventurer as your Secondary Archetype, you’ll still select 2 non-Lore Skill Suites to pull from, however, if your Primary also has a Skill Focus, you can’t pick that Skill Focus’s Skill Suite.

HELPING HAND The Adventurer is not just about knowing things but also giving others a helping hand in times of need. The Adventurer may allow another person in the party to reroll 1 non-Lore Skill Check per session at first level and an additional time for every 3 levels obtained after that.

SKILL ADEPT This character has a knack for skills. All Advanced Skills have their cost reduced by 1 CrP. If that would reduce a cost to 0, this character instead gets two purchases of the skill for 1 CrP. Additionally, they gain a 10% discount to any Skill Suites they have a Skill Focus for.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Skill Focus increases to every level or Helping Hand increases to every other level

MAGICAL Skill Focus increases to every level

SERVANT Skill Focus increases to every level or Helping Hand increases to every other level

WARRIOR Helping Hand increases to every other level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

Adventurers tend to eschew book smarts for street smarts: the Lore Skill Suite and any Lore Advanced Skills cost double.

d4

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Foils receive 1 free purchase of the Streetwise Skill Suite

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

MIRRORING By agreeing with your enemy, you can sometimes lull them into a false sense of security, causing them to lower their guard. Once per escalation, a Foil can make a guess at a Characteristic his opponent is going to use in that escalation. If he’s correct, that Characteristic is lowered by 1 size for every 3 levels he’s obtained, down to a d4 in size. He also receives 1d4 per dice size he’s lowered in this way.

So a level 15 Foil who correctly guesses a 3d6 characteristic, lowers it to 3d4 and gains 2d4

GASLIGHTING A Foil’s particularly devious when it comes to other’s flaws and weaknesses. Once per Conflict and an additional time for every 2 levels they've obtained, they can guess a character’s Mental or Behavioral Flaw. If correct, they gain it as a d6 Leverage to their dice pool, and prevents their opponent from adding it to their own dice pool.

SHIFT BLAME There’s strength in numbers, especially when those numbers are used as a shield. If the Foil is one of the main players in a Conflict and gets another to put in dice to help, they can make that person the new target of the Conflict. The person coming in, however, is thrown head first into a stressful situation, giving them 1 Stress for every die left in their opponent's dice pool.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Mirroring is increased to every other levels

MAGICAL Gaslighting is increased to every level

SERVANT Mirroring is increased to every other levels or Gaslighting is increased to every level

WARRIOR Mirroring is increased to every other levels

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

Foils are so good at mirroring others that they often forget to reflect on themselves. They only gain positive benefits from social Repercussions if they roll two natural 1s.

LUCKY The Opportunist is naturally blessed with the ability to get out of trouble and using that adrenaline that comes as death comes near to dodge the grim reaper and keep running. By taking an amount of Stress up to a half your level rounded up, they can modify a dice roll by that number. These may be done with the following conditions:

🜂

They can never be used to make you a better fighter. Stress gained this way can only be used to dodge death or escape a bad situation but never to make your character a superman.

🜂

The character must think of a situation that saves his rear end, something that happens to him that’s to his advantage.

NATURAL ADVANTAGE A Opportunist lives on his wits and his ability to use every advantage. If he doesn’t start the combat, he may change one thing about the field for every 2 levels he’s obtained. RUNEGALDER

Unlike other magic-users, the Runegalder seals magical power in his bond, allowing him to not risk his body when working with supernatural forces. This allows him to cast quickly and consistently but limits the amount and flexibility of his spells.

VITALITY DIE

d6

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Runegalders receive 1 free purchase of the Lore Skill Suite as well as an additional free purchase of Literacy in their native language and the Arcana skill.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

VANCIAN CASTING The Runegalder chains the power of spells to various runes that he then invests, sealing them with his blood until he’s ready to release them.

Every 24 hours, the Runegalder can seal a number of spell equal to 1 + his Min Spell Point from his Wisdom. He can also bind an additional rune for every 3 levels he’s obtained. This means that he may cast those spells at any time by simply speaking the rune they’re linked to, once he's prepared them.

Speaking the rune releases it from it’s binding so speaking the same rune again before he’s resealed it will not recast the spell - the spell’s power is gone.

He may cast a spell multiple times per day but he’ll need to bind each instance of the spell to a different rune

Speaking a rune in this way doesn’t affect the Runegalder and so he gains no Stress by casting like other magic users.

He may also unseal a rune without actually casting it but he not able to immediately reseal a new rune right after.

GRIMOIRE This is the Runegalder’s spell book and keeps the spells that he binds to runes. Unlike a normal spell book, however, the spells are scribed in meteorite ink and blood that seals their magic power in the text itself. Essentially, the Runegalder’s Grimoire does the channeling latently for him.

This Grimoire starts with 3 random spells of 1st Degree and gains an additional spell every other level. New spell’s have a Degree equal to the Runegalder’s level divided by 3, rounded up.

A spell has a number of SP equal to 10 x its Degree that are invested in it when it is scribed into the Grimoire. These SP cannot be redistributed and changed: spells in the Grimoire are immutable.

So, a Runegalder scribes Disorient as a 2nd Degree spell. He gains 20 SP. He chooses to make it a Sphere spell (3 SP), to increase its Duration up to 5 Seconds (16 SP), and increase the Ability Check by 1 (1 SP). Now, whenever he speaks a rune seal to that spell, he casts it with an Area of Effect of a Sphere, a 5 second Duration, and +1 to his Ability Check roll.

Spells inscribed at different Degrees count as completely different spells and must be sealed to different runes. So if the Runegalder has a 1st Degree Flash Grenade and a 5th Degree Flash Grenade, he would need two runes to cast both. He couldn’t simply bind “Flash Grenade” to one rune and then choose which he wanted to use in the moment.

SKILL FOCUS (Arcana, 1) Through the course of her career, a Runegalder seeks to further their knowledge of the occult. They receive 1 free purchase of the Arcana Advanced Skill at every other level up.

PRIMARY BOON

SERVANT Grimoire is increased to every level or Skill Focus is increased to every level

WARRIOR Vancian Casting is increased to every other level

The Runegalder uses his body as a prison for latent magical energies which wears on a person over time. He ages at twice the normal rate.

Additionally, the Runegalder's magic is 'dead' and so is less potent than other types of magic, thus allowing some to shrug off their spells.

When casting a spell, the Runegalder rolls a d20! + 10 + their level. All those that would be affected by the spell roll a d20! + their level. If their total is above the Runegalder's, they're unaffected by the his magic. This roll is made regardless of whether or not the person wants to be affected by the spell.

AGAINST THEM ALL When they’re in the thick of it is when the Vanguard thrives. The Vanguard gains their Honor Dice to their Attack rolls against 1 opponent when engaged with 2 or more enemies.

IN & OUT & AROUND Knowing how to slip and out of your opponent’s guards allow the Vanguard to get in and out of a jam quickly.

Once at first level and an additional time for every 2 levels they’ve obtained per combat, the Vanguard may change their engagement zone with one opponent, without any associated penalties.

So, if they’re Threatening their opponent, they could move to Engaged or they could move themselves out to Not Engaged without giving their enemy that free attack.

Additionally, if their enemy has successfully employed the In Their Guard Tactic, the Vanguard can move them back out to simply Engaged.

CROWD CONTROL Surrounded by enemies, the Vanguard learns to use the crowd against themselves, forcing his enemies to get in each other’s way.

A Vanguard can use his full defense against an additional opponent if the two opponents are adjacent to each other

FIRST STRIKE The Vanguard hits hard and hits first. Or at least, they try to.

At the beginning of combat, Vanguards recieve a pool of Measure equal to 1 ft + 1 per every 2 levels they’ve obtained. When they first become Engaged with an opponent, they may spend ft from that pool to temporarily increase their Measure to determine who strikes first.

So, a level 6 Vanguard would have a pool of 4 ft to work with. If wielding a Stiletto (Measure 1.5 ft) and closing in to Engage with an enemy wielding a Rapier (Measure 4 ft), he could spend 3 ft of it to allow him to get his hit off first, perhaps crippling his enemy before they have change to respond. Doing so would leave him with 1 ft in his pool for the rest of combat.

After that, combat resumes as normal, so he wouldn’t be able to attack the Rapier wielder until he got In Their Guard.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT In & Out & Around is increased to every level

MAGICAL In & Out & Around is increased to every level

SERVANT In & Out & Around is increased to every level or First Strike is increased to every level

WARRIOR In & Out & Around is increased to every level and First Strike is increased to every level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

Vanguards are a position of Honor and they don’t take charging into battle lightly. A Vanguard loses 1 Honor for every battle they’re not the first on either side to land a melee attack.
