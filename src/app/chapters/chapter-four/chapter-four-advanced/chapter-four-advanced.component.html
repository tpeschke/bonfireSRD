Archetypes represent your character’s background training and starting skills. Your character’s class is a gestalt of two Archetypes of your choosing.

Archetypes are divided into Archetype Categories: Expert, Magical, Servant, Socialite, and Warrior. These represent the overall theme of the individual Archetype. For instance, Warrior Archetypes tend to be good at things related to combat. So, if you’re looking to make a combat focused character, you can look there.

You choose a Primary and a Secondary Archetype and pay the price in CrP for both based on your race.

You use the Vitality Die, the Skills & Masteries, and the Primary Drawback sections from the Primary, and combine both Archetype Abilities section.

Each Archetype also has a Primary Boon, which modifies the Archetype Abilities of your Primary and is based on the Secondary's Archetype category.

While you may want to double down into the exact same Archetype but Magical Archetypes are the only ones that you can do that with. You may choose two Archetypes from the same Category, however.

Your GM may choose to reject a Class based on the fact that the two Archetypes don’t fit together thematically. So, while a Thief/Priest (Beast Covenant) might be really powerful because of the Beast Covenant’s Shape Changing ability, if there’s not a Patron of Thieves and Animals in the campaign world, you’re out of luck.

Expert Archetypes are those that focus on real-world non-combat skills, as opposed to relying on the Divines or magic as a source of strength.

Expert Archetypes tend to rely on Intellect and Wisdom, primarily.

MAGE

🜂 Advanced Rule

The mage is one that allows himself to be buffeted by the astral winds and so channels great power but also is in great danger. The mage has the most raw power of the Magical Archetypes but it could backfire on him.

VITALITY DIE

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

WILD MAGIC CASTING The Mage is able to channel the power of the stars, harnessing it and creating magical energy. While doing so the caster must stand still and he glows with light, being illuminated by a column that reaches above them. This light is equivalent to a candle’s brightness.

After 7 seconds, the Mage gains their Spell Points. Unlike other Archetypes of this type, however, it is not a single pool. Instead, the Mage rolls a d3!-1 SP for each of the following: the power of the Positive effects and the Negative effects of the spell, and Area of Effect, the Range, and the Duration. A 0 on a roll could result in the spell fizzling out. Additionally, they get a d3!-1 worth of SP that are “free” and can be used to buff or shrink any category.

So, for example, a Mage finishes channelling and casts Flashbang. He rolls d3!-1 and gets a 1 for Positive effects, so X for the Positive effects would be replaced by 1. He rolls another d3!-1 and gets a 4 ( from exploding ) and so X for the Negative effects are replaced by 4. He rolls another time and gets a 0 for Area of Effect, so it's Full, another for Range, getting a 3 (which doesn’t give him enough to move it), and another time for Duration and gets a 0.

The Mage finally rolls their free dice and gets a 3. Since Duration is 0, that would mean the spell would fizzle out, so he invests 1 point there and the remaining 2 to buy down the Negative effects. Note that even though he has 3 SP in Range, which he can’t use because it isn’t enough to move the spell, he can’t redistribute those points to other parts of the spell: they’re considered lost.

Should the caster stop channelling but still have SPs they haven’t spent, they begin to lose them at a rate of 1 SP per 6 seconds.

Speaking the rune releases it from it’s binding so speaking the same rune again before he’s resealed it will not recast the spell - the spell’s power is gone.

He may cast a spell multiple times per day but he’ll need to bind each instance of the spell to a different rune.

Speaking a rune in this way doesn’t affect the Runegalder and so he ignores Magical Fallout to himself.

He may also unseal a rune without actually casting it but he is not able to immediately reseal a new rune right after.

GRIMOIRE This is the Runegalder’s spell book and keeps the spells that he binds to runes. Unlike a normal spell book, however, the spells are scribed in meteorite ink and blood that seals their magic power in the text itself. Essentially, the Runegalder’s Grimoire does the channeling latently for him.

This Grimoire starts with 3 random spells and gains an additional spell every level. New spell’s are equal to the Runegalder’s level.

A spell has a number of SP equal to 5 + their level (so at 1st level, you would get three that each have 6 SP) that are invested in it when it is scribed into the Grimoire. These SP cannot be redistributed and changed: spells in the Grimoire are immutable.

So, a 2nd level Runegalder scribes Disorient. He gains 7 SP. He chooses to make it a Sphere spell (3 SP), increase the Magnitude to 2 and buy down the Negative effect 2 times. Now, whenever he speaks a rune sealed to that spell, he casts it with an Area of Effect of a Sphere, with a Magnitude of 2, and no Negative effects to him.

Spells inscribed at different levels count as completely different spells and must be sealed to different runes. So if the Runegalder has a 6 SP Flash Grenade and a 11 SP Flash Grenade, he would need two runes to cast both. He couldn’t simply bind “Flash Grenade” to one rune and then choose which he wanted to use in the moment.

SKILL FOCUS (Arcana, 1) Through the course of her career, a Runegalder seeks to further their knowledge of the occult. They receive 1 free purchase of the Arcana Advanced Skill at every other level up.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

DIVINE COVENANT During Character Creation, a Priest selects 1 Covenant and 1 Cosmic Link, representing their diety’s sphere of influence and personality, respectively.

Covenants change which Miracles the Priest has access to while Cosmic Links change the ways you gain and spend Favor.

SHEPHERD OF THE PEOPLE If some else prayers for a Miracle within site of the Priest, the Priest can spend his own Favor to increase the effective cost of that Miracle. FAVORED OF THE DIVINES The Priest’s mission isn’t a selfish one, but it does have some benefits and they’re loved for their devotion to their Divines beyond others. They add 1/2 their level (rounded down) to their Max Favor.

CATECHISM By studying the holy texts, the Priest is about to discern the will of the Divines and better understand the Higher Realms. They receive a free purchase of the Religion (Divines) Advanced Skill at every other level up.

A Priest has sworn themselves to a Higher Power and as a token of their Covenant, they must select a taboo; an action or thing they must never do, such as cut their hair or eat meat. This must be appropriate to the Divine and be approved by the GM before play begins.

SHAMAN

🜂 Advanced Rule

A Shaman is one who’s invested their second soul into a Bonfire, thus they carry a part of the flame with them where they go. Because of this, others can link into their power and stand on hallowed ground where ever they go.

VITALITY DIE

d8

SKILLS & MASTERIES

They have been to the womb of creation and back, they gain one free purchase of the Lore Skill Suite.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

LINKED TO THE BONFIRE The Shaman has the soul of the Bonfire and so is a generator of holy power that flows from them. They increase their Max Favor by 1/2 their level.

They’re considered part of the Nature, Fire, Fate, and Honor Covenant.

MANTLE of FIRE The Shaman’s power projects out from him so that call can feel it. This is an aura of light that starts out projecting 5 ft form him and is increased by 5 ft for every 5 levels they’ve obtained.

He may temporarily increase the radius using Favor, with 1 Favor expanding it by 5 ft though this only lasts 15 seconds.

BURNING GROUND Wherever a Shaman walks is holy ground and those that have no respect for him, find themselves on shaky footing. Enemies within the Shaman’s Mantle of Fire reduce their Knock-Back Threshold by 5. This is increased by 5 for every 5 levels the Shaman has obtained.

WELCOMING HEARTH Those close to the Shaman, both physically and emotionally, find that they too are gifted with the gift of fire. Any friendly person standing with the Shaman’s Mantle of Fire also gains the benefits of any Miracle currently affecting the Shaman but only when they stand within the Mantle.

SACRIFICE of SOUL The Shaman can remove their mortal coil to manifest their true nature but doing so may kill them. For every 2 Vitality the Shaman sacrifices, they gain 1 temporary special Favor. This Favor doesn’t count towards your Current Favor and only the Cost when invoking a Miracle. All damage dealt this way goes on one Wound and the Favor is wasted if not used before the next sun rise.

The Shaman may also use this extra Favor to expand his Mantle of Fire.

WARMING SOUL The Shaman warms the souls of others like the fire warms the body. Those around the Shaman heal an additional Stress after a day of travel together. This is increased by 1 for every 3 levels the Shaman’s obtained.

PRIMARY BOON

EXPERT Linked to the Bonfire becomes + level and Warm Soul is increased to every other level

MAGICAL Linked to the Bonfire becomes + level and Warm Soul is increased to every other level

SERVANT Shamen can’t select another Servant Archetype as their Secondary.

SOCIALITE Linked to the Bonfire becomes + level and Warm Soul is increased to every other level

WARRIOR Linked to the Bonfire becomes + level and Warm Soul is increased to every other level

PRIMARY DRAWBACK

The Shaman literally glows with inner light, making Hiding and Camouflage checks 3 Difficulties harder.

Additionally, when they invoke a Miracle, the Bonfire swells within them, making 10 ft around them Bright Illumination. The Shaman is penalized as well if they don’t close their eyes, though there’s no permanent damage.

He gains Favor equal to his Honor Category + 1/4 his level. Unlike other Servants, he can’t gain additional Favor through the normal means (sacrifices and holy days): this Favor is static and only increases with level.

MARKS of the EARTH Through ceremony and ritual, the Symbolist is able to invoke Miracles that are normally reserved only for the Divines. Given an hour, the Symbolist can endow a mark on him or another person. During the ceremony, he selects a certain number of his Favor and “locks” into the mark.

The Favor is “locked” and can't be used for other marks and Miracles until released.

Should the Symbolist die, all his marks are released. Also, should the Symbolist’s Favor drop below their currently locked Favor (from lowering an Honor Category, for example) they’re forced to unlock one of their marks and dispel a Miracle.

LAW of AVERAGES The Symbolist is a pillar of certainty in a sea of chaos. Once at first level and an additional time for every 3 levels he’s obtained, the Symbolist lowers or raises a die roll 1 point towards the average. This bonus is also increased by 1 for every 3 levels they’ve obtained and can negate perfect attacks and defenses, as well as going above the average.

CONDUIT OF REALITY The Symbolist can invoke Miracles from the following Covenants: Dream, Guardians, Adversity, Battle, Beauty, Fate, Fire, Home, Honor. He is unable to invoke any Miracles outside of these Covenants, even for the normal price.

WARRIOR Law of Averages is increased to every other level and Honor Category + 1/2 level

By anchoring themselves to the Bones of Ymir, the Symbolist cuts off his connection to the God’s Realm in a way. Invocation Die are 3 sizes larger.

The Philosopher reserves his strength, allowing his opponent in a Conflict to escalate for him. While others suffer strain from not meeting the argument straight on, the Philosopher uses it to his advantage.

VITALITY DIE

d4

SKILLS & MASTERIES

Philosophers receive 1 free purchase of the Lore Skill Suite.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

MATCH-POINT In defeat, a Philosopher gains ground. At the end of the round, should the Philosopher have lost that round, for each time a dice that the Philosopher put up that matches the result of a dice that their opponent put up, they gain d4! Leverage in the next round.

RHETORIC By knowing the fundamentals of how to form an argument, a Philosopher can sometimes gain something in the long run by conceding a point. Once per round at first level and an additional time for every 3 levels he’s obtained, a Philosopher can flip a dice over one side, to a lower number. So, they can move a 7 on a d10 to 5, 3, 2, but not 10, or a 6 on a d12 to a 2 or 3 but not up to 8, 9, or 12.

STOIC Philosophers aren’t interested in winning a Conflict by simply destroying their opponent’s points: they’re interested in helping both get to the truth of the matter. As such, they have less pride invested in the Conflict; always gaining Advantage in the case of a tied result.

Additionally, they can sacrifice 1 of the Leverage dice gained through their Rhetoric to steal Advantage for free.

CLOSING REMARKS Regardless of whether they want to hear it or not, the Philosopher knows how to make you listen. If they have an argument left over, even if they don’t have Advantage, they get Last Words.

PRACTICE DEBATE Even if you don’t get what you want, you can often get practice for next time. Whenever a Philosopher loses a Social Conflict, they gain 1 use of their Honor Die in their next Conflict. This increases by 1 use for every other level they obtain, however, they can’t use more than 1 per round.

EXPERT Rhetoric increases to every level

WARRIOR Rhetoric increases to every other level

The Philosopher never tries to over extend himself. He gains 1 Stress whenever he he beats his opponent's total in a round.

Additionally, there is a price to pay for driving your body for so long; when you drop out of the Frenzy, you take enough damage, on one Wound, to drop you another Vitality Category. You also gain no benefits from Taking a Breather after doing so until you drop another Vitality Category (so you can't just heal it back up, sorry).

This Frenzy is awoken by the Berserker and takes 3d6 seconds to get into, during which time the Berserker beats his chest, screams at the sky, and just generally psyches himself up. The more wounded the Berserker becomes, however, the easier it is to get into the right frame of mind, so remove a d6 for every Vitality Category he has dropped.

Failure indicates they keep attacking.

SKILLS & MASTERIES

The Man-at-Arms receive 1 free purchase of the Athletics Skill Suite. They also receive 3 free Weapon Basic Training Masteries.

ARCHETYPE ABILITIES

KNOW YOUR ENEMY A Man-at-Arms is good at using watching her foe and exploiting weaknesses. For every 20 seconds she is either Threatening someone or someone is Threatening her, she gains a cumulative bonus to Attack, Defense, Speed, or Damage. This bonus, and what she can choose to apply it to is based on her Advanced Weapon Basic Training.

Tier 4 gives her a +1, Tier 3 gives her a +2, and Tier 2 and under give her a +3. She can only apply this bonus to a metric she has an Advanced Training Bonus in. So, for example, if she is fighting someone with a slashing sword and she has a +1 to Attack and Defense with all Slashing weapons (Tier 3), that means, for every 20 seconds she is fighting that person, she can add an additional +2 to her Attack or Defense.

This bonus is applied to one metric per person and everything reset should that foe become completely unengaged wit her. So, if the Man-At-Arms is Threatening two foes for 20 seconds, she can apply her bonus to attacking one, while the other bonus goes to defending against another. If one she is defending against leaves, she has to continue applying her bonus to attacking the other. Should that foe threaten her again, she gains no bonuses again him but can change to attacking him as well.

The time it takes to accure this bonus is lowered 1 second for other level the Men-at-Arms obtains, down to a minimum 1 second.

COUNTING COUP Part of being a Man-at-Arms is keeping a reputation for your skill in combat. If you give yourself a penalty to Attack, you gain 1 Honor and heal an amount of Stress equal to that penalty if you score a hit.

MARTIAL TRAINING A Man-at-Arms receives his choice of a free Tier 3 or 4 Armor, Shield, or Weapon Advanced Training for every 2 levels he's obtained.

IMPROVED ADVANCED TRAINING A Man-at-Arms studies the greatest weapon, armor, and shield techniques to pick them up easier. For them, Tier 2 Weapon, Shield, Armor Advanced Training has a Base Cost of 7, Tier 3 has a Base Cost of 8, and Tier 4 a Base Cost of 10.

So, a level 6 Vanguard would have a pool of 4 ft to work with.
