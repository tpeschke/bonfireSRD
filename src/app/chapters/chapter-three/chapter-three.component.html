A BRIEF OVERVIEW OF THE HISTORY OF THE MIDDLE REALM

In the beginning, the Divines created the Painted World, in which they placed their first children. The Painted World was mutable and the source of all magic and there the First Children lived happily, beautiful, skilled, and perfect in everyway.

They were the delight of all the Divines, save one: the trickster Divine, Rostam. Rostam quickly grew bored of the First Children and the constant fawning they received from the Divines.

And so, under the guise of creating servants for the Divines, he created the Dwarves. He made the opposite of the First Children - crude, mean, ugly, and incorrigible in the extreme. They caused such an uproar in the Higher Realm that the Divines banished them to the Nothing beyond the Gate of Heaven, where certain death awaited them in the embrace of non-existent nothing.

The Father of the Dwarves took his people and before passing through the Gate of Heaven, slew the giant Ymir, who stood guard there and tossed his body into the Nothing. The All-Father and his sons jumped from the Gate and onto Ymir and from his bones they made the Middle Realm; the giant‚Äôs blood, the oceans and his muscles, the earth.

There they lived a profane life, in defiance of the Divines. With the protection of the Bones of Ymir, the dwarves were able to avoid the direct displeasure of the Divines and so the Divines needed to think of different ways to reach them in their holds.

They created the Bonfire, links to the Higher Realm, through which they sent the unthinking evils of the world through but the dwarves repulsed them.

And so they created their Second Children, avenging angels robed in flesh, to travel through the Bonfires and war with the dwarves.

But after centuries of war, the Second Children changed: they became obsessed with the total destruction of the dwarves and so they chained themselves to the Bonfires, harnessing the Divine against its wills. They became the orcs.

Appalled at what their Children had become, the Divines sent their First Children to bring the orcs back into the fold, but the orcs rebelled, sacrificing the some of the First Children on the Bonfires and thus was born the human race.

Centuries have passed and the Middle Realm changes those that walk there. The First Children eventually called it home and became the elves.

WHICH RACE SHOULD I CHOOSE?

Your race effects character creation in 3 ways: Ability Score Modifiers, Gameplay Rewards, and Archetype Costs. If you're new to RPGs in generally, go for a race that interests you based on the flavor.

ABILITY SCORE MODIFIERS

These modify the base stats that you roll. You can use it to balance out your character's weak spots or you can use it to maximize what your character is already good it. We recommend the latter as Bonfire is a game built around teamwork: you'll have friends who will be good at things your character isn't.

GAMEPLAY REWARDS

Each race is meant to encourage a certain type of play. For instance, orcs gain bonuses for starting things, like combats or social conflicts, so they reward players who are aggressive, while elves get rewarded for being careful with how they engage with others. These are noted under the Racial Temperament section in their summary.

ARCHETYPE COSTS

Some races can spend less time mastering things than others, freeing up time (CrP) to focus on other pursuits or becoming true masters of their vocation. This is reflected in the Archetype Costs.

You'll notice that some races will have Archetype Exceptions: these are based on the lore of that race for the Middle Realm, the official campaign setting of Bonfire. GMs should feel free to modify these prices. Actually, GMs should modify any prices to fit their setting.

The Elves are the first born of the Divines. Originally, the inhabitants of the Painted World, the source of all magic, they now inhabit the Middle Realm as mentally ethereal beings.

HISTORY

After the corruption of the Orcs, the Divines sought another means of assailing the Dwarves in their holds. They turned to the First Children, the Elves.

So, they marshaled the elven forces and invaded the Middle Realm.

The invasion proved more fruitful than the first one. The Elves‚Äô ethereal nature allowed them to interact with the Middle Realm without being corrupted by it and their corporeal bodies allowed them to wage war on the Dwarves on their own terms.

But there were side effects of their new environment. The Elves, who used to be nearest to the Divines, found that they were now less connected to their creators. With all that reality in the way, their prayers had a harder time of reaching the Divines.

After generations, the Elves have become dispersed, their common cause mostly forgotten and their heritage lost to the mists of time. Some continue the war, but most of them have integrated into the societies of the Middle Realm as artisans, scholars, and wizards.

APPEARANCE

Elves are slender beings that are widely considered classically beautiful, though many find their features alien and off putting. They uniformly have long ears that taper to a point at the end.

Their eyes are pure white, with no iris or pupil visible, this matched with their sharp teeth, can give them a feline turn to their face.

Elves naturally survive off the ebb and flow of magic that drifts through the air of the Painted World. While still present in the Middle Realm, these winds are a sliver of their natural magnitude and so Elves need to find other sources; the main one being through the magic inherent in all living creatures. While vegetables do have magic in them, an Elf would have to consume 100s of pounds of lettuce each day to not die of starvation. The only realistic way of getting enough magic to survive is through the consumption of raw meat.

Humans are the first natives of the Middle Realm. They are creatures born from strife, who‚Äôve carved for themselves a place in the world through fire, steel, and grit.

HISTORY

The first Humans were born from the embers and ash of the Bonfires during the Elf-Orc Wars. Orc warlords discovered they could strengthen and corrupt the Bonfires by burning Elves alive in the its flame.

But out of the remains rose new life: a creature of both heaven and earth.

These children of the Middle Realm survived by forming clans in the hills and mountains, away from the fighting, and there they began to thrive.

The turning point in their civilization was when the Divines discovered their unique connection with the Humans. Each Human carried a Bonfire inside them, making them perfect vessels for the favor of the gods. The Divines began focusing their attention on them and bestowing their gifts on their adopted children.

Humans have now become the preeminent race in the Middle Realm, with kingdoms and empires across the world.

Avenging angels who know how to make war like others know how to breathe. The Orcs have been corrupted by the Middle Realm, now forging their destiny with their own hands.

HISTORY

Confounded by the Dwarves, who used the corpse of Ymir to form the Middle Realm and shield themselves from the power of the Divines, the Divines began to formulate an invasion to massacre the Stone Folk.

They crafted corporeal angels to wage war in the Middle Realm and they created the Bonfires as heavenly invasion points.

Centuries passed and the Orcs changed. Ages of violence transformed them and they became narrow minded in their pursuit of the total destruction of the dwarvish race.

It was discovered that Orcish warlords could link themselves to the Bonfires, wresting power from the Divines against their will, and so the gods turned their backs on their wayward children.

But the wars continued and the Orcs used the link to elevate their own ancestors to a faux deity state, replacing the Divines.

The Orcs, since blinded by war, have been crushed and dispersed, their descendants found as mercenaries and bandits across the Middle Realm, with a few Orcish Warlords still offering resistance from their ancient citadels.

APPEARANCE

Orcs have dark green or grey skin with porcine nose and tusks that jut up from their lower jaw. They tend to be stocky, with thick muscles and hunched shoulders.

Their hair is wiry, closer to steel wool than anything else and their eyes are completely black, with no pupils or irises, giving them an indescribable inhumanness to it.

Naturally, Orcs are scavengers, able to survive on practically anything, no matter how rotten or foul.
