Which Race Should I Choose?

Your race effects character creation in 3 ways: Stat Score Modifiers, Gameplay Rewards, and Archetype Costs. If you're new to RPGs in generally, go for a race that interests you based on the flavor.

STAT SCORE MODIFIERS

These modify the base stats that you roll. You can use it to balance out your character's weak spots or you can use it to maximize what your character is already good it. We recommend the latter as Bonfire is a game built around teamwork: you'll have friends who will be good at things your character isn't.

GAMEPLAY REWARDS

Each race is meant to encourage a certain type of play. For instance, orcs gain bonuses for starting things, like combats or Confrontations, so they reward players who are aggressive, while elves get rewarded for being careful with how they engage with others. These are noted under the Racial Temperament section in their summary.

ARCHETYPE COSTS

Some races can spend less time mastering things than others, freeing up time (CrP) to focus on other pursuits or becoming true masters of their vocation. This is reflected in the Archetype Costs.

For having stolen weirdsecrets from the covetous Alwealda, we and our children are cursed. We bear it happily for the price is worthy of the reward. Let others look skyward; we shall look ahead.

Changelings are strange little beings. The rumors are that their demons who've trade places with human children left out in the wild or that they're the stillborn children of wizards and witches. Either way, their wiliness and technical prowess make them welcome guests where every they end up.

APPEARANCE

Changelings are about the size of a 10-year-old human, with extremely pale skin, unusually long fingers and large eyes. Besides this, they appear like little humans, with wispy hair on their head and face.

They dress in whatever the area custom is but tend towards bright and clashing colors.

PERSONALITY

Changelings are wily and crafty, and, while not lacking in morality, tend to get caught up on whatever project they're currently on. They enjoy puzzles more than anything - the thrill of having everything click into place just perfectly is the finest thing a person can experience.

They also tend to be on the shy side, as much as possible, keeping out of the limelight. Worldly power and accolades hold little appeal for them, but there is a sense of wanting to be apart something greater than keeps them reaching out to the outside world.

Why does it matter where we come from? Sod off.

Dwarves are called the 'Stonemen' not because they're made of stone but because they're hard as rock. They're obstinate and incorrigible and, as a rule, they never do anything that someone else wants them to do. As such, they're the most disagreeable beings in the Middle Realm. They're also the most straightforward, brave, and loyal beings as well.

APPEARANCE

Dwarves are the opposite of elves. They're short, barrel chested with thick hair growing all over their bodies. This hair ranges from coal black to a golden hue, depending on which hold the dwarf hails from. They also always support big bushy beards that are near hard as steel.

These beards are their pride and joy and they take great care in them. Woven into each beard is the tale of that dwarf's life and accomplishments as well as their job within the hold. You can learn a lot from reading a dwarf's beard.

Besides this and their tools, the dwarves really don't take care of themselves, eschewing anything showy or fancy, like nice fabrics or baths.

Dwarf anatomy is, thankfully, a mystery to most outsiders. All dwarves, to other races, appear human and use human pronouns, though in dwarvish, there are no pronouns because that's more bothersome. There is a rumor that there are female dwarves but that there's 0 sexual dimorphism in the species, which makes courting a very careful affair, but the running theory is that female dwarves are just anvils.

PERSONALITY

As stated before, dwarves are stubborn. They're very rarely, however, psychopathically so. More often, once they have decided something, they tend to get defensive and refuse to relent unless they absolutely have to.

This makes dwarven governments and social structures very slow moving and difficult to work with but, it also means that if you get a dwarf to agree with you, they will happily die on that hill.

In fact, the only time that a dwarf seems to be happy is when he's being stubborn about something.

Finally, dwarves do not ascribe to social niceties. It's not that they're incapable to understanding them or aware of them: they just don't think they're worth paying attention to, in the same way that most people don't notice insects.

We who were trapped in prisons of flesh look to our forebears. For as the Alwealda ascended to return to the Divines, we, their children, will also ascend when we too have perfected ourselves in their sight.

The Elves are the first born of the Divines. Originally, the inhabitants of the Painted World, the source of all magic, they now inhabit the Middle Realm as mentally ethereal beings.

APPEARANCE

Elves are slender beings that are widely considered classically beautiful, though many find their features alien and off putting. They uniformly have long ears that taper to a point at the end.

Their eyes are pure white, with no iris or pupil visible, this matched with their sharp teeth, can give them a feline turn to their face.

Elves naturally survive off the ebb and flow of magic that drifts through the air of the Painted World. While still present in the Middle Realm, these winds are a sliver of their natural magnitude and so Elves need to find other sources; the main one being through the magic inherent in all living creatures. While vegetables do have magic in them, an Elf would have to consume 100s of pounds of lettuce each day to not die of starvation. The only realistic way of getting enough magic to survive is through the consumption of raw meat.

PERSONALITY

To describe an Elf as “careful” would be a huge understatement. They tend to live longer than other races, not because some supernaturally long lifespan, but because they're always careful about their actions. They see other races as brash and impetuous while they're in turn seen as slow and cowardly.

It's not they're incapable of great emotion or quick action but elves seem to understand that with every action comes a consequence, whether that action was well meaning or not, and that this karmic force is more powerful than any other law or power.

However, once set on a course, it is hard to dissuade an elf, for they do nothing lightly.

For what shall it profit a man, if he shall gain the whole world, and lose his own soul? It is not enough for a man to ask what he would die for, but he must ask himself, what would he live for?

Gaunts are said to be depraved creatures stuck between life and death. They love filthy lucre more than what made them human. They're those that, for whatever reason, have sold part of their souls for either money or at a change at eternal life in the world to come.

APPEARANCE

Gaunts of all races look generally the same as the soul shiving process drains them of their vitality. Their skin turns ghost white, their hair a weak grey, and their eyes become milky as if they're blind. Their bodies becoming desiccated, thin and reedy.

They still need to eat, drink, and do other bodily functions - they are, even after all that, living.

Gaunts tend to dress in flamboyantclothing that is a mix and match of whatever they kind find. Most say this is because they loot the clothing off of those they kill but it's more likely because they just wear what they can find or because they wish to make up for their dull color palette

PERSONALITY

As stated, gaunts get a bad rep for being whollywithout morals but that's not always the case. Some have undergone the process because they need to feed their families or out of a powerful sense of devotion or religious zeal so actually gaunts run the spectrum of personality types: they're just saddled with their reputation because of an admittedly not small section of them.

What makes up the gaunt's personality is why he made the sacrifice to do what he did. Why was so precious to him that he would make such a huge sacrifice for? That's a complicated question and not easy to answer by the gaunt player should have their answer before play begins.

Born again and with a New Covenant, we forsake the one that made us to seek after greater things. Created for evil, we now seek in the ruins of the Alwealda to bring back their Golden Age; to repent for what we have done.

Beastmen are uncivilized creatures of an untamed nature but not Minotaurs. Through the Bonfire they've formed a New Covenant with the Divines, remaking their minds in their image. They were the founders of the First Empire and their legacy is still cleaved to by the kings of the age.

APPEARANCE

Minotaurs are huge creatures that are half man and half bull. Specifically, the head and upper torse, as well as the legs are bull, while the chest, shoulders, and arms are those of a man.

Both male and female are larger than the average person, with males standing on average about 7 ft tall and the woman standing at around 6 ft tall. Males sprout horns, though they lack the strength to effectively gore with them like their bull counterparts.

Their skin color, as well as their fur color and pattern, vary by region, with traditional tanner skinned minotaurs coming from the south and light skinned ones coming from the north but during the days of the First Empire there was so much population movement that this is a trend rather than a rule.

Note that Minotaurs are more closely related to Beastfolk than they are to cows and so are, in fact, carnivorous.

PERSONALITY

Minotaurs cut royal figures and their personalities match. They have an extreme sense of pride and a great depth of feeling in accordance with their bestial nature.

They're extremely devoted to whatever cause they throw their weight behind and there is no difference to them between their opinions and themselves, making them very cocksure. This can, unsurprisingly, make them bullies as they care little for other's opinions about things.

Days of ashes and nights of fire; we were great warriors then. But our swords and shields were stolen from us and now we, the nomads, search for things that no hand can hold. Where must we go? We who search the wasteland for our better selves?

Heirs to a great empire long since lost to the ages, the orcs cling to tradition in the wake of uncertain and dark times. Some say that they value only strength but to truly known an orc is to know the soul of honor.

APPEARANCE

Orcs have dark green or grey skin with porcine nose and tusks that jut up from their lower jaw. They tend to be stocky, with thick muscles and hunched shoulders.

Their hair is wiry, closer to steel wool than anything else and their eyes are completely black, with no pupils or irises, giving them an indescribable inhumanness to it.

Naturally, orcs are scavengers, able to survive on practically anything, no matter how rotten or foul.

PERSONALITY

An orc’s life is defined by struggle: from birth to death, they conceptualize the universe as having a vendetta against them. This can often give them a martyr complex and make them morose traveling companions at times because they feel the weight of the world.

They can also come across as aggressive, which some have pointed out is a defense mechanism, but orcs would simply scoff at that.

There’s nothing an orc values more than their honor and the chance to prove themselves against a worthy foe. This is often seen in their deities across orcish cultures, which are almost exclusively pantheons of their greatest foes. It is taken for granted that you are only as good as your enemies.

What a broken and fractious world; what a fallen state to toil in. Is it not enough to suffer the cruelty of fate that we should inflict wounds on one another? Consign yourself to oblivion or pick up your brother and fight for the hope of starlight in the infinite void.

Ratfolk are social creatures. They love people and being around people. Others find them pleasant to be around. As such, Ratfolk can be found as diplomats, traders, and minstrels .

APPEARANCE

Ratfolk are smaller and slimmer than other races, with lithe, fur covered bodies that range from auburn to dark black. They have beady eyes and long tails. For the most part, they appear like their smaller cousins, though with lighter frames and opposable thumbs.

PERSONALITY

As stated, ratfolk are people people. They're extremely social and, as a rule, are extroverted. They tend to be agreeable and try to get along with everyone but this shouldn't be taken as people pleasing or weak because ratfolk are clever - dangerously so.

People tend to think that because ratfolk are so easy going that they're push overs and that they can be taken advantage of but ratfolk sees it as "I'll scratch your back, you scratch mine" kind of deal so they're not just going to do things to do things. They very much believe in the social contract, but their animal nature makes them ruthless as some points.

In fact, ratfolk can be quite ruthless to those they deem outside their pack, which has earned them some reputation as having a herd mentality. For ratfolk the only war is total war.
