Every person have flaws; little quirks of their personality or odd behaviors. These flaws are often a way of dealing with troubles in their life, letting off Stress or rationalizing their behavior to others. Other times they’re artifacts of a hard life, like a soldier whose missing an arm.

Benefits & Penalties

Flaws in Bonfire are a tradeoff: they’ll make your character less able to do things in certain arenas or harder to be around but allow you to invest in abilities and powers elsewhere. This is represented by the CrP bonus that you get from taking Flaws

Another bonus is that by overcoming a character's Flaws, a person gains confidence and Honor. Essentially, this means by applying your Flaws to your detriment, your character becomes stronger.

This is done very simply by saying that you think a Flaw would make something harder for your character in this situation and then giving yourself a -d4! to your Skill, Attack, or Defense roll or giving your opponent d4! Leverage. Regardless of whether or not you succeed, they gain Honor equal to your penalty.

The GM may award more Honor or inflict you with a larger penalty as seems appropriate for the situation. After all, if it's something your character really struggles with and it gives them a -d8!, it makes sense that they would get an equivalent amount of Honor for overcoming the challenge.

So, note that if you're the one to bring up your Flaws, you get Honor regardless but if the GM has to do so, you only get Honor if you succeed so it is always better to bring them up yourself (unless you have a very inattentive GM).

CHERRY PICKING

For the roleplayers among us with a specific character idea in mind, you may Cherry Pick your Flaws.

Each Flaw loses 10 CrP of its value per Flaw to a minimum of 0 CrP received. So, the 3rd Flaw you pick will be at a -30 CrP.

While most Flaws can be reasonably looked up, some might need some clarification which is why they're listed below or additional penalties. We've also provided some handy tables you might find useful for figuring out the details of that Flaw.

ATHEIST

The way of the divine is a mysterious path and someone mistake it for no path at all. Some refuse to believe that intelligent higher powers exist and so forfeit some of the blessings while others find worshipping such beings laughable, like giving reverence to the biggest bully on the block.

BLIND, TOTAL

This character has completely lost his sight. He sees only pure white or pure black, unable to sense any movement around him.

In melee combat, they defend and attack with a 1d6! And ranged combat is right out, however, if you insist, you’ll be attacking and defending with a 1d4, so enjoy your fumbles, you stubborn bastard.

Additionally, any Skill Challenges involving sight halves the person’s Skill Rank.

BOAR FACED

This is the polite way of say that someone is ugly as sin. This could be the result of parentage or a bad accident, either way, people don’t like being around them or dealing with them. As mean as it might be, people judge books by their covers.

DARK SECRET

There’s something that this character is hiding and she’s worried that she’ll be found out. She’ll jealously guard every aspect of her life if she can for fear of being found out.

DEBTOR

This character has gotten into trouble financially. This character starts with 5d20 + 150 sc of debt instead of gaining a like amount to start off with. All their equipment much then be purchased on a credit of 50 sc that is also taken out of their debt. If they don’t make payments on it, they may find themselves on the bad end of the law or the loan shark they borrowed from.

DERANGED

There’s holes in this character’s brains and he’s starting to slip into madness. The nature of this neurosis is up to the character.

The character doesn’t suffer the effects of Stress until it matches his Wisdom score, at that point, he has an episode and doubles his total Stress. Once, he’s recovered (dropped his Stress back down below his Wisdom), he suffers a permanent reduction 1 to both his Intellect and Wisdom.

EPILEPTIC

Something is not wired up just right in this character’s brain and that causes them to have little short outs.

When something triggers a Trauma check for the character, or their Stress reaches above their Int score, there is a 1 in 8 chance of the character having a seizure, in which they’re completely helpless for the next 1d3 minutes.

EXCOMMUNICATED

This character has invoked the wrath of an angry god, calling down divide judgement on his head. This is more commonly a member of the faithful whose fallen away or lost his way or willfully defies the cult’s teachings, but less commonly, it’s an outsider whose been declared an enemy of the faith.

Excommunicated members cannot gain the blessings of any miracles related to a single Covenant. Additionally, baleful invocations from priests of the deity that excommunicated them half their threshold.

FATED

Certain character’s bear a strange sign on them that marks them for doom. The cruel strings of fate weave together to form a noose for them.

At creation, the GM rolls 1d8!. This is the number of sessions the character has before Fate begins to sharpen her shears. After that point, the character takes extra damage each time they're hit equal to how many sessions have passed since then.

FERAL

The wild and untamed lands are home to frontier men and families. Occasionally, children escape and are raised only by the wild, occasionally wolves.

Feral characters still receive any free skills from their Archetype but any additional skills they try to pick up can only be from the Survival Skill Suite.

HALF-LIFE

This character has died and forced back to life, maybe against his will. This character is affected by the Force Bind Spirit spell, though the Duration is set to 1d4! - 2 (minimum 1) years and a day. Why he came back is up to you. Perhaps he had some unfinished business or perhaps he was targeted accidently. In any case, now he lives a half-life.

HAUNTED

Mostly commonly, this character has done something to dishonor his clan and has been since been haunted by one of their ancestors, displeased by their actions.

The spirit takes the corporeal form of a rotting corpse wearing its death mask, bothering the possessed at all hours to restore his family’s honor and stop being such a failure. The ancestor will do nothing to protect their descendant and often make life harder for them when they can.

Unfortunately, decades, if not centuries, in the grave have made the ancestor more than a bit senile. Even if the character is to obtain Great Honor, the ancestor doesn’t care; what it really wants is for the character to sacrifice themselves in a heroic way or perform suicide.

This character doesn’t heal Stress naturally and loses 1 Honor at the end of every session.

This Flaw may be paid off for half cost if the character has lasted 2 sessions in Great Honor, however it goes back up to full price if they slip out of Great Honor later.

HEMOPHILIC

These guys love to bleed. Well, they may not love it but they certainly do it a lot more than the rest of us. They take an additional d4 points of damage every time they're wounded.

IDIOT SAVANT

This character was born gifted. Or in other words, they were born with the ability to understand something but nothing else. This character picks an Advanced Skill or Training; all other Skills and Trainings cost double. The chosen Skill or Training now costs half.

IMPISH

This character loves getting into trouble, mostly through pranks of all kinds. While she’s not inherently malicious, her pranks tend to be unwelcome.

IMPOTENT

If this character was a tree, he’d have particularly softwood, if you know what I mean. His lack of confidence in his abilities in the bedroom, tend to affect the other parts of his life, often snowballing; he loses an additional Honor for every 10 lost.

INBRED

Often a curse of the nobility that get too close to their cousins, inbreeding can stunt the mental capacity of their offspring. Typically, this is pretty mild for the most part, but it means that increasing this character’s Intellect score costs double.

LIMP

Something isn’t working just right, whether from a birth defect or a war wound, causing this character to hobble along at a slower pace. Characters with a limp move at half their speed at all times.

MISSING ARM

Most likely this is the result of some terrible accident or horrific war wound, though some people are born with an arm missing.

A character with this Flaw is unable to wield two handed weapons, employ bows or crossbows, or anything else that needs two hands.

This Flaw may be rolled multiple times, with the second time you take this flaw making it worth 60 CrP, as it prevents the character from using any weapons, and makes them unable to cast spells with Somatic components

MISSING EAR

While it won’t limit your ability to hear entirely, the shape of the ear does make it easier to hear things.

The first time you roll this Flaw, all Listening Checks take a penalty of -3.

The second time, all Listening checks are 3 Difficulties harder and your initiative dice becomes one worse. You gain 25 CrPs instead of 20 (in addition to the CrPs from the first time you picked this Flaw up).

MISSING EYE

You can imagine that a character with one eye gone would have an issue with his depth perception. You’d be correct.

The first time a character gains this Flaw, he automatically attacks second when engaging in melee, even if his weapon has the greater reach and everything is counted as two range categories further for ranged weaponry.

The second time this Flaw is rolled, the penalties are replaced by those of the Blind, Total Flaw and he gains an additional 25 CrP.

MONOTHEIST

For this character, there is only one true god and all other divines are false deities or the work of devils.

He will refuse help from pagan clerics and is bound to only miracles within his chosen Covenant.

MULE

While they may not show it, this character is the result of mixed ancestry and something about their unnatural genesis makes them sterile, unable to promulgate. While this will definitely make creating a dynasty an issue, certain carousers may hardly consider this a Flaw.

NO DEPTH PERCEPTION

This character has a hard time reading distances making it so that he automatically attacks second when engaging in melee combat, even if his weapon has the greater reach and everything is counted as two range categories further for ranged weaponry.

OPUSPHOBIA

Opusphobia is defined by a fear or repulsion of a certain career choice. Someone with this Flaw may dislike horse merchants because he sees them all at crooks or innkeepers because he’s afraid they’re working with the Thieves Guilds.

PASSIONLESS

Whether because of imbalance in their brain or they haven’t really found that thing that calls to them, a Passionless character has problems really being interested in any given subject they try to engage in. Passions for them cost double to purchase and increase and any situation die they gain from their Skills are always one smaller.

You can, of course, choose whatever fear you feel fits your character, but we’ve provided a table you can use to generate one for you.

REFUGEE

Fleeing from their homeland for either economic, religious, or conflict reasons, this character finds themselves in a foreign land. Their flight has forced them to leave much of their belongings behind, halving their starting cash and they tend to be unfamiliar with the language and customs. Their native language is not the main one for this area and the Language and Law of the country they’re fleeing to costs double as they had to learn it under stressful circumstances

SECRET IDENTITY

For whatever reason, whether they’re hiding from enemies or they want to put their old life behind them, this character is trying to keep their past a secret. When this flaw is rolls, this character selects one of their Archetypes to be the remnant of their past. Any mention of the Archetypes abilities nets this character 1 Stress.

SELF MARTYR

Life doesn’t feel complete if people don’t know how much you’re sacrificing for them.

SENSITIVE

This character has superb senses compared to others, however it makes it challenging for them to function sometimes as they can just get overloaded.

SENSITIVE HEARING

This character is incredibly prone to noise. Other’s normal talking voice sound to them like they’re yelling. You’ll think this would help them suss out noise more effectively but instead it makes it harder to hear because there’s too much sensory input.

TALKER

Whether around the campfire or in stressful situation, this person can’t keep their mouth shut. They’ll just talk and talk, only stopping to take necessary breathes.

THRALL

Thralls are those taken in raids or the children of slaves. Technically, they’re ‘free’ but they have a social stigma that follow them. Thralls gain no extra starting cash and half the amount of equipment the kit normally starts with and starting a Confrontation with someone of high status automatically gives you 3 points.

VOW of POVERTY

The Middle Realm corrupts and those that wish to come closer to the Gods’ Realm sometimes eschew worldly possessions. This character only keeps enough wealth on them to buy basic food to sustain them for that day, all other wealth is donated to any local cults. They give their goods to the needy, not fellow adventurers. Their Max Favor is increased by 1.
