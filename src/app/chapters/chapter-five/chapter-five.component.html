Every person have flaws; little quirks of their personality or odd behaviors. These flaws are often a way of dealing with troubles in their life, letting off Stress or rationalizing their behavior to others. Other times they’re artifacts of a hard life, like a soldier who’s missing an arm.

Flaws in Bonfire are a trade off: they’ll make your character less able to do things in certain arenas or harder to be around but allow you to invest in abilities and powers elsewhere.

There are two ways to select Flaws: Rolling and Cherry Picking. Rolling gives you more CrP per Flaw but Cherry Picking gives you more control.

CHERRY PICKING

For the roleplayers among us with a specific character idea in mind, you may Cherry Pick your Flaws.

Each Flaw loses 10 CrP of it’s value per Flaw to a minimum of 0 CrP received. So, the 3rd Flaw you pick will be at a -30 CrP.

FLAW DESCRIPTIONS

ABSENT-MINDED

CrP: 15

This character has their mind elsewhere and can disconnect from the real world. Whenever leaving on a major expedition, this character has to make a Wisdom check for any minor equipment. Failure indicates that they left it behind.

ABUSIVE

CrP: 20

Traveling with this character is difficult as he tends to take out his own problems on others. This character transfers 1 point of Stress to the party member with the least amount of Honor every day they travel together, though means of belittling them and criticizing everything they do.

ADDICT

CrP: 20

Vice

Roll D20

Name

1 - 5

Drugs

6 - 10

Sex

11 - 15

16 - 20

Alcohol

Their vice is their life. They live for whatever binds them. Every day they don’t indulge in their sin of choice, they heal no Stress, regardless of how much they relax or what hobbies they engage in.

You may roll on the Vice table to determine your vice or choose one freely.

Gamblers need that risk of failure to get their high and so can’t take the Gambling/Gaming skill, as they’re unable to approach any gambling situation with a clear enough head to take advantage of their knowledge.

AGEUSIA

CrP: 15

This affliction is characterized by lack of taste and smell. While you can compensate this handicap through work arounds, you might find it problematic when dealing with poisons and airborne toxins.

The first issue is that the Cooking/Baking skill costs thrice as much. The second is that any saving throws against attacks using poisons and those airborne toxins are automatic fails, unless someone else is there to give the ageusic enough warning to spit out the food or cover his nose.

AGORAPHOBIA

CrP: 25

The a fear of being exposed is a very real reality. The idea of being attacked or embarrassing yourself in public can make going out a traumatic experience.

very day spent out in the open (while this usually means ‘out of doors’, a very large cathedral could also count), an agoraphobic gains 1 point of Stress.

APPEASER

CrP: 20

This character can’t help but let people have their way. He’s a pushover.

Appeasers set aside their highest dice roll in any Social Conflict they engage in. Additionally, they may never escalate either, however they only gain half the Stress from the conflict if they lose.

ARROGANT

CrP: 20

This person is so confident, they don’t even consider others a viable threat. They may never flee from combat, though they can take other defensive actions. Additionally, they can never just match in Social Conflict; they have to raise or pass.

ASTHMATIC

CrP: 40

This character has difficulty breathing, causing them to come up short of breath. This doubles the penalties to your Fatigue score gained from Fatigue, so if you have 2 levels of Fatigue, you’ll take a penalty of 4 to your Fatigue instead of 2.

ATHEIST

CrP: 30

The way of the divine is a mysterious path and someone mistake it for no path at all. They refuse to believe that intelligent higher powers exist and so forfeit some of the blessings.

Atheists never gain Favor from sacrifices and gain Stress equal to the amount of Favor they spend if they break down and supplicant the gods.

Others may invoke Miracles on them, if the faithful choose to waste divine favor on heathens.

AUDACIOUS

CrP: 25

This character gains immense satisfaction from pulling off dangerous stunts. She’s not trying to kill herself, but she does like to take calculated risks.

If there’s a chance to do something, where failure would result in being reduced to the Dying wound category, and the audacious character succeeds, they gain an amount of Honor or heal an amount of Stress equal to the number of people watching the stunt.

BASTARD

CrP: 30

This character’s parents got around and so his legitimacy is in question. Even if his father claims him as his own, the circumstances of his birth follow him his entire life.

Bastard characters don’t gain their Devotion to their clan starting off. They also half their starting cash and only gain 9 Honor for every 10 earned.

BIGOT

CrP: 35

Something about a certain race rubs this person the wrong way. It could be from a bad experience or a cloistered upbring, either way this person goes out of their way to abuse members of a selected race.

Traveling with this race nets the person 1 Stress per day, however, the person can transfer 1 point of Stress to that party member by being mean and spiteful to them.

BLIND, PARTIAL

CrP: 40

This character has partially lost his sight. In this case, the character can see vague shapes and shadows but can’t make out any details.

In melee combat, they defend and attack with a 1d12! And ranged combat is right out, but if you insist, you’ll be attacking with a 1d6!.

Additionally, any Skill Challenges involving sight is increased by 2 difficulty levels.

BLIND, TOTAL

CrP: 60

This character has completely lost his sight. He sees only pure white or pure black, unable to sense any movement around him.

In melee combat, they defend and attack with a 1d6! And ranged combat is right out, however, if you insist, you’ll be attacking with a 1d4, so enjoy your fumbles, you stubborn bastard.

Additionally, any Skill Challenges involving sight halves the person’s Skill Rank.

BOAR FACED

CrP: 20

This is the polite way of say that someone is ugly as sin. This could be the result of parentage or a bad accident, either way, people don’t like being around them or dealing with them. As mean as it might be, people judge books by their covers. Anyone engaging with this individual in a Conflict gains a Stress but once committed, they’re resistant to what the person has to say, giving them a d12 to their dice pool.

CALOUS

CrP: 15

Some of this character’s actions can come off as cold or aloof. He is unmoved by the crying of orphans and widows.

A callous character in a Social Conflict puts an Honor on the table for not exactly matching his opponent’s total when he exceeds the total, however, he doesn’t have to put 1 Honor on the table for matching with the exact same total as his opponent.

CLAUSTROPHOBIC

CrP: 25

To say that this character dislikes enclosed spaces is an understatement. Any time that a character enters an area where they can touch two different walls with their arms outstretched, they gain a point of Stress. This can be a hallway, or small room. Additionally, this could be a corner, if the character is unable to immediately escape.

CLUBFOOTED

CrP: 20

This is a fairly common ailment characterized by a misshapen foot, either from odd muscle growth or odd bone shape.

In either case, a character with this deformity can go about most of his day without an issue but he’s unable to Sprint if it comes down to it.

CRAVEN

CrP: 60

When the going gets tough, this character gets going - in the opposite direction. They don’t have a brave bone in their body and are affected by the Morale Advanced rules, even if they’re a player character and they’re always treated as one Honor Category lower to determine their Panic and Routed Thresholds.

CRUEL

CrP: 10

This character gets his jollies from being unnecessarily mean to others and by dragging their pain out over the longest possible duration.

A cruel character gains 1 Honor for inflicting only 1 point of damage to a person but, because people generally don’t like his behavior and bad mouth him, he only gains 9 Honor for every 10 earned, regardless of source.

CULT UPBRINGING

CrP: 15

Whether it’s a child of a monastery or raised in a suicide cult, this character had an extremely religious upbringing to the point where they had to sacrifice more worldly knowledge. Characters with this background have to spend at least 20 CrP on the Religion Advanced Skill.

DARK SECRET

CrP: 20

There’s something that this character is hiding and she’s worried that she’ll be found out. She’ll jealously guard every aspect of her life if she can for fear of being found out.

This dark secret must be determined beforehand. Any time the secret is even obliquely mentioned, that character gains 1 Stress.

DEAF

CrP: 25

There’s no two ways of splitting it: this character can’t hear anything, preventing him from taking the Listening skill or using the Streetwise Suite to try and hear something. Also, because of the lack of audio clues, their initiative is increased by 1 dice size.

DEATHWISH

CrP: 35

Whether from a chemical imbalance or from tragic events, this character is longing for the skeletal arms of death. He never uses any defensive tactic in combat and will volunteer first for anything remotely dangerous.

DECADENT

CrP: 25

Only the best is worthy of this character and how do you know what’s best? The price tag. This character pays twice as much for any given thing as he seeks out the most expensive version of whatever he’s buying.

While this is completely unrelated from the quality of the item in question, it does mean that whatever he buys is uniquely identifiable because of color, shape, or other unnecessary shit crammed onto it.

DEBTOR

CrP: 30

This character has gotten into trouble financially. This character starts with 5d20 + 150 sc of debt instead of gaining a like amount to start off with. All their equipment much then be purchased on a credit of 50 sc that is also taken out of their debt. If they don’t make payments on it, they may find themselves on the bad end of the law or the loan shark they borrowed from.

DEPENDENT

CrP: 20

This character is clingy. She attached to someone and won’t let go. Any day she doesn’t see the person she’s attached to she gains 1 Stress.

DERANGED

CrP: 40

There’s holes in this character’s brains and he’s starting to slip into madness. The nature of this neurosis is up to the character.

The character doesn’t suffer the effects of Stress until it matches his Wisdom score, at that point, he has an episode and doubles his total Stress. Once, he’s recovered (dropped his Stress back down below his Wisdom), he suffers a permanent reduction of 10 fractional points to both his Intellect and Wisdom.

DISHONEST

CrP: 10

This character likes to lie. Whether it’s to protect a perceived vulnerability or to seem more impressive, this character bends or omits the Truth. Whenever they do so, they lose 1 Stress but also lose 1 Honor if they’re caught.

DISLOYAL

CrP: 50

The issue with attachments is that they prevent you from getting ahead, which is why this character puts such a low price of loyalty. This character can have no Devotions.

DISTRUSTING

CrP: 30

Somebody hurt this individual and they have trouble trusting and forming bonds with others. All Devotions for this character cost double to buy and increase.

EGOTISTICAL

CrP: 15

This character thinks a lot of himself. Maybe too much, honestly, and when people don’t pay attention to him, it bothers him. An egotistical character gains 1 Stress per day if he’s not in the Great Honor category. Additionally, the number of repercussion dice from Social Conflict is doubled if they lose.

ENVIOUS

CrP: 20

It bothers this person when people do better than her. It really gets under her skin and causes issues. This character gains 1 Stress per day if there’s anyone in the party with higher Honor than her.

EPILEPTIC

CrP: 25

Something is not wired up just right in this character’s brain and that causes them to have little short outs.

When something triggers a Trauma check for the character, or their Stress reaches above their Int score, there is a 1 in 8 chance of the character having a seizure, in which they’re completely helpless for the next 1d3 minutes.

EUNUCH

CrP: 60

A Eunuch is one whose ability to reproduce has been surgically removed from them. Eunuchs are apart of many cultures, serving as members of the government or in religious vocations.

While eunuchs tend to wield worldly power, they do so while subservient to others, and so can never achieve Great Honor, instead falling into Too Much Honor.

EXCOMMUNICATED

CrP: 50

This character has invoked the wrath of an angry god, calling down divide judgement on his head. This is more commonly a member of the faithful who’s fallen away or lost his way or willfully defies the cult’s teaches, but less commonly, it’s an outsider who’s been declared an enemy of the faith.

Excommunicated members cannot gain the blessings of any miracles related to a single Covenant. Additionally, baleful invocations from priests of the deity that excommunicated them half their threshold.

FATED

CrP: 100

Certain character’s bear a strange sign on them that marks them for doom. The cruel strings of fate weave together to form a noose for them.

At creation, the GM rolls 1d8!. This is the number of sessions the character has before Fate begins to sharpen her shears. After that point, the character takes extra damage each time they're hit equal to how many sessions have passed since then.

FEAR of HEIGHTS

CrP: 15

There’s two unlearned fears; spiders and heights. This character will never willingly put them in situation where they could fall off any ledge. Doing so forces them to gain 1 Stress per minute.

FERAL

CrP: 25

The wild and untamed lands are home to frontier men and families. Occasionally, children escape and are raised only by the wild, occasionally wolves.

Feral characters still receive any free skills from their class but any additional skills they try to pick up can only be from the Survival Skill Suite.

FINICKY

CrP: 20

Picky is an understatement when it comes to this character. They tend to waffle quite a bit when it comes to everything. These character’s gain 1 point of Stress every time they go out shopping and whenever they engage with someone in Social Conflict.

FLIRTY

CrP: 10

It’s nice to feel wanted, but sometimes it’s uncomfortable for the people around you if you make them feel too wanted. Flirty characters often cross that line, hitting on others even when it’s unwelcome. They can transfer 1 point of Stress to anyone they flirt with.

GLUTTON

CrP: 20

This character can’t get enough of food and must stuff themselves at every opportunity. They gain a +4 to their Fatigue Score and must eat twice as much or become increasingly fatigued as if they were starving.

GREEDY

CrP: 20

No matter how much money is in their purse, it will never be enough for this character. They lose either 1 Honor or gain 1 Stress for every gold piece worth of goods and coin that goes to someone else in the party.

HALF LIFE

CrP: 100

This character has died and forced back to life, maybe against his will. This character is affected by the Force Bind Spirit spell, though the Duration is set to 1d4! - 2 (minimum 1) years and a day. Why he came back is up to you. Perhaps he had some unfinished business or perhaps he was targeted accidently. In any case, now he lives a half life.

HAUNTED

CrP: 60

Mostly commonly, this character has done something to dishonor his clan and has been since been haunted by one of their ancestors, displeased by their actions.

The spirit takes the corporeal form of a rotting corpse wearing it’s death mask, bothering the possessed at all hours to restore his family’s honor and stop being such a failure. The ancestor will do nothing to protect their descendant and often make life harder for them when they can.

Unfortunately, decades, if not centuries, in the grave have made the ancestor more than a bit senile. Even if the character is to obtain Great Honor, the ancestor doesn’t care; what it really wants is for the character to sacrifice themselves in a heroic way or perform suicide.

This character doesn’t heal Stress naturally and loses 1 Honor at the end of every session.

This Flaw may be paid off for half cost if the character has lasted 2 sessions in Great Honor, however it goes back up to full price if they slip out of Great Honor later.

HEMOPHILIC

CrP: 60

These guys love to bleed. Well, they may not love it but they certainly do it a lot more than the rest of us. They take an additional d4 points of damage every time they're wounded.

HUMORLESS

CrP: 15

Don’t bother cracking a joke or even inviting this person to your party because they just don’t get humor at all. Could be that they just don’t have funny bone in their body or they ‘get it’ but just don’t think what’s being served up is humorous.

If a humorless player can keep a straight face when someone at the table cracks a joke, they gain 1 Honor. If not, they gain 1 Stress and lose an Honor.

HYPEROPIA

CrP: 25

This is the scientific term for farsightedness, which means a character can’t see what’s right in front of his face.

When in melee combat, this character lowers their Attack and Defense dice by one size. Also, any checks involving looking at things near at hand are two difficult levels harder.

IDIOT SAVANT

CrP: 60

This character was born gifted. Or in other words, they were born with the ability to understand something but nothing else. This character picks an Advanced Skill or Mastery; all other Skills and Masteries cost double. The chosen Skill or Mastery now costs half.

IMMATURE

CrP: 15

Something made it so that this character never fully grew up. She gets anger and throws fits whenever she doesn’t get her way. Whenever the party doesn’t go with an idea she suggested, she loses 1 Honor.

IMPATIENT

CrP: 20

This flaw manifests in two ways: either in impatience with people they perceive are dumber than they are (which probably includes everyone) or in the lack of the ability to wait for something.

Either way, when having to wait, the character gains 1 point of Stress per minute.

IMPIOUS

CrP: 30

While he may believe in the gods, this character tends to be a little sloppy in their sacrifices and prayers and the gods can tell. He halves the amount of Favor he would normally receive from Holy Days.

IMPISH

CrP: 20

This character loves getting into trouble, mostly through pranks of all kinds. While she’s not inherently malicious, her pranks tend to be unwelcome.

Whenever an impish character pulls off a prank, they receive an amount of Honor equal to the amount the target lost. They gain 1 Stress every week they don’t pull off at least 1 prank

IMPOTENT

CrP: 25

If this character was a tree, he’d have particularly softwood, if you know what I mean. His lack of confidence in his abilities in the bedroom, tend to affect the other parts of his life, often snowballing; he loses an additional Honor for every 10 lost.

INBRED

CrP: 15

Often a curse of the nobility that get too close to their cousins, inbreeding can stunt the mental capacity of their offspring. Typically, this is pretty mild for the most part, but it means that increasing this character’s Intellect score costs double and they half any points they add to it when they level up.

INFIRM

CrP: 45

There’s just some people that seem to get sick no matter what precautions they take. This character’s effective Con score is halved for any saving throws vs disease, poisons, or toxins of any kind.

INSOMNIAC

CrP: 45

Who knows what causes this, but the character in question has a hard time falling and remaining sleep.

Every night, the character makes a Wis check vs d20! - 1 for every previous day failed by between successful checks. A failure indicates the character didn’t sleep well and heals no Stress or Health.

INTOLERANT

CrP: 35

When it comes to other’s culture or way of life, this character wants nothing to do with it.

Traveling with a character that speaks another native language nets the person 1 Stress per day, however, the person can transfer 1 point of Stress to that party member by being mean and spiteful to them.

KLUTZ

CrP: 35

This character is just unlucky. It’s like she’s a lode for bad happenstance. This character fumbles on the lowest and next lowest roll when applicable, so on a d20!, they fumble on a natural 1 and natural 2.

LAZY

CrP: 20

Whether it’s morning, noon, or night, you’ll be hard pressed to get this character going, even under the most strenuous of circumstances. They receives a +6 to their Fatigue Score and can never take the Marathoner Mastery.

LIAR

CrP: 25

While some would assume this trait is relegated to the legal profession, liars can be found all walks of life. What separates this type of liar from your garden variety liar, however, is that this character lies even when he doesn’t have to. He gains 1 Stress for every truth he tells.

LIMP

CrP: 30

Something isn’t working just right, whether from a birth defect or a war wound, causing this character to hobble along at a slower pace. Characters with a limp move at half their speed at all times.

LOUD

CrP: 15

This character has no volume control and can't seem to use his inside voice. When trying to talk quietly, they gain 1 Stress and even then, the check to overhear them is 1 Difficulty easier.

MANIPULATIVE

CrP: 15

There’s a need for control that drives this character’s motivation: the need to get others to bend to his will. At the start of every session, the player hands the GM a secret agenda. If the agenda is not completed by the end of the session, the player loses 1 Honor and gain 1 Stress.

This agenda should be to get a character to do something that, while not necessarily against their best interest, is certainly something they weren’t going to do in the first place. The GM may veto any agenda.

If another player discovers what the agenda is and calls the character out on it, the manipulative character drops an Honor category.

MASOCHIST

CrP: 20

This character loves to be punished and feel pain exquisite. He heals all his Stress if he suffers and fails a Trauma check in combat as the pain releases him.

MEGALOMANIAC

CrP: 20

The only thing that matters in this world is power, and more importantly, having people at your beck and call.

Every title and honorific a character gets heals them 1 Stress, while every title another gets in the party induces 1 Stress.

MISSING ARM

CrP: 35

Most likely this is the result of some terrible accident or horrific war wound, though some people are born with an arm missing.

A character with this Flaw is unable to wield two handed weapons, employ bows or crossbows, or anything else that needs two hands.

This Flaw may be rolled multiple times, with the second time you take this flaw making it worth 70 CrP, as it prevents the character from using any weapons, and makes them unable to cast spells with Somatic components

MISSING DIGIT

CrP: 25

Before safety laws were a thing, workplace accidents could deprive you of more than 1 finger.

For each digit missing, increase the fumble chance of that character by 1, and increases the worth of this Flaw by 10 CrP.

MISSING EAR

CrP: 20

While it won’t limit your ability to hear entirely, the shape of the ear does make it easier to hear things.

The first time you roll this Flaw, all Listening checks are 1 Difficulty harder.

The second time, all Listening checks are 3 Difficulties harder and your initiative dice becomes one worse. You gain 30 CrPs instead of 20 (in addition to the CrPs from the first time you picked this Flaw up).

MISSING EYE

CrP: 30

You can imagine that a character with one eye gone would have an issue with his depth perception. You’d be correct.

The first time a character gains this Flaw, he automatically attacks second when engaging in melee, even if his weapon has the greater reach and everything is counted as two range categories further for ranged weaponry.

The second time this Flaw is rolled, the penalties are replaced by those of the Blind, Total Flaw and he gains an additional 30 CrP.

MONOTHEIST

CrP: 25

For this character, there is only one true god and all other divines are false deities or the work of devils.

He will refuse help from pagan clerics and is bound to only miracles within his chosen Covenant.

MULE

CrP: 10

While they may not show it, this character is the result of mixed ancestry and something about their unnatural genesis makes them sterile, unable to promulgate. While this will definitely make creating a dynasty an issue, certain carousers may hardly consider this a Flaw.

MUTE

CrP: 40

It’s not an uncommon punishment for a cult to cut a heretic’s tongue out, preventing him from being able to spread his lies. While that’s more metal, it’s more common to simply be born without the ability to speak.

Characters with this Flaw can only describe their actions and gesticulate wildly to communicate with NPCs and other players during the game.

MYOPIA

CrP: 25

Otherwise known as nearsightedness, everything beyond a certain range from the character becomes blurred and hard to see.

While attacking with ranged weapons, all targets are considered two range categories further away.

NATIONALIST

CrP: 35

For this character, the first priority for them is their nation’s glory and rise to power. Beyond this, there is little. Their culture stands supreme above all over and soon the world will know it. All racial preferences are made one step worse for this character, only having Preferred with those of her exact same nationality. Additionally, any Devotion not related to their national identity costs double to purchase and improve.

NERVOUS

CrP: 45

He’ll jump easily at any scare and never seems to relax even in the best of times. This character increases their initiative die by 1 size as it takes them longer to remember they have emotions other than fear.
