Stat Scores represent different dimensions of your character and how good their baseline is at certain activities. These dimensions are Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intellect, Wisdom, Charisma.

Stat Checks

There many come a time where a Skill Check doesn’t exactly cover what needs to be tested. What skill covers trying to deadlift a boulder or remember a fact about a person? When skills fail and it’s simply a raw test of an ability, Stat Checks come into play.

Players making an unopposed ability check roll a d20 and try to get a result equal to or under their their stat score. If the check is made at 1/2 the score, they're trying to roll equal to or under their score, rounded down. Should they do so, they succeed.

OPPOSED CHECKS

Opposed checks work almost the same. The player rolls a d20! + their relevant stat score vs an opposing d20! + any relevant modifiers (such as the boulder being really heavy or only their foes relevant stat score). The person with the higher total succeeds.

Strength (Str) represents your character’s pure brawn. How much can she lift and carry? How strong are her punches?

DAMAGE

This number is added to your damage whenever your character hits something with their fits or uses thrown ranged weapons. It’s the raw, physical force he can put into an object.

CARRY

This number represents the number of items a character can carry in their hands before he gets more Encumbered. More on Encumbrance is found in Chapter 10: Equipment.

SKILL

This number represents the discount you apply to Skill costs whenever you buy a Skill with this as a listed Stat.

CONFRONTATION DIE

Sometimes your character’s strength can play into social situations, such as trying to intimidate your opponent. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Confrontations if your Strength is applicable.

Dexterity (Dex) is a measure of how agile your character is. Can she dodge arrows or can she execute a graceful pirouette? Dexterity is crucial for avoiding blows in combat or traps in a hallway.

ATTACK

Being graceful allows your character to take advantage of openings others can’t. This number gets added to all attack rolls your character makes.

DEFENSE

If you can move quicker than your opponent, you can often get out of harm’s way before it becomes a problem. This number gets added to all melee defense rolls your character makes.

INITIATIVE

Another bonus of being quick on your feet is the ability to get moving quicker when manure hits the windmill. This number gets added to your initiative rolls at the beginning of combat.

Constitution (Con) represents your character’s overall health and toughness. Can he shrug off the Dancing Death or does he succumb to a head cold? Can she take a solid blow in combat and not even blink? These are the things Constitution oversees.

Constitution is the base of your starting Vitality so don’t neglect it.

MIN VITALITY

Every other level, a character gains a reroll of their Vitality Die. This number is the minimum amount that reroll can be.

So, if a Fighter with 15 Constitution gets a 3 on their reroll, they add 4 to that result to get it to 7. This cannot exceed the amount that can be rolled on your dice. If a wizard has a minimum reroll of 7, they will still only get 4 Vitality from their dice.

ENCUMB.

Encumbrance plays a critical role in any fight and represents your character’s ability to mentally and physically overcome his weariness and struggle on.

More on calculating Encumbrance is found in Chapter 10: Equipment.

Intellect (Int) measures your character’s raw knowledge: how much data can he remember? Can she do mathematical calculations or remember who the Elector of Stanitz is? Intellect doesn’t help in applying that knowledge but it does give you knowledge to draw on.

ATTACK

Being able to pinpoint holes in your opponent’s defense allows you to see openings that others don’t. A cunning intellect can help land a telling blow. This number is added to all attack rolls your character makes.

LvL CrP

A character with a high Intellect is able to grasp concepts and skills quicker than others, allowing him to learn more things than others in an equivalent amount of time. This is the amount of CrP that a character gets when they level up. More on leveling is found in Chapter 13: Character Progression.

SKILL

This number represents the discount you apply to Skill costs whenever you buy a Skill with this as a listed Stat.

CONFRONTATION DIE

Sometimes your character’s intelligence can play into social situations, such as convincing someone that you’re right simply because you know more. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Confrontations if your Intellect is applicable.

Roll to Recall

Details are hard to remember and notes can get lost or disorganized. To keep play moving, the player can make a Int Ability Check vs d20! to remember a piece of information that was heard in a previous session, such a person’s name or where someone came from.

To clarify: this can’t be used to remember information that was given to you this same session.

Wisdom (Wis) is applied knowledge. Does she process information quicker and learn from other’s mistakes? Does he understand life is more than just a set of numbers of probabilities? Can she see other’s viewpoints? Wisdom can also be defined as force of will, the ability to overcome physical problems with mental force.

DEFENSE

Having greater body awareness allows you to keep your form tight and your limbs out of danger and where they need to be. This coupled with the ability to read your opponent allows the wise character to keep from getting hit. This number gets added to all melee defense rolls your character makes.

ENCUMB.

The ability to disconnect your mind from the pain and ignore your screaming muscles is part of what gives long distance runners their ability to run miles without tiring. Your ability to put yourself “in the zone” is represented by this number.

More on calculating Encumbrance is found in Chapter 6: Equipment.

INITIATIVE

Being able to process any situation quicker is important to staying alive. This number gets added to your initiative rolls at the beginning of combats so lower is better

Charisma (Cha) represents force of personality. Does he bend others to his will? Is she the focus of any room she enters? Do people look up to them or what to be him? Charisma is not looks or even likeability: it represents this animal magnetism that people follow.

FAVOR

The gods are fickle creatures, bestowing their gifts on some and their ire on others, and often switch with the seasons, however, charismatic characters tend to do well for themselves.

This is the minimum amount of Favor a person gets whenever they roll to gain Favor. More information about that is found in Chapter 12: The Divine.

START HONOR

Being well thought of allows your character to hold his head high in any situation, giving him the edge. This is your character’s starting Honor category. You can find out more in Chapter 6: Character Vitals.

FOLLOWERS

This is the total number of Followers your character has to draw on during his adventuring career. More on Followers is found in Appendix 1: GM's Guide.

TRAIT POINTS

Characters with strong personalities tend to have a lot of definable qualities that allow them to blend into any social group or a few really strong ones that people seem drawn to. These points are spent increase the number and/or strength of your character’s Traits.

SKILL

This number represents the discount you apply to Skill costs whenever you buy a Skill with this as a listed Stat.

CONFRONTATION DIE

Sometimes your character’s charisma can play into social situations, such seducing someone. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Confrontations if your Charisma is applicable.
