Strength (Str) represents your character’s pure brawn. How much can she lift and carry? How strong are her punches?

DAMAGE

This number is added to your damage whenever your character hits something with their fits or uses thrown ranged weapons. It’s the raw, physical force he can put into an object.

CARRY

This number represents the base weight a character can carry before he moves up to the next level of encumbrance.

More on encumbrance is found in Chapter 12: Equipment.

SOCIAL DIE

Sometimes your character’s strength can play into social situations, such as trying to intimidate your opponent. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Social Conflicts if your Strength is applicable.

Dexterity (Dex) is a measure of how agile your character is. Can she dodge arrows or can she execute a graceful pirouette? Dexterity is crucial for avoiding blows in combat or traps in a hallway.

ATTACK

Being graceful allows your character to take advantage of openings others can’t. This number gets added to all attack rolls your character makes.

DEFENCE

If you can move quicker than your opponent, you can often get out of harm’s way before it becomes a problem. This number gets added to all melee defense rolls your character makes.

INITIATIVE

Another bonus of being quick on your feet is the ability to get moving quicker when manure hits the windmill. This number gets added to your initiative rolls at the beginning of combat.

SOCIAL DIE

Sometimes your character’s dexterity can play into social situations, such as trying to seduce your opponent. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Social Conflicts if your Dexterity is applicable.

Constitution (Con) represents your character’s over all health and toughness. Can he shrug off the Dancing Death or does he succumb to a head cold? Can she take a solid blow in combat and not even blink? These are the things Constitution oversees.

Constitution is the base of your starting Health so don’t neglect it.

MIN HEALTH

Every other level, a character gains a reroll of their Health Die. This number is the minimum amount that reroll can be.

So, if a Fighter with 15 Constitution gets a 3 on their reroll, they add 4 to that result to get it to 7. This cannot exceed the amount that can be rolled on your dice. If a wizard has a minimum reroll of 7, they will still only get 4 Health from their dice.

FATIGUE ADJ.

Fatigue plays a critical role in any fight and represents your character’s ability to mentally and physically overcome his weariness and struggle on.

More on calculating Fatigue is found in Chapter 6: Character Vitals.

Sometimes your character’s constitution can play into social situations, such as convincing your opponent she really doesn’t want to fight you. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Social Conflicts if your Constitution is applicable.

Intellect (Int) measures your character’s raw knowledge: how much data can he remember? Can she do mathematical calculations or remember who the Elector of Stanitz is? Intellect doesn’t help in applying that knowledge but it does give you knowledge to draw on.

ATTACK

Being able to pinpoint holes in your opponent’s defense allows you to see openings that others don’t. A cunning intellect can help land a telling blow. This number is added to all attack rolls your character makes.

LvL CrP

A character with a high Intellect is able to grasp concepts and skills quicker than others, allowing him to learn more things than others in an equivalent amount of time. This is the amount of CrP that a character gets when they level up. More on leveling is found in Chapter 14: Character Progression.

SOCIAL DIE

Sometimes your character’s intelligence can play into social situations, such as convincing someone that you’re right simply because you know more. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Social Conflicts if your Intellect is applicable.

Roll to Recall

Details are hard to remember and notes can get lost or disorganized. To keep play moving, the player can make a Int Ability Check vs d20! to remember a piece of information that was heard in a previous session, such a person’s name or where someone came from.

To clarify: this can’t be used to remember information that was given to you this same session.

Wisdom (Wis) is applied knowledge. Does she process information quicker and learn from other’s mistakes? Does he understand life is more than just a set of numbers of probabilities? Can she see other’s viewpoints? Wisdom can also be defined as force of will, the ability to overcome physical problems with mental force.

DEFENSE

Having greater body awareness allows you to keep your form tight and your limbs out of danger and where they need to be. This coupled with the ability to read your opponent allows the wise character to keep from getting hit. This number gets added to all melee defense rolls your character makes.

FATIGUE ADJ.

The ability to disconnect your mind from the pain and ignore your screaming muscles is part of what gives long distance runners their ability to run miles without tiring. Your ability to put yourself “in the zone” is represented by this number.

More on calculating Fatigue is found in Chapter 6: Character Vitals.

INITIATIVE

Being able to process any situation quicker is important to staying alive. This number gets added to your initiative rolls at the beginning of combats so lower is better

MIN SP

When a caster is forcing his will over the flow of magic from the Painted World, a strong center allows him to reap more power from the ether than others of the same skill. This is the minimum Spell Points granted when rolling for them when you channel.

SOCIAL DIE

Sometimes your character’s wisdom can play into social situations, such knowing exactly what to say to a grieving friend. This is the die that gets added to your dice pool for Social Conflicts if your Wisdom is applicable.

ABILITY CHECKS

There many come a time where a skill check doesn’t exactly cover what needs to be tested. What skill covers trying to deadlift a boulder or remember a fact about a person? When skills fail and it’s simply a raw test of an ability, Ability Checks come into play.

Players making an unopposed ability check roll a d20 and try to get a result equal to or under their their ability score. If the check is made at 1/2 the score, they're trying to roll equal to or under their score, rounded down. Should they do so, they succeed.

OPPOSED CHECKS

Opposed checks work almost the same. The player rolls a d20! + their relevant ability score vs an opposing d20! + any relevant modifiers (such as the boulder being really heavy or only their foes relevant ability score). The person with the higher total succeeds.
