ANATOMY of the DIVINE

COVENANTS

Divines have spheres of power that represent their aspects. These are codified into Covenants, or promises that make with their servants in exchange for a portion of their power.

Cults of that Divine typically pick one, maybe two Covenants two of their chosen Divine to focus on.

For instance, Wülder, a Gothic god, watches over Raiding, Industry and Trade. There could be 3 different priests to Wülder, one focusing on his role as the Bridge Between Men, one seeing his as primarily a watcher over people going on cattle raids, and one worshiping him as the greater crafter and source of inspiration.

While they all worship the same Divine, the Miracles they can invoke in his name are different.

This means that priests of different cults may not get along with each other and there can even be intra-faith wars to purge heretics (those that focus on different Covenant from them).

The main bonus of Covenants is that they make Miracles within them easier to invoke. The specifics are elaborated on in Step 2 of the Invoking Miracles section. You notice a list of which Miracles a Covenant falls into underneath the name but there’s no comprehensive list - players and GM are encouraged to think of their own and use their judgement call on what Miracles would fall under that sphere of influence.

COSMIC LINKS

The Higher Planes are those that exist above the crude and primitive world and so, naturally, the Divines are linked to the events and behaviors of the celestial bodies that make their homes in the heavens.

A Cosmic Link is the specific celestial body that the Divine is associated with and it usually gives an insight to the personality of the god. The Cosmic Link doesn’t necessarily mean your Patron is primarily the Divine of that Cosmic thing. So, a Divine associated with the moon might not be primarily a moon god, in the same way that while Venus was associated with the Morning Star, she was primarily viewed as a love goddess by the Romans. Still, your holy symbols may incorporate the moon motif commonly to represent her.

For clarification’s sake: when something says “decrease” in this section’s explanations, you go down the chart by that many steps. If it says “increase” go up, so increase will always be a bonus, and decrease will always be bad.

COMET This is associated with fervent but fleeting powers. You increase the Miracle Strength of any invoked by 3 for free but you decrease the Miracle’s Duration by 1. This is added after all other considerations but if Longevity becomes lower than Momentary because of this, the Miracle fails and you drop an Honor Category.

EVENING STAR The evening star is a source of strength and beauty: it's the first star to rise in the evening. You increase the Miracle Delivery Time of any invoked by 2 for free. This is added after all other considerations.

MOON The moon is a steady force, its effects are subtle but constant. Increase the Duration of invoked Miracle by 1 for free. This is added after all other considerations.

MORNING STAR The morning star is the last star in the heavens before day and is a lone watcher. You either increase the Miracle Area of Effect or Miracle Distance of any invoked by 1 for free. This is added after all other considerations.

SUN The sun is the lord of heaven, strong and unbending, its power unquestioned. You increase the Miracle Strength of any invoked by 2 for free. This is added after all other considerations.

CYCLICAL Often hailing from older, more primitive times, the Cyclical deity represents earthly inevitabilities in a dichotomy, sometimes with themselves and sometimes with an opposing deity. This can be Spring and Summer, life and death, creation and destruction. They may be weak now, but they will wax unstoppable. For every prayer unanswered, the Priest reduces the next Miracle’s Invocation Die by 1 size. For every prayer answered, he increases it by 1 size. This can stack, so failing 2 times reduces the 3rd attempt’s Invocation Die by 2 sizes, but it resets as soon as one or the other is triggered, so a success here resets this bonus to +0.

The followers of cyclical divines accept all things as they are in their own time: they don’t drop an Honor Category for unanswered prayers.

FAVOR of the DIVINES

WHAT is FAVOR?

Favor is a measure of your standing with a given god. It’s not an absolute measure, however, but more a measure of the chance that a Divine will answer your prayer. How far your Favor goes ranges widely based on how devoted you are to a god.

LEVELS of DEVOTION

There are 3 levels of commitment to a Covenant, that determine how often you get Favor and how much Favor you can have:

UNANOINTED are the lowest level: those outside the cult and its mysteries. The gain no benefits of any kind.

ANOINTED are those that are the lay members of the faith. They gain small bonuses to their max Favor for the deity they’re devoted to and a greater chance to invoke Miracles of their Covenant overall. You can only be anointed to one Covenant at a time.

If you have a positive Devotion to a deity, one of their cults, or one of their Covenants, you can choose to begin the game as an anointed to that deity. Otherwise, you will need to have the Anoint Miracle performed on you.

You can only be anointed to one deity at a time.

SERVANTS are the highest rung of the commitment ladder and serve as the officers of the cult of the god. They’re priests, abbots, hierophants, pontiffs, nazarites, hermits, etc.. These are men and women that have taken vows to devote their life to the cult. In game terms, this means that these are those who have a Servant Archetype as either their Primary or Secondary Archetype.

ACQUIRING FAVOR

Servants turn their eyes from the earth below to higher worlds, both literally and metaphorically. In this way, they’re the keeper of the times and seasons, as well as the calendar to note the occurrence of Holy Days.

When a character gains Favor, he gains a number of points that can be later spent to try and invoke a Miracle.

As such, players who play Servants will need to keep track of the passage of time. Doing so gives them special bonuses according to the schedule laid out below. You’ll note that is follows the Gregorian Calendar layout with holy days happening according to the Judeo-Christian traditions. The assumption that the characters will be following an in-game calendar with random Holy Days specific to their cult but we’re “translating” into the Gregorian calendar, in the same way that we assume the characters are actually speaking in their native fantasy tongues and not in English. Doing this makes it easier for players to keep track because the calendar is a familiar shape. They won’t have to track out a whole year and remember that Beltane is on the 5th of Seed’s Row.

Rules for using your own calendar and keeping all Servants relatively balanced against each other is going to be the Advanced Rules.

Favor is unique to the Divine you’re petitioning. Gaining Favor with other Divine, means that you lose an equal amount of Favor with all others because gods are bad at sharing.

DAILY SACRIFICES (every day) These are small, daily sacrifices. These tend to cost about 1 or 2 sc, 5 to 6 points of Stress, or 1 or 2 Vitality, depending on your Divine's preferences. They can be the sacrifice of a small, sacred animal, the burning of a portion of your meal, or the lighting of incense during morning prayers. They net 1 Favor for Servants of that Divine.

SABBATIC HOLY DAY (every 7 days) This is a day of rest and contemplation, where the faithful are instructed to put aside the things of the world and focus on the Divine with activities related to their Covenant. There is an emphasis on ritual over practicality to emphasis this divide, so while a Divine of Battle encourages his followers fighter every day, on his sabbath he only wants them to focus on ritualistic combat and controlled bloodshed, as opposed to actual combat. Again, this is to remind his followers of the holies of the action and by forcing his followers to stop and think about their actions, he forces them to contemplate the deeper realities of combat. The sabbath takes up 1 to 3 hours and the rest of the day can be spent at the faithful wants.

Servants who observe the sabbath of their Divine gain all Favor back, while anointed worshippers gain their Min Favor from Charisma if they roll a 4 or better on their highest Devotion die related to that Divine. Unanointed worshippers gain nothing.

LUNAR HOLY DAY (4 Sabbatic Holy Days) Where normal Sabbaths are days of rest, Lunar Holy Days are about sacrifices and consecration, which are thematically appropriate to the cult’s Covenant. Those observing the holy day need to stop and spend the whole day or they gain no benefit.

Servants gain all Favor back plus extra Favor equal to their Min Favor. This extra Favor is counted seperately from their normal Favor and is reset on the next Lunar Holy Day (which means you can stock pile it). It also doesn't count towards your total Favor when praying for a Miracle. Those anointed in the cult gain Favor equal to their Devotion die, and the unanointed gain 1 Favor. These bonuses are capped, however.

SOLAR HOLY DAY (every 3 Lunar Holy Days) These holy days celebrate the turning of the seasons and where the other festivals can be severe, with fasting and reading and nerd stuff like that, Solar Holy Days are about feasting and letting loose a bit. The Divines demand their faithful to party all day.

Servants gain all Favor back plus extra Favor equal to a roll of their Devotion die. This extra Favor is counted seperately from their normal Favor and is reset on the next Solar Holy Day (which means you can stock pile it). It also doesn't count towards your total Favor when praying for a Miracle. The anointed faithful gain their full Favor back and the unanointed gain 1 Favor.

ANNUAL HOLY DAY (every 4 Solar Holy Days) The Annual Holy Day either celebrates the new year or an important event in the cult, such as the birth of their messiah or the founding of their faith. It is a 2 day affair and is often occupied by feasting, gift giving, public rituals, and other such nonsense.

Servants and anointed followers gain all Favor back with Servants also gaining extra Favor equal to the max of their Devotion die and the anointed followers gaining extra Favor equal to a roll of their Devotion die. For Servants, this extra Favor is counted seperately from their normal Favor and is reset on the next Annual Holy Day (which means you can stock pile it). It also doesn't count towards your total Favor when praying for a Miracle. The unanointed gain 1 Favor

TAKING AN OATH (SPECIAL) Not a sacrifice or holy day parse, Taking an Oath is essentially getting Favor on loan from the Divines for the promise that, if you get out the bad situation you're in, you'll make a huge sacrifice to them soon after. By Taking an Oath, you gain a die worth of Favor for every 20% of treasure you gain from the adventure you promise to the Divine, should you survive.

This die is equal to the highest Devotion die related to the Divine you're petitioning if you're Anointed to them. The Unanointed must also promise to become Anointed in addition to the cash and goods, in which case they gain a d8! worth of Favor. This Favor can put you over your max but must be spent on a Miracle immediately.

The Divines are extremely litigious. If you don't fulfill your oath in 1 month + 1 for every Honor Category higher than Average Honor you're in, they will seek vengeance. You may no longer gain any Favor with any Divine that shares a pantheon with the one you're in and what you do have will decay, causing you to lose 1 Favor per day. Priests of the Divine will also condemn you as an oath breaker, a very serious accusation, causing you to drop 1 Honor Category per month and preventing you from increasing your Honor. Should you fall into Dishonor, you gain the Excommunicated Flaw.

CRUSADES & PILGRAMES (special) There are holy places to visit and holy things to be done. Crusades and Pilgrimages are special rewards the GM can give out for further the Divine’s will. This can either be through an actual holy war or by waging a spiritual war of conversion. These don’t even need to be things done on purpose and can be part of the campaign.

The GM should award a roll of the crusader or pilgrim's Devotion dice, allowing it to go over the Max Favor allowed, and upping them an Honor Category for furthering their deity’s influence in the Middle Realm. Additionally, if it was an amazing feat of faith, the GM may also increase the character’s Devotion dice size.

The unanointed gain no benefit from crusades and pilgrimages.

CONDEMNATION & ABSOLUTION (special) Servants and Anointed followers are expected to hold to the Divine’s tenets more closely and so can come under condemnation for disobeying their deity. GMs may subtract Favor from Servants and Anointed followers for sins against their religion or prevent them from gaining Favor for prolonged transgressions. Servants and Anointed followers with the Religion Advanced Skill in their Covenant receive a warning before they act (having studied their religion to some extent).

Those that have transgressed must seek absolution before they gain the benefit of holy days and sacrifices again. What this means varies based on the magnitude of the sin.

INVOKING MIRACLES

Now that you’ve got a nice stash of Favor, you probably want to spend it. That’s pretty simple: pray out loud for the Miracle in question. Silent prayers don’t work because no Divine is going to answer a prayer they’re not going to get credit for.

During your prayer, follow the steps below to figure out if your call is heard. Prayer can be done by doing other actions since it is almost solely vocal.

STEP 1: DETERMINE COST

Start by determining how much you’re going to spend on it: this is your cost. You have to spend at least 1 Favor, but you can pay in an additional amount to increase the chance the Miracle will actually be invoked and the strength of it if it does. This cost can’t be greater than your current Favor, but isn’t actually taken out of it until Step 5.

STEP 2: SELECT, MODIFY, and ROLL YOUR INVOCATION

Your base Invocation Die is determined by Miracle you’re trying to invoke and then modified by your commitment to the deity.

Unanointed people increase their dice size by 5, anointed followers increase it by 2, but servants get a free pass and don’t increase their dice at all.

This roll represents the mood of the Divine when you ask. The universe is a big place and deities tend to be busy putting our cosmos-ending fires so, while they try to be attentive to the needs of the mortals, they can’t always give their full attention to it.

If the Miracle you’re asking for is outside of your Covenant, add another dice of equivalent size to your roll.

If an anointed person is invoking a Miracle with a d12! Invocation Die, he actually rolls a d20!+d4!. If the Miracle is also outside the Covenant he’s anointed to, he’ll instead roll 2d20!+2d4!.

SACRED & PROFANE SPACES

🜂 Advanced Rule

Holy places are those areas that are closer to the God’s Realm, where the Middle Realm is weaker. There the faithful can almost feel the the Divines walking around.

In a sacred space dedicated to a certain Divine, Invocation Die are 1 size smaller, down to a d2. It also fills the devoted with a sense of purpose, temporarily increasing their Devotion dice size by 1 for Conflicts and, if defending the space, they add it to the dice pool for free with every escalation.

There are also those areas that are abhorrent to the gods. Desecrated altars, dwarven holds, and other spaces the Divine presence is absent.

These Profane spaces net 1 dice larger for each Invocation Die spent for the Divine space is Profane to. The unanointed are completely unable to spend invoke Miracles.

STEP 3: ADD IT ALL TOGETHER

Add the amount you’re spending to your current favor and then subtract the result of your Invocation Die. If the total is greater than or equal to 0, the Miracle is invoked.

Current Favor - Invocation Roll + Cost = Points to Spend

If your total is below 0, your prayer remains unanswered and you lose face, dropping 1 Honor Category.

STEP 4: MODIFY YOUR MIRACLE

If your total is above 0, you can spend that excess as points that you can spend to modify the Miracle, making it quicker and more powerful.

Since you're probably doing this on the fly and want to do this arithmatic on the fly quickly, we've provided you with a tool to help you do so which can be found here.

DO I KNOW EXACTLY WHEN A MIRACLE IS COMING?

Short answer: yes. but also, no. Your character knows the time frame of when it will be ready and they will know when it's ready to evoke before that time, they'll have no idea.

Once ready, the supplicant will have a time frame to actually use the miracles power. This time frame is equal to his max Devotion dice in units equal to her chosen Delievery Time. This means that 1 Second miracles need to be used right then and there.

So, for example, a character with a d6 Devotion prayers for a miracle and sets the Delivery Time for 1d10 minutes. Her GM rolls a 3. At this point, the character knows the miracle will be ready in the next 1 to 10 minutes but is unsure when at that time it'll be. 3 minutes pass and the GM signals to the player that the miracle is ready. She may now envoke it at anytime within the next 6 minutes.

MIRACLE DESCRIPTIONS

The following are some of the Miracles that you can try and petition your god(s) for in hopes of channeling their power. It is not an exhaustive list and players should feel creative. The rule of thumb is that, as much as possible, the Divine should do as little as possible to achieve the miracle.

We've also provided the Great Library App to help you quickly filter and search through these Miracles to find the one you want.

CREATING MIRACLES on the FLY

The list of Miracles below isn’t supposed to be an exhaustive list that your holy man can pray for but it is here to cover the (hopefully) most common ones so that you don’t have to break a sweat most of the time.

If you need to create a Miracle on the fly based on something your player wants, you can use the following process to figure out how to balance them quickly.

1. Base d12! Invocation Die, minimum d2

2. If it requires a certain condition (such as having stressed or being wounded), lower the dice by 2 size

a. If it gets better depending on that condition (such as getting more powerful the more wounded or stressed they get), lower the dice by 1 sizes instead

b. If this bonus requires the blessed to die, you can probably ignore that.

3. If it has multiple different effects, raise it by 2

4. If the strength has a multiplier (X * 2 or X * 3), raise it by that multiplier - 1

a. If the strength has a divider (X / 2 or X / 3), lower it by that amount - 1

5. If it relies on their Devotion to give the bonus, raise it by 1

6. If it deals direct damage, raise it by 2

a. If that damage would only affect less than half of the enemies, only raise it by 1

b. If it’s a blanket thing, affecting friend and foe alike, don’t worry about raising it at all.

7. If it protects against damage, raise it by 1

8. If it doesn’t do any damage, lower it by 1

9. If the application is probably only for this one specific circumstance, lower it by 2.

10. If it continues to keep on giving over the full duration, raise it by 1.

a. If that bonus is dice, instead of a flat bonus, raise it by 1 (unless it’s conflict based)

11. If it would completely negate a skill check or dice roll, raise it by 3.

12. The final touch: adjust it based on how powerful you think the spell is. If it turns out to be more powerful than you thought and the player tries to use it again, you can increase it.

DESCRIPTION LAYOUT

The first line is the common name of the Miracle, the second line is the primary Covenants for that Miracle. This is not a definite list - if you can make a good case for why a Miracle belongs in your Covenant, you’re free to do so, but note that while some Covenants seem close (like Nature and Beast), they’re not the same and represent different aspects.

After those ones are the effects, or what the Miracle actually does and any exceptions it may contain to the base rules.

ALTER WEATHER

Nature

BASE INVOCATION DIE d6!

EFFECT The Divine changes the weather, bringing storms and floods, or intense periods of drought upon the fields of the heathen. This can also keep a period of particularly fair or foul weather.

The Area of Effect and Distance notes where the miraculous weather phenomenon is centered, with it extending X number of days out in all directions. The supplicant can decide if the weather follows those affected or simply stay stationary.

Rain falls on the righteous and sinner alike and the Divine can’t be bothered to divert floods or aim the storm’s lightning. The supernatural weather affects everyone in the area.

ANOINT

All

BASE INVOCATION DIE d10

EFFECT This is the right of initiation that inducts a heathen into the specific cult of the Divine. Unlike other Miracles, the initiate must be the one to spend the Favor, though if a Servant is officiating, you’ll modify the Base Invocation Die by 0, instead of 5.

The initiative must have a Devotion to either the cult, Covenant, or the Divine

On a success, the Divine has accepted the sacrifice and shows their approval with a divine omen. From then on, the person is considered anointed. His Invocation Die for Miracles will be smaller and, unless it states otherwise, he is completely immune from the ill effects of Miracles invoked by priests of his cult. His Devotion die to either the cult, Covenant, or Divine is increased 1 size permanently.

A person can only be anointed to a single cult. If they decide to renounce their faith they gain the Flaw Excommunicated. If they wish to shift to a different cult of the same Divine, they’ll need to be re-Anointed and drop an Honor category. If they’re converting to a completely new faith (a new Divine), they drop two.

AUGER DEAD

Death | Dreams | Guardians

BASE INVOCATION DIE d10!

EFFECT By offering up sacraments to the Divine, they may allow you to bring back the dead for a short time for a chat.

The priests may ask the spirit of a body you have present X number of questions though it doesn’t gain any unnatural knowledge and, in fact, it’s knowledge of the afterlife is fairly foggy as well. So, if a person was killed by a blow to the back of the head, they won’t know who their attacker was.

This spirit is also aware of its surroundings to some extent and has some freedom of movement, allowing it to move around an area equal to the Miracle’s Distance. Being incorporeal, it can move through otherwise solid objects. This spirit makes an excellent scout as it can only be seen and heard by those affected by the Area of Effect.

This Miracle can be used on undead with the following notes: undead with their spirits trapped elsewhere (liches, for example) are immune. Those with their spirits piloting their dead corpses (vampires, sacred guardians) are not forced to answer the questions but those that don’t normally have the ability to speak (sacred guardians) gain the ability to do so. Those undead animated through magical or profane means (zombes, skeletons) can have their spirits brought back but don’t gain any additional influence over their old bodies.

BLESSING of PROTECTION

Adversity | Battle | Beauty | Fate | Fire | Home | Honor

BASE INVOCATION DIE d12!

EFFECT This endows the worshiper with godly protection, allowing otherwise normal skin to turn aside the blows of the infidel. Blessing of Protection increases the DR of anyone affected by the Miracle by X.

CHARM ANIMAL

Beasts | Nature

BASE INVOCATION DIE d4!

EFFECT By the exertion of the divine will over a lesser creature, the deity temporarily links it with the soul of supplicant, allowing them to control it as if it was perfectly domesticated.

With this Miracle you can charm a non-intelligent (Int < 3) creature with up to X * 3 Vitality. It doesn’t bestow the animal with supernatural intelligence, so don’t expect your new pet crow to be able to pick a lock but it will perfectly understand that you want it to pick a lock. It will always try its best.

During the Duration, the creature will be perfectly loyal, defending its master with its life, if need be but the Duration is over, prey will simply retreat into nature while predators will go berserker and try and get revenge if they’re around their old master.

The base Duration times are also altered for this Miracle. Seconds become weeks, minutes become months, and hours become years.space

COMMAND

Beauty | Deception | Fate | Home | Honor

BASE INVOCATION DIE d20!

EFFECT Few can resist the will of the gods. Even the heathen bends the knee at the unfurled power of the heavens. Any one up to an Area of Effect of Earshot is affected by the divine commandment spoken by the faithful and tries their best to comply.

This command should be simple and something that the targets has control over, as well as something they can accomplish within the Duration. So, “Stop” halts the target, while “Die” does nothing as the target doesn’t control that. Increasing the Strength simply expands the command by a word.

For people resisting, consider what is the least the target can do and still technically follow the command.

If the person is a member of the cult, in which case they gain extraordinary zealotry: they subtract X from the Stress they get from Stressing on checks when it comes to fulfilling the command.

CONFOUND

Adversity | Beauty | Deception

BASE INVOCATION DIE d20!

EFFECT By means of channeling the seraphic presence, the supplicant can cause the infidels to shudder for a moment. This miracle causes anyone in the affected to delay their action by Xd10! seconds or have to roll an additional dice of Xd10! while doing a Skill Check.

If the supplicant wishes, the delayed can temporarily fall into a stupor of thought about what just happened, allowing them to pass guards at a gate. The guards will still remember them being there, but they’ll completely blank on them stopping them and only really remember when someone else brings it up, even after the Duration is done.

CURSE

Adversity | Dreams | Disease

BASE INVOCATION DIE d10!

EFFECT The horrors of the other world can shrink and break the feeble minds of mortals by unleashing a portion of it on them.

By means of this Miracle, the cleric can temporarily inflict a Flaw from the Mental or Behavior Flaws table on the heathen and pagan. This is randomly rolled unless the supplicant increases the Miracle’s Strength past 10. If they get the Strength above 15, they may also choose from the Physical Flaws table.

DISGUISE

Beauty | Deception

BASE INVOCATION DIE d10!

EFFECT This shrouds an object or person in a holy glamour, changing their appearance. This Miracle, however, only changes superficial appearances, such as looks, race, or height but not tactile things such as texture or weight initially. The supplicant can change a number of specific features (so, ‘nose’, ‘ears’, and ‘eyes’ count but not ‘face’) equal to X of anyone affect for the Duration. If multiple people are affected, their features don’t have to be changed in the same way.

If the supplicant wants the disguise to be complete (as in it will hold up to texture and weight tests), they need to spend 2 points of the Miracle’s Strength for every specific feature they altar.

DIVINE CALL

Adversity | Dreams | Fate | Fire | Honor

BASE INVOCATION DIE d12!

EFFECT This binds the targets to a holy geas to serve the Divine on a quest or crusade that relates directly to furthering the Divine’s will in the Middle Realm.

While on this quest, the endowed crusaders gain X Favor every session and their Max Favor is increased by their Honor Die. On top of this, crusaders are empowered with holy fervor, allowing them to trade in Favor to modify Trauma checks, or ignore Stressed gained from Stressing about Skill Checks and Encumbrance on a 1 for 1 basis.

Additionally, they lose X Honor per session they don’t actively pursue their divine mandate. If they hit the Dishonorable Category, they additionally gain X number of Stress at the end of each day. Of course, the servants of the gods can’t really be bothered to view things so closely so what “actively pursue” means enforced in a more letter rather than the spirit of the law.

Only those with Devotion to the cult of the supplicant can be affected by this Miracle, unless the target stands on ground holy to that Divine.

The base Duration times are also altered for this Miracle. Seconds become weeks, minutes become months, and hours become years.

DIVINE CHARISMA

Beauty | Fire | Home | Honor

BASE INVOCATION DIE d8!

EFFECT The gods have an unearthly grace and poise, their charm irresistible to mortals. By means of this Miracle, the servant is gifted with an additional Xd12 dice to use in a Social Conflict.

If they escalate while the Miracle is still active, they gain an additional Xd12 dice.

EFFECT The mortal coil finds itself weak and impotent in the face of the eternal, eager to give up in its sight.

This Miracle increases the person's Encumbrance by 10 * X.

EARTH GRIP

Adversity | Death

BASE INVOCATION DIE d8!

EFFECT The Middle Realm may protest the will of the Higher Powers but even it must come when they call. This Miracle causes the ground to reach up, grappling and restraining any one affected, preventing them from moving and effectively defending themselves, defending with a d8!.

EARTHQUAKE

Nature
