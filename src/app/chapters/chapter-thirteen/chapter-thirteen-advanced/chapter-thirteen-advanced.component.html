Anatomy of the Divine

COVENANTS

Divines have spheres of power that represent their aspects. These are codified into Covenants, or promises they make with their servants in exchange for a portion of the Divine‚Äôs power.

Cults of that Divine typically pick one, maybe two Covenants, of their chosen Divine to focus on.

For instance, W√ºlder, a Gothic god, watches over Raiding, Industry and Trade. There could be 3 different priests to W√ºlder, one focusing on his role as the Bridge Between Men, one seeing him as primarily a watcher over people going on cattle raids, and one worshiping him as the greater crafter and source of inspiration.

While they all worship the same Divine, the Miracles they can invoke in his name are different.

This means that priests of different cults may not get along with each other and there can even be intra-faith wars to purge heretics (those that focus on different Covenant from them).

The main bonus of Covenants is that they make Miracles within them easier to invoke. The specifics are elaborated on in the Invoking Miracles section below.

COSMIC LINKS

The Higher Planes are those that exist above the crude and primitive world and so, naturally, the Divines are linked to the events and behaviors of the celestial bodies that make their homes in the heavens.

A Cosmic Link is the specific celestial body that the Divine is associated with, and it usually gives an insight to the personality of the god. The Cosmic Link doesn‚Äôt necessarily mean your Patron is primarily the Divine of that Cosmic thing. So, a Divine associated with the moon might not be primarily a moon god, in the same way that while Venus was associated with the Morning Star, she was primarily viewed as a love goddess by the Romans. Still, your holy symbols may incorporate the moon motif commonly to represent her.

For clarification‚Äôs sake: when something says ‚Äúdecrease‚Äù in this section‚Äôs explanations, you go down the chart by that many steps. If it says ‚Äúincrease‚Äù go up, so increase will always be a bonus, and decrease will always be bad.

COMET This is associated with fervent but fleeting powers. Whenever you invoke a Miracle, you gain two Honor Dice with a +3 but lose an additional Favor.

EVENING STAR The evening star is a source of strength and beauty: it's the first star to rise in the evening. When you invoke a Miracle, if you roll below the average, you gain the average instead. So, if you roll d8 and get a 3 or less, you get 4 instead.

MOON The moon is a steady force, its effects are subtle but constant. When you invoke a Miracle, if you roll two Honor Dice, keeping the highest.

MORNING STAR The morning star is the last star in the heavens before day and is a lone watcher. When you invoke a Miracle and roll the minimum amount, you don‚Äôt lose any Favor.

SUN The sun is the lord of heaven, strong and unbending, its power unquestioned. When you invoke a Miracle and roll the max amount, you don‚Äôt lose any Favor.

CYCLICAL Often hailing from older, more primitive times, the Cyclical deity represents earthly inevitabilities in a dichotomy, sometimes with themselves and sometimes with an opposing deity. This can be Spring and Summer, life and death, creation and destruction. They may be weak now, but they will wax unstoppable. When you invoke a Miracle, and roll under its average, your next Miracle gains a +1. This is cumulative.

If you roll above its average, this bonus is then removed, and you get a -1 to your next Miracle. Again, this is cumulative and resets when you roll below.

So, if you have a d6! Honor Dice, and you roll a 2, the next Miracle you invoke gets a +1. That next Miracle you roll, you get a 3 for a total of 4 (because of the +1), giving you a +2 the next roll. On that roll, you roll a 5 for a total of 7 ( 5 + 2 ). The next Miracle you then invoke gets a -1.

Favor of the Divines

WHAT IS FAVOR?

Favor is a measure of your standing with a given god. It‚Äôs not an absolute measure, however, but more a measure of the chance that a Divine will answer your prayer. How far your Favor goes ranges widely based on how devoted you are to a god and how Honor you are.

LEVELS OF DEVOTION

There are 3 levels of commitment to a Covenant, that determine how often you get Favor and how much Favor you can have:

UNANOINTED are the lowest level: those outside the cult and its mysteries. They gain no benefits of any kind.

ANOINTED are those that are the lay members of the faith. They gain small bonuses to their max Favor for the deity they‚Äôre devoted to. You can only be anointed to one Covenant at a time.

If you have a positive Devotion to a deity, one of their cults, or one of their Covenants, you can choose to begin the game as an anointed to that deity. Otherwise, you can become Anointed during gameplay once you have that Devotion.

You can only be anointed to one deity at a time.

SERVANTS are the highest rung of the commitment ladder and serve as the officers of the cult of the god. They‚Äôre priests, abbots, hierophants, pontiffs, nazarites, hermits, etc. These are men and women that have taken vows to devote their life to the cult. In game terms, this means that these are those who have a Servant Archetype as either their Primary or Secondary Archetype.

ACQUIRING FAVOR

Servants turn their eyes from the earth below to higher worlds, both literally and metaphorically. In this way, they‚Äôre the keeper of the times and seasons, as well as the calendar to note the occurrence of Holy Days.

As such, players who play Servants will need to keep track of the passage of time. Doing so gives them special bonuses according to the schedule laid out below. You‚Äôll note that it follows the Gregorian Calendar layout with holy days happening according to the Judeo-Christian traditions. The assumption that the characters will be following an in-game calendar with random Holy Days specific to their cult but we‚Äôre ‚Äútranslating‚Äù into the Gregorian calendar, in the same way that we assume the characters are actually speaking in their native fantasy tongues and not in English. Doing this makes it easier for players to keep track because the calendar is a familiar shape. They won‚Äôt have to track out a whole year and remember that Beltane is on the 5th of Seed‚Äôs Row.

Rules for using your own calendar and keeping all Servants relatively balanced against each other is going to be the Deluxe Rules.

Favor is unique to the Divine you‚Äôre petitioning. Gaining Favor with another Divine, means that you lose an equal amount of Favor with all others because gods are bad at sharing.

DAILY SACRIFICES (EVERY DAY) These are small, daily sacrifices. These tend to cost about 1 or 2 sc, 5 to 6 points of Stress, or 1 or 2 Vitality, depending on your Divine's preferences. They can be the sacrifice of a small, sacred animal, the burning of a portion of your meal, or the lighting of incense during morning prayers. They net 1 Favor for Servants of that Divine.

SABBATIC HOLY DAY (EVERY 7 DAYS) This is a day of rest and contemplation, where the faithful are instructed to put aside the things of the world and focus on the Divine with activities related to their Covenant. There is an emphasis on ritual over practicality to emphasize this divide, so while a Divine of Battle encourages his followers to fight every day, on his sabbath he only wants them to focus on ritualistic combat and controlled bloodshed, as opposed to actual combat. Again, this is to remind his followers of the holiness of the action and by forcing his followers to stop and think about their actions, he forces them to contemplate the deeper realities of combat. The sabbath takes up 1 to 3 hours and the rest of the day can be spent at the faithful wants.

Servants who observe the sabbath of their Divine gain all Favor back, while anointed worshippers gain their Min Favor from Charisma if they roll a 4 or better on their Honor die adding one Devotion related to that Divine. Unanointed worshippers gain nothing.

LUNAR HOLY DAY (4 SABBATIC HOLY DAYS) Where normal Sabbaths are days of rest, Lunar Holy Days are about sacrifices and consecration, which are thematically appropriate to the cult‚Äôs Covenant. Those observing the holy day need to stop and spend the whole day or they gain no benefit.

Servants gain all Favor back plus extra Favor equal to their Min Favor. This extra Favor is reset on the next Lunar Holy Day (which means you can‚Äôt stockpile it). The Anointed gain all Favor back, while the unanointed who celebrate gain 1 Favor.

SOLAR HOLY DAY (EVERY 3 LUNAR HOLY DAYS) These holy days celebrate the turning of the seasons and where the other festivals can be severe, with fasting and reading and nerd stuff like that, Solar Holy Days are about feasting and letting loose a bit. The Divines demand their faithful to party all day.

Servants gain all Favor back plus extra Favor equal to a roll of their Devotion. This extra Favor is reset on the next Solar Holy Day (which means you can‚Äôt stockpile it). The anointed faithful gain their full Favor back and the unanointed gain their Min Favor from Charisma if they roll a 4 or better on their Honor die adding one Devotion related to that Divine

ANNUAL HOLY DAY (EVERY 4 SOLAR HOLY DAYS) The Annual Holy Day either celebrates the new year or an important event in the cult, such as the birth of their messiah or the founding of their faith. It is a 2 day affair and is often occupied by feasting, gift giving, public rituals, and other such nonsense.

Servants and Anointed followers gain all Favor back with Servants also gaining extra Favor equal to their Devotion and the Anointed followers gaining extra Favor equal to a roll of their Honor die. For Servants, this extra Favor is reset on the next Annual Holy Day (which means you can‚Äôt stockpile it). The unanointed gain 1 Favor.

TAKING AN OATH (SPECIAL) Not a sacrifice or holy day parse, Taking an Oath is essentially getting Favor on loan from the Divines for the promise that, if you get out the bad situation you're in, you'll make a huge sacrifice to them soon after. By Taking an Oath, you gain a die worth of Favor for every 20% of treasure you gain from the adventure you promise to the Divine, should you survive.

This die is equal to their Honor dice related to the Divine you're petitioning if you're Anointed to them. The Unanointed must also promise to become Anointed in addition to the cash and goods, in which case they gain a d6! worth of Favor. This Favor can put you over your max but must be spent on a Miracle immediately.

The Divines are extremely litigious. If you don't fulfill your oath in 1 month + 1 for every Honor Category higher than Average Honor you're in, they will seek vengeance. You may no longer gain any Favor with any Divine that shares a pantheon with the one you're in and what you do have will decay, causing you to lose 1 Favor per day. Priests of the Divine will also condemn you as an oath breaker, a very serious accusation, causing you to drop 1 Honor Category per month and preventing you from increasing your Honor. Should you fall into Dishonor, you gain the Excommunicated Flaw.

CRUSADES & PILGRIMAGES (SPECIAL) There are holy places to visit and holy things to be done. Crusades and Pilgrimages are special rewards the GM can give out for furthering the Divine‚Äôs will. This can either be through an actual holy war or by waging a spiritual war of conversion. These don‚Äôt even need to be things done on purpose and can be part of the campaign.

The GM should award a roll of the crusader or pilgrim's Honor dice, allowing it to go over the Max Favor allowed, and awarding them Honor for furthering their deity‚Äôs influence in the Middle Realm. Additionally, if it was an amazing feat of faith, the GM may also increase the character‚Äôs Devotion size.

The unanointed gain no benefit from crusades and pilgrimages.

CONDEMNATION & ABSOLUTION (SPECIAL) Servants and Anointed followers are expected to hold to the Divine‚Äôs tenets more closely and so can come under condemnation for disobeying their deity. GMs may subtract Favor from Servants and Anointed followers for sins against their religion or prevent them from gaining Favor for prolonged transgressions. Servants and Anointed followers with the Religion Advanced Skill in their Covenant receive a warning before they act (having studied their religion to some extent).

Those that have transgressed must seek absolution before they gain the benefit of holy days and sacrifices again. What this means varies based on the magnitude of the sin.

MAXIMUM FAVOR

A person can only have so much Favor, with the Divines giving a little extra leeway for those of strong character. The Max Favor a person can have is limited to their Honor Category.

Anointed members increase this threshold by 1 while Servants increase their Max Favor by their level, but both cannot gain any Favor while in the Dishonorable Category, regardless of level.

Should a person drop an Honor Category, if their Favor is above their new max, it automatically drops.

Invoking Miracles

Now that you‚Äôve got a nice stash of Favor, you probably want to spend it. That‚Äôs pretty simple: pray out loud for the Miracle in question. Silent prayers don‚Äôt work because no Divine is going to answer a prayer they‚Äôre not going to get credit for.

For every Favor you spend, you get another use of your Honor Dice but comes with a few differences: the first, and most major, is that you can spend it on ANYTHING - you want to run faster or jump higher? You want to heal your wounds? You want to attack slightly faster? You can with Favor, however, the effects are only temporary, of course.

The second is that you can spend to change the world around you. Every Favor you spend can change a 5 ft square area with the following restriction: it can‚Äôt directly contradict things that have already been said.

For instance, you cannot wish to have an avalanche suddenly on the steppe or an inn to appear before the party that wasn‚Äôt there before, but you could wish for a flash flood or for an inn to be over the next rise, which was just out of view when you prayed.

Miracles are also bound by Covenant: you can‚Äôt pray for something your Divine doesn‚Äôt have power over, so a Divine of the city can‚Äôt really help you in the mountains, for example.

But this is where being in a pantheon does help out: you can pray to another Divine in your pantheon for their blessing instead, but it requires twice as much Favor and you don‚Äôt gain the benefit of your Cosmic Link.

The other restriction is that the Miracles need to be subtle: no angels coming down from the skies to keep you from falling to your death but perhaps there‚Äôs water or a passing hay cart that catches you just in time to soften your landing. There needs to be room for doubt.
