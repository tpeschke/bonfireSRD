CASTING SPELLS

Casting spells is a rather simple process with a lot of steps as it allows you to change almost every aspect of what you cast to fit your needs in the moment.

A spell has 5 dimensions:

COST

The base cost for every spell is 1 Spell Point. This is increased by changing things about the other 3 dimensions of the spell (Duration, Saving Throw, and Area of Effect) and then multiplied by the spell's Tier.

It is completely plausible that you’ll have some left over SP after casting a spell. If that’s the case, you can immediately cast another spell on the next second.

COMPONENTS

There are 3 types of Components: Verbal, Somatic, and Material.

Verbal components are words or sounds that need to said, Somatic are gestures and movements that need to be done, and Material are objects that need to be destroyed to release the spell.

It doubles the base SP cost for a spell to ignore a needed component, though not all spells need all 3.

DURATION

Each spell has a basic unit of duration but all start as instantaneous. The common duration denominations are seconds, minutes, half days (12 hours), days, weeks, lunar months, half years (6 months), years.

Every increase in Duration adds one to the duration time. So an increase of 1 to Duration for a spell with days, moves the Duration from instantaneous to 1 day, an increase of 3 moves it to 3 days, and so on.

The first increase starts at 1 SP in cost and doubles each time. So, 2 SP for the second, 4 for third, 8 for fourth, 16 for 5th and so on. This gets added to the Base Cost.

If the caster dies or is knocked unconscious, the spell immediately dispels.

SAVING THROW

The base saving throw is a d20! + double the caster’s level vs d20! + the target’s level.

If the target’s total is above the spell caster’s, the target has saved and the On Save effect takes place.

The caster can give himself a bonus to his roll. He can expend additional SPs, with the number he’s adding to his roll multiplied by itself being the additional SP that needs to be spent.

So, to get a +1, you add 1 SP (1 x 1), while adding a +3 costs you 9 SP (3 x 3).

Unless otherwise specified, anyone can try and resist a spell cast on them.

AREA of EFFECT

Magic is a powerful force and channeling it can be akin to opening a door just a hair in a hurricane.

All spells start with an Area Effect of Zone for no cost. Focusing it into a more manageable area costs more effort and power. The decreases are as follows (with their SP cost in parenthesis besides them):

Zone (0) > Chained (1) > Cone (2) > Sphere (3) > Ray (4) > Touch (5) > Self (6) > Target (6).

The SP cost isn’t cumulative; it’s simply added to the Base Cost.

ZONE The spell effects everyone in an X radius, centered and including the caster (where the spell emanates from), where X is the amount noted in the spell description.

You can increase it’s area by expending additional SPs, with the number the caster is adding to the radius multiplied by itself being the additional SP that needs to be spent.

So, to get from 1 ft radius to 2 ft radius, you add 1 SP (1 x 1), while getting to 4 ft radius costs you 16 SP (4 x 4).

CHAINED A chained spell leaps from target to target, starting at a random person within the radius of the Zone and then leaping to a random person within line of sight. If you can’t draw an imaginary line with no obstructions between the two targets, they’re not eligible. A spell travelling this way can travel outside the original zone.

The spell continues to leap from person to person until someone saves, with each person receiving a +1 to their save for every person before them.

CONE Starting at the caster and covering an area in front of her in a 180° arc, it extends a distance from her equal to a fourth of its Zone’s radius. You can spend additional Spell Points to modify this.

Each additional SP halves the degrees of the arc and each additional SP increases the range that it extends by an additional fourth of the Zone’s original radius.

The cone’s point “sticks” to the caster but can be rotated freely around them at will.

SPHERE This manifests the spell as a ball of pure magic that the caster can control for the duration of the spell. Any one struck by this Sphere suffers the full of effects of the spell without a saving throw, even if they try to resist it. Attacking with the Sphere is the same as a ranged attack.

The sphere is 2 ft in diameter and has a movement rate of 2 ft/second. That can be increased by expending additional SPs, with the number he’s adding to the speed multiplied by itself being the additional SP that needs to be spent.

So, to get 3 ft/sec, you add 1 SP ((1 x 1) + 2), while adding a 5 ft/second costs you 9 SP ((3 x 3) + 2).

RAY A spell ray is a beam from the caster to the target and functions identically to a ranged attack. The range is twice the radius of the Zone.

You can increase its range by expending additional SPs. The first time costs 1 SP and doubles the range, with every additional time doubling the SP cost to increase and halving the distance added.

So, a ray with a base range of 10 ft doubles to 20 ft for 1 SP, 25 ft for 2 SP, 27.5 ft for 3 SP and so on.

TOUCH This brings the spell’s range down to what a caster or her weapon can touch. The spell is delivered by means a melee attack roll. On a hit, the spell effects the target.

If you choose to increase the saving throw of a Touch spell, instead of the usual amount to add a bonus to your roll, you instead increase it on a 1 to 1 basis. This means that to get a +3 to your roll, instead of expanding an additional 9 SP, it instead only costs 3.

SELF The spell only affects the caster. Don’t bother rolling a saving throw in most cases (but the option is open to you, if you need it).

TARGET This spell effects a single target. You don’t have to see them but you do need a general location that’s about the size of a room and they have to be in range.

The range at which you can target someone is the radius of the Zone for that spell. As always, you can increase its range by expending additional SPs. The new range is the SP you’re adding multiplied by the base range.

This does mean that you’ll need to spend at least 2 SP before you see results.

At this stage, you can also start to increase the number of targets your spell effects by this increases the number of SPs required by 5 per additional target, so 5 for the second, 10 for the third, and so on.

CANTRIPS

Cantrips are just severally reduced version of the spell. They always cost 1 SP and are limited to how creative you can be with your spells. So, if you want to scratch your back with the Telekinesis spell, be my guest. They can’t do direct damage, or really affect others but besides that, go nuts.

MAGICAL FALLOUT

Making the primal forces of the cosmos your bitch is tiring work. All casters automatically gain a level of Fatigue after they cast their spell.

On top of this, being around a gate of the eldritch is unsettling for those unused to it. Everyone who’s not a caster of the same magical training who’s within 200 ft of where the spell goes off gains 1 point of Stress.

OCCULTIC MEDIUMS

The Wizard Archetype gains 1 or more magical items that serve as a fulcrum that helps them better channel or cast spells.

Over the ages, Wizards have found that certain types of Occultic Mediums are more adept at certain things and so the choice of Medium is often indicative of the Wizard’s personal style.

ANCESTOR BONES This Magic User was gifted the bones of his ancestors, powerful wizards from ages passed that are able to gift their descendant with some of their prowess.

At 1st level, a Wizard with Ancestor Bones as his Occultic Medium chooses a particular Order of Magic (or single element if he chooses the Elemental Order). As long as he’s carry his Bones, all spells of that Order cost 1 SP less for every 5 levels he’s obtained, down to a minimum cost of 1.

INVESTED ITEM This Medium comes in a many shapes and sizes. By binding her soul to an item, a Wizard can make a metal object an extension of her power.

While holding this item, they may cast any spell with Somatic components as if the hand was free. For armor, the Invested Item offsets the penalty to channeling by 5 seconds for every 5 levels the Wizard has obtained. This can only offset the penalty, and never lower it below the base speed. For weapons and other items, they may also cast spells with an Area of Effect of Touch on a successful hit, storing one spell indefinitely until it’s used. Needless to say, this a much favored Medium of battle mages.

ORBS These are often called a Seer’s Orb, as this is the favored by Wizards of the Mystical Order. Orbs are often glass, but those of other materials are not uncommon, though they tend to be precious metals or stones. The only requirement is that they’re larger than your fist.

Orbs bend reality, gaining an automatic increase to any spell’s range for every 3 levels of the Wizard.

SPELLBOOK A large tome, traditionally, the spell book is the favored Medium of the more scholarly magic user.

The Spell Book is not simply incantations jotted down or notes about rituals - it’s an extension of the Wizard’s brain. As long as he’s touching it, he automatically gains the ability to cast any spell in the book.

Wizards who select this Occultic Medium also gain double the starting spells, however, these are only stored in their Book. They also gain a new spell every level.

SPELL TATTOOS Painted Men are common in the frozen tundras of Askemark. The metal heavy ink serves to store channeled magic and allows the Wizard to keep invested power for longer.

With Spell Tattoos, the time it takes to lose a spell point when not channeling is doubled for every 4 levels the Wizard has obtained.

STAVES Traditionally made of holly wood harvested with a silver sickle at midnight on the night of the new moon, Staves serve as extensions of the Wizard’s soul, much like an Invested Item, but because it’s not made of metal, it holds different properties.

While welding a Stave in one hand, the Wizard offsets the penalty to channeling from armor by 5 seconds for every 5 levels the Wizard has obtained. This can lower the speed below the base channeling speed, but below the base channeling speed the Stave only lowers it by 3 seconds for every 5 levels.

FAMILIARS Favored by the traveling Wizard, a familiar is a small animal or homunculus that is bonded to the Wizard’s soul. Because of this connection, a wizard can see and hear through their Familiar’s eyes and ears as well as channel SP through it, as long as it is standing still, allowing the Wizard full movement.

Familiars are small sized creatures. The player selects three candidates, but the GM makes the final decision on which creature came when she called, unless the Wizard opts to have built herself a homunculus.

A Wizard who would get 2 Occult Mediums but chooses familiar twice may select a medium or large sized creature for their choices.

WANDS The Wand is an exact tool, a tool of precision. It is favored by more aggressive spell casters because it makes their spells hard to resist.

While casting with a Wand, the Wizard increases their Saving Throw by 1 for every 3 levels the Wizard obtains.

ORDERS of MAGIC

Some that study magic wish to categorize it so that they may better understand it.

There are technically 7 different Disciplines of Magic: Animancy, Alteration, Conjuration, Elemental, Illusion, Invocation, Mysticism, though the Elemental Order is further subdivided into the individual elements: Air, Chaos, Earth, Fire, Order, Water.

ANIMANCY This Order focuses on the study of life and death. Its spells focus on the manipulation of the body and spirit.

ALTERATION This Order focuses on manipulation of magic into crude matter. It focuses on wards and barriers.

CONJURATION This Order focuses on summoning things from the other planes of existence and forcing them into your control. It focuses on summoning and banishing.

ELEMENTAL This Order focuses on the primitive forces on the Middle Realm; the building blocks of creation. It focuses on conjuring elementals or channeling them into different forms.

ILLUSION This Order focuses on fooling the senses and the hijacking of the brain to make things appear and disappear. Its spells focus on making hallucinations of all kinds.

INVOCATION This Order focuses on the shifting of kinetic and potential energy in an area. Its spells can Fatigue enemies or change the direction they’re heading.

MYSTICISM This Order is more subtle. It’s the art of tapping into the secrets of the Painted World and shifting the unseen winds of fate. It’s spells focuses on scrying.

Arcane Training

Through years of study, a Wizard is able to develop an affinity for an Order or two of magic

A Wizard can spend 5 CrP to gain a + 1 in a certain Order of Magic. When casting a spell of that Order after channeling, they automatically increase their effective SP by that number.

A Wizard may continue to increase his knowledge in that school, or another Order, with the cost of each increase equal to the total training across all Orders x 5 x the number of Orders you have trainings in.

So, if Malazar the Unseen has a +1 in Alteration, a +3 in Air, and +1 in Animancy, and wants to increase his Alteration to +2, he’ll have to pay 75 CrP ( ( 3 + 1 + 1 ) x 5 x 3 ).

ELEMENTAL TRAINING

You can’t actually train in the Elemental Order itself. Instead, you train in a specific element with the following caveat:

Once you purchase a training in a particular element, you’re unable to purchase any trainings in that element’s opposite.

Air - Earth

Fire - Water

Chaos - Order

SPELLS of MULTIPLE ORDERS

If you have a spell that has 1 or more Orders assigned to it, you only get to apply one school’s training to it.

SPELL DESCRIPTIONS

ADDLE

TIER 1

ORDERS Alteration, Chaos, Mysticism

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 20 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This causes a mind fog to envelope everyone in the Area of Effect. During the Duration of this spell, any skill checks in the area are 1 difficulty harder.

On top of this, it subtracts a d4 from all Attacks, Defenses, and adds a d4 to Initiative rolls.

This difficulty can be further increased by 1 if the caster doubles the amount of SP required.

ALARM

TIER 1

ORDERS Mysticism

DURATION Half Days

AREA of EFFECT 20 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This spell has two Area of Effects; the trigger zone and the alarm area, which are modified independently of each other.

When someone enters the trigger zone, any one in the alarm area hears the tolling of a bell.

By default, the trigger zone and alarm area are the same areas, but any other Area of Effects for the alarm area means that only the people in those Areas hear the alarm.

While it is technically pointless in most cases, you can make the trigger zone personal by shrinking the Area of Effect, however it just automatically triggers.

ANCESTRAL LORE

TIER 1

ORDERS Conjuration, Mysticism

DURATION Skill Check

AREA of EFFECT 1 Fire

COMPONENTS Fire, Caster’s Blood

EFFECT By channeling the link in your blood, you can summon an ancestor spirit to endow you some of their knowledge temporarily.

One random person in the Area of Effect decreases a number of Int, Wis, or Char based skill checks by 1 difficulty.

An instantaneous version of this spell doesn’t have any effect.

ANIMATE CORPSE

TIER 3

ORDERS Animancy

DURATION Hours

AREA of EFFECT 20 ft

COMPONENTS Dead Body. You can't ignore this component.

EFFECT This animates the corpse into a mindless undead that serves the caster unquestioningly. This undead doesn’t think for itself and can only follow simple commanded like “Stand here and attack anyone that comes through”. It can’t speak or even defend itself properly and has no survival instincts.

To override the first command, or to take control of another’s animated corpse, you must cast this spell again, rolling a saving throw against the original caster (which may be yourself).

The Area of Effect only affects 1 random corpse in the Area of Effect.

ARCANE GROUNDING

TIER 1

ORDERS Alteration, Order

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 50 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT All spells are attracted to the Area of Effect of this spell. If the area is out of range of the spell, as long as it’s within eyesight, it shifts it’s target the maximum distance it can towards the zone.

ASTRAL PROJECTION

TIER 2

ORDERS Alteration, Mysticism

DURATION Hours

AREA of EFFECT 10 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT The caster can separate the corporeal form from the spiritual form.

Any willing target caught in the Area of Effect, assumes a semi-translucent form that can travel up to 50 miles per hour. They can’t interact with the physical realm but can see and hear as normal.

While traveling in this form, the person is almost transparent, requiring a Heroic level Skill Challenge to notice something off. The astral projection can’t speak or interaction with the Middle Realm, simply passing through walls and objects but stopped by magical wards and living beings.

Anyone resisting the separation needs to make a saving throw, failure indicating that they only barely shrugged off the effects, delaying their action by d4! seconds.

It’s recommended that you return to your body before the duration is up. Otherwise, your spirit is automatically thrown back into your body, which is an upsetting experience, netting you 1 Stress per mile you are away from your body.

While under the influence of this spell, the person’s body is completely helpless, with the caster being completely unaware about what’s going on with his body unless he’s killed and then he’s trapped as spirit between worlds.

AUGER SECRET

TIER 2

ORDERS Mysticism

DURATION Seconds

AREA of EFFECT 5 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This finds anything that is purposely hidden from others, including hidden doors or a love letter sequestered away, within the Area of Effect. It also detects the surface level emotions of anyone in the area.

The caster doesn’t automatically know what is hidden, they only know it’s exact location and general area the item takes up. They also know when they touch it, even after the spell’s duration is over.

AURA of INNOCENCE

TIER 2

ORDERS Illusion, Mysticism

DURATION Seconds

AREA of EFFECT 5 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT Anyone within the Area of Effect of this spell is affected by a mental glamour causing them to assume the best of the caster, even if he’s standing over the fresh corpse of their mother with the bloody knife in his hand.

At the end of the spell, any victims instantly clue into anything they overlooked.

BANISH

TIER 2

ORDERS Conjuration, Order

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 15 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT By sliding an object sideways into another plane, a conjuror can temporarily banish an object to another plane of existence where it remains in stasis until the duration runs out, at which point, it’s returned.

Any living creature can make a saving throw to resist this side step but creatures from other planes of existences that are brought into the Middle Realm by summoning use their summoner’s save and roll a d12! instead of a d20!. If these extra-planar being do fail, they’re permanently banished back to their home plane.

BAR PORTAL

TIER 1

ORDERS Alteration

DURATION Hours

AREA of EFFECT 35 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This spell has two parts: the first is that it locks and seals any door or latch in the Area of Effect. Anyone trying to bust through needs to make a Strength check vs the spell’s saving throw to successfully break down the door.

The second part is that it prevents any sort of teleportation, summoning, or banishing in the Area of Effect for the duration of the spell. A caster can still attempt these things but must make a successful saving throw to have it actually work. Banishing requires two saves: one to break through the Bar Portal and the second to actually banish the creature, should it resist.

This affects all casters (including the origin of this spell).

BIND ELEMENTAL HEART

TIER 2

ORDERS Elemental, Conjuration

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 25 ft

COMPONENTS A piece of the element

EFFECT This spell either conjures an elemental heart from it’s realm or binds one that has gone rogue.

Once in this realm, the elemental heart robes itself in nearby sympathetic elements, giving a form where it can then move. Without it’s dead kin around, the heart becomes inert and defenseless. So, while there are Order and Chaos Elementals, neither of those two things have a physical manifestation in the Middle Realm and so they simply lay there.

The basic spell conjures just the baseline elemental, which has a magnitude of 0. You try to summon a more powerful elemental servant by increasing the cost by 1 SP which gives you a d4! magnitude elemental. You can additionally double that to 2 binding a d6! magnitude, 4 for a d8! magnitude, and so on.

You also can expends additional SP to guarantee a minimum magnitude, on 1 to 1 basis, however, this minimum can never be increased beyond the current dice. So you can’t just increase your spell cost by 4 to gain a minimum 4 magnitude elemental if you haven’t increased it by 1 to gain a d4! Chance.

Elementals are ‘liquid’ and can shift and change their form. Their magnitude can be invested to increase their baseline stats and special abilities. How much this costs is determined by the type of heart summoned. Magnitude points can assigned at the time of summoning but also rearranged after, though this takes 1 second per point shift.

A summoned elemental heart appears in a random location within the Area of Effect and rolls a d12 initiative dice. During this time, the elemental is getting its barings and forming itself proper.

Elementals are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change an elemental’s command, the elemental rolls a saving throw, adding it’s magnitude (and any other summoned elementals’ magnitudes: controlling multiple bound beings at once is difficult and stressful) to its roll. If it wins, the caster loses control and the elemental goes rogue, first seeking out the caster to try and kill them as quickly as possible.

The caster can try cast Bind Elemental Heart again to try and regain control. The elemental will make another saving throwing, this time adding the difference between its magnitude and the caster adding his level plus a roll of his magnitude dice. So, a magic-user that would otherwise be able to summon a 4 magnitude heart (having pumped up to a d6! and then rolled a 4), gains a +4 to trying to rebind a rogue elemental.

BINDING GLYPH

TIER 1

ORDERS Invocation, Order

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 45 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This binds another spell effect to a random item within the Area of Effect of the spell. The item is marked with a faintly glowing sigil that requires an Average Perception check to see if you’re not looking for it specifically.

On a condition indicated when the caster first casts the spell the bound spell goes off as if cast from the item in question.

The mage is in effect casting two spells at one time and needs the SP to cast both for this spell to work. After she’s done, she receives 3 levels of Fatigue, instead of just the usual 1.

CHARISMATIC GLAMOUR

TIER 1

ORDERS Illusion

DURATION Hours

AREA of EFFECT 35 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT By weaving the dwemer of magic, the caster can affect the perceptions of those interlocking with her.

This has two effects: the first is that a random person within the Area of Effect can’t take hostile actions against the caster and treats them as if they were friends. The spell is broken if the caster makes any hostile actions against the person, but the victim also receives another Saving Throw with a -1 for each previous Saving Throw against the spell, should the caster do some that might annoy them, such as putting the victim in danger, attacking his friends, stealing something right in front of them, etc..

The second is that when in a Conflict with a person affected by the glamour, the caster increases the number of dice of one of their characteristics by 1. This can be further increases with the cost in SP being the number of dice added times itself. So increase a Trait by 4 dice increases the SP cost by 16 (4 x 4).

COMMUNE

TIER 2

ORDERS Elemental, Mysticism

DURATION Questions

AREA of EFFECT 15 ft

COMPONENTS The elemental in question. This component can't be ignored

EFFECT This allows the caster to talk with a random element or elemental within the Area of Effect of the spell.

The answers are limited to what the elemental or object might know or have paid attention to, with the object revealing information about how it was interacted with (such as some vague features of the people who’ve handled it or the places it’s been) and the elemental being more helpful with more refined details but typically rough around the edges.

CONJURE WALL

TIER 2

ORDERS Elemental

DURATION Seconds

AREA of EFFECT 25 ft

COMPONENTS A piece of the element

EFFECT Through the power of will, the caster creates a rift in time and space, connecting our realm with the elemental realms. Through this rift, the element of choice comes bursting forward, creating a barrier.

This wall can be placed along any wall, floor, or ceiling and isn't subject to our gravity once it's through the rift and be made from Air, Earth, Fire, and Water. Each comes with their own special benefit:

Air: This wall gives all those standing behind it an additional cover die of d4!. This dice size can be increased by 1 size by doubling the SP cost. Despite its protection against missile weapons, anything more solid than 50 lbs can just pass through the air wall.

Because of its nature, this air may be moved and shifted and bent.

Earth: This creates a solid wall of earth and rock, which nothing can get through. You can take a siege weapon to it and it won’t budge. It blocks both melee, missile, and targeted (though not zonal) spells. However, because of its nature, it can only be summoned in a straight line and not moved.

Earth walls knock people aside easily. If it would push them into a solid object (like another wall), a failed Saving Throw on the part of the victim results in d6! damage.

Fire: This creates a roaring inferno that floods the room with heat. Everyone within 25 ft of the wall, gains 1 level of Fatigue from this heat for every 20 seconds they remain in the area. The caster can increase this heat, halving the time between getting Fatigued by doubling the spell cost.

The wall itself doesn’t protect against anything coming through, but living objects that walk through become Fatigued and take d4! points of damage.

This wall may be moved. Shifted, and bent, similar to walls of air.

Water: Water walls have the fantastical power to dampen spells that pass through them. If a spell pass through a wall of water, its targets receive an additional d4! to their saving throws. This dice size can be increased by 1 size by doubling the SP cost.

Water walls do offer cover from missile fire, however, the cover dice is 2 sizes smaller than their saving throw dice, and those trying to get through them must slow to make an Acrobatic check to not slow down, but this is increased by 2 Difficulties for every time the spell was doubled.

So, a caster that’s doubled his water wall thrice, gains a d8! to saving throws, a d4 to cover, and those trying to pass through at Walking speed need to make a Heroic check or be stopped in their tracks.

A water wall can be summoned bent but not moved after that.

This wall can be placed anywhere within the Area of Effect and is roughly 30 ft long, 5 ft width, and 10 ft tall. These dimensions can be doubled by doubling the spell cost. This additional length, width, and height can be placed on top of, besides, or connecting, but it must be touching the 'base'. So a 30 ft long, 5 ft width, and 20 ft tall wall is perfectly okay but so is a T shaped wall.

The wall manifests in an single second, pushing everyone aside. This does no damage, unless in special circumstances.

A caster can keep still their wall up indefinitely, as long as they stay still and concentrate on the spell, with the Duration serves as a ‘countdown timer’ until the wall dissolves after the caster has moved on to do other things.

CORPSE TONGUE

TIER 3

ORDERS Animancy

DURATION Questions

AREA of EFFECT 25 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This affects a random corpse in the Area of Effect and allows the caster to ask a number of questions of the corpse.

The corpse only answers simply, with 3 words at max and is limited to the things they would have know in life. For example, they’d be unable to tell you who killed them if they were clubbed in the back of the head.

At the end of this spell, the corpse’s tongue dissolves into ash, preventing further castings of this spell.

An instantaneous duration does nothing by dissolve the tongue.

CORROSION

TIER 3

ORDERS Alteration, Invocation

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 35 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This adds magical corrosion to a random item on any person within the Area of Effect, inflicting a penalty of 1 to all metrics of that item. So, armor receives a -1 to DR, Initiative Modifier, while it also gets a +1 to Speed and Fatigue, while a weapon receives a -1 to Attack, Damage, Defense, and a +1 to Speed.

This spell can be further modified to target a particular brand of item: such as all backpack straps or boot laces in the Area of Effect by doubling the cost.

Additionally, you can increase the penalty by 1 by spending an number of spell points equal to the penalty times itself (so a -4 would take an additional 16 SP).

If the penalty reaches -5, anything that doesn’t have specific metrics to measure by (coin purse drawstrings, spectacle glasses) break permanently.

The illumination level for the area is dark, obviously. Shooting into the darkness gives the targets a +20 to defense.

You can’t resist the effects of this spell but the casting of Illumination does afford a saving throw to dispel the area of Darkness the Illumination covers.

DISORIENT

TIER 1

ORDERS Chaos, Illusion

DURATION Seconds

AREA of EFFECT 15 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This spell fools the brain and plays with energy causing any one caught in the Area of Effect to change their direction according to the caster.

DISPEL ENCHANTMENT

TIER 2

DIVINE OBJECT

TIER 1

ORDERS Mysticism

DURATION Seconds

AREA of EFFECT Miles

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This locates a certain object in the Area of Effect, however, each layer of complexity doubles the SP cost.

The object can only be found through a physical description, so “a good blacksmith” or “the duke’s necklace” will return all people in the area or all necklaces in the area.

Each additional description is a layer of complexity. So, a “gold necklace” doubles the base SP cost, while “A gold necklace with 3 rubies” quadruples it.

The key is to return as little information as possible as having that much information assault your brain at once is tiring, resulting in 1 Stress per 10 results found. So, a careless magic user in a large city searching for a blacksmith will net somewhere around 10,000 Stress.

For the duration of the spell, the caster knows the exact location of any of the objects in relation to herself. After it’s over, the memory remains but the inner compass has faded.

DOMINATE

TIER 3

ORDERS Animancy, Conjuration, Order

DURATION Words

AREA of EFFECT 10 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT By means of this spell, the caster is able to exert a small level of control over anyone in the Area of Effect, by way of a command.

This command needs to be simple and something that the target has control over, as well as something they can accomplish in a few seconds. So, “Stop” halts the target, while “Die” does nothing as the target doesn’t control that.

Increasing the duration simply expands in the command, however, the action needs to still be simple. “Push that button” works, for example, while “Do a dance” will result in a quick step ball-change and that’s it.

Consider what is the least the target can do and still technically follow the command.

SUMMONING

TIER 1

ORDERS Conjuration

DURATION Minutes

AREA of EFFECT 10 ft

COMPONENTS None

EFFECT This spell pulls a demon, a leftover of creation, from the aetherial void and binds it to the will of the summoner.

Demons are notorious shapeshifters and beguilers but through this spell, the summoner is able to force them (somewhat) into a single shape, though this is often a grotesque and twiced form. From there, the caster can order the demon to do their bidding.

There are different types and categories of demons, each with different abilities that can get a specific job. Some are brutish and stupid, while others are cunning and skillful. Some come linked with certain symbols, such as gateways or the birth of children, that give them powers related to them.

The basic spell conjures just a random demon type, which Circle value is 0, or the lowest rung of the aetherial world that exists between the other realms. You try to summon a more powerful demonic servant by increasing the cost by 1 SP which gives you one from one of the first four Circle (d4!). You can additionally double that to 2 binding a d6! Circle, 4 for a d8! Circle, and so on.

You also can expends additional SP to guarantee a minimum Circle, on 1 to 1 basis, however, this minimum can never be increased beyond the current dice. So you can’t just increase your spell cost by 4 to gain a minimum Circle 4 demon if you haven’t increased it by 1 to gain a d4! Chance.

The summoner can also weight the odds in his favor when it comes to what categories of demon he summons. He can modify the dice roll by any amount in either direction but this increases the SP cost of the spell by that amount times itself. So, if you roll a 5, but have spent 16 additional SP, you can summon any demon of your choice between 1 and 9.

A demon appears in a random location within the Area of Effect and rolls a d3 initiative dice.

Demons, even relatively dumb ones, are intelligent creatures and will struggle to overcome their bindings. Anytime you change a demon’s command, the demon rolls a saving throw, adding it’s Circle (and any other summoned demon’s Circle: controlling multiple bound beings at once is difficult and stressful) to its roll. If it wins, the caster loses control and the demon goes rogue, first seeking out the demon to try and kill them as quickly as possible.

The caster can try cast Summoning again to try and regain control. The demon will make another saving throwing, this time adding the difference between its Circle and the caster adding his level plus a roll of his Circle dice. So, a magic-user that would otherwise be able to summon a Circle 4 demon(having pumped up to a d6! and then rolled a 4), gains a +4 to trying to rebind a rogue demon.

Demons are wily creatures, always trying to find loopholes in their orders and while there is implicit bindings in the summon that prevent obvious loophole breaching, they’ll do the bare minimum of what’s asked of them.

This binding does make them more susceptible to suggestion as well and others may enter into a Conflict with the demon to get it to do or reveal things, on one condition: it can’t go against their summoner’s orders.

So, a demon ordered to patrol a hall can be forced to reveal information about its master or how many people are in each room, but it can’t be forced by others to stop patrolling (or even not attack the players, should it be told to kill anyone in the hall).
